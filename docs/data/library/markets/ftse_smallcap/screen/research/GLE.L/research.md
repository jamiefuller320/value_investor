## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

MJ Gleeson is a northern England and Scottish Borders affordable housebuilder trading at a deep discount to net assets (P/B ~0.5, P/E ~11, yield ~4.1%) after several years of earnings compression. The quantitative screen flags a persistent buy across cheapness, dividend, GARP and risk families, supported by minimal leverage, a current ratio above 3.8 and equity that has continued to grow despite softer profits. The central debate is whether asset-backed valuation and resilient spring trading (per May 2026 RNS headline) outweigh a clear downward trend in reported earnings (FY2022–FY2024), rising finance costs, legacy-site provisions of £5–7m and timing slippage on land profits into FY2027. Primary filing body extracts are unavailable in the research library, limiting verification of going-concern language, covenants and interim detail.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, Gleeson presents a classic asset play in a cyclical sector: the shares screen as statistically cheap while the balance sheet remains defensively structured.

**Quantitative alignment.** The screen passes 10 of 22 models across all four metric families—cheapness (Graham Defensive, Schloss Low P/B, Deep Value, Earnings Yield), dividend (Low P/E + High Yield, Dividend Growth), GARP (Neff PEGY, Magic Formula, Acquirer’s Multiple) and risk (Financial Health). Headline metrics—P/E 10.8, P/B 0.52, yield 4.1%, current ratio 3.87—are well inside typical value thresholds. The signal has been stable for five weeks with high data quality (19/20 metrics).

**Business quality vs. screen.** Gleeson’s model—affordable homes on owned land in lower-cost regions— differs from premium national builders and supports a tangible book value heavily weighted to inventory and work-in-progress (Yahoo FY2024: inventory £345m on total assets £378m). Equity has risen from £272m (FY2022) to £298m (FY2024) even as net income fell from £35.1m to £19.3m, suggesting land and WIP carry values are holding up while margins and volumes normalise post-cycle. Lease-adjusted debt is modest (~£5.1m; Yahoo FY2024), which underpins the Financial Health and Graham Defensive passes.

**Valuation hook.** At P/B 0.52 on shareholders’ equity of £297.7m (Yahoo FY2024), implied market capitalisation is roughly £155m against a net asset base approaching £300m—a substantial discount typical of distressed or deeply cyclical builders, but here paired with positive FY2024 free cash flow (£15.9m; Yahoo) and ongoing dividends (£7.6m cash paid in FY2024).

**Caveat.** Reported ROE is only 4.8% (screen), reflecting the earnings decline rather than balance-sheet weakness. The screen’s trailing FCF metric is negative (£-5.8m), which conflicts with Yahoo’s FY2024 FCF of +£15.9m and signals that near-term cash generation may be lumpy; this is a quality overlay, not a screen veto.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Cyclical and operational**
- UK affordable housing is highly sensitive to mortgage rates, Help to Buy / policy shifts and regional employment. News flow (May 2026 trading update) flags softer recent customer activity despite resilient spring sales.
- Reported earnings have fallen ~45% from FY2022 to FY2024 (Yahoo), with margin and interest-cost pressure ongoing; ROE remains low for a “quality” compounder.
- **Legacy sites:** £5–7m provision flagged (May 2026 trading update, news)—suggests historical site issues not fully reserved.
- **Land profit timing:** Slippage of key transactions into FY2027 (Jun 2026 headline) introduces forecast risk and may explain screen vs. Yahoo FCF divergence.

**Balance sheet / working capital**
- Inventory is ~91% of total assets (Yahoo FY2024). In a downturn, write-down risk could erode the book-value anchor that supports the value case.
- FY2023 demonstrated that inventory build can absorb cash rapidly (FCF -£18.9m); liquidity improved in FY2024 but remains event-driven.

**Governance / market**
- Long-term share-price performance has been poor (Yahoo headline, 15 Jan 2026: investors who bought five years ago sit on a ~37% loss despite a recent bounce).
- July 2026 PDMR and HMRC share-scheme purchases (news) are modest positive alignment signals but not thesis-changing.
- **Filing gap:** Without annual/interim body extracts, contingent liabilities, related-party transactions, pension deficit and covenant headroom cannot be assessed from primary sources—material for a land-heavy builder.

**Macro (colour only)**
- UK rates and housing policy remain backdrop variables (e.g. “Housing Policy Changes Put These Homebuilder Stocks In Focus,” *simplywall.st*, 11 Jul 2026). Not used to override the screen signal.

**Not observed (data absent)**
- No going-concern qualification, covenant breach or pension crisis language could be confirmed; absence of body extracts is not evidence of absence.
