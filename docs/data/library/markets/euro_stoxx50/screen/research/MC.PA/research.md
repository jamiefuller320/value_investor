## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

LVMH remains the dominant global luxury conglomerate, owning Louis Vuitton, Dior, Hennessy, Sephora and a portfolio of 75+ maisons. The quantitative screen rates MC.PA as a **buy**, supported by quality, dividend, GARP and risk factors (6/22 models passed; composite score 57.5%). After a multi-year share-price drawdown (news sources cite ~26–38% declines), valuation has compressed to roughly 23× P/E, 3.7× P/B and a 2.6% yield — unusual territory for a franchise of this calibre. The central debate is whether the current earnings trough reflects a cyclical luxury slowdown (China, aspirational consumer pullback) or structural margin erosion. Yahoo-sourced financials show three consecutive years of revenue and profit decline from the 2023 peak, which tempers enthusiasm despite strong balance-sheet metrics and cash generation.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Cyclical and geographic exposure**
Luxury demand is highly correlated with Chinese consumer sentiment and global wealth creation. News coverage repeatedly flags China as the swing factor (“Chinese Luxury Rebound Puts LVMH Valuation And China Exposure In Focus”, 3 Jun 2026). A delayed China recovery would extend the earnings trough visible in Yahoo data.

**Earnings momentum**
Three-year revenue and profit decline from peak is the most concrete red flag in the available data. Without interim filings, it is unclear whether deceleration has stabilised or is still deteriorating quarter-on-quarter.

**Inventory build**
FY2025 inventory of €22.7bn and a €1.3bn inventory working-capital drag suggest potential markdown or mix-shift risk in fashion/leather goods and cognac, though primary filing commentary is absent.

**Competitive dynamics**
Richemont’s Q1 FY2026 sales beat (20% growth vs 11% forecast, 15 Jul 2026) highlights divergent performance within luxury — jewellery/watches outperforming while LVMH’s broader portfolio may be more exposed to aspirational softening. Hermès’s pricing power remains the benchmark LVMH must match.

**Governance and legal**
Google News references “Lawsuit Scrutiny Following Fair Value Debate” (2 Jul 2026) — details are thin in the manifest, but any securities litigation warrants monitoring. The Arnault family’s controlling stake (~48% via Groupe Arnault) provides long-term alignment but limits minority influence.

**Primary filing gaps**
Without URD/AMF extracts, standard risk disclosures — related-party transactions, tax disputes, environmental liabilities, lease commitments beyond IFRS 16 totals, and going-concern statements — cannot be reviewed. This materially limits deep-dive confidence.

**Special items and impairments**
Yahoo data shows recurring restructuring charges and asset impairments (€720m FY2025). Filing language would clarify whether these are one-offs or structural.

**Macro and FX**
~65% of revenue is generated outside the eurozone (historical company disclosure; not re-verified here). Euro strength and tourism flows affect reported growth.
