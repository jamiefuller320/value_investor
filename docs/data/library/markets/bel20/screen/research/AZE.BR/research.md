## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, Azelis offers a classic **“cheap cash flow vs. weak reported earnings”** setup. The quantitative screen passes five models—FCF Yield, Neff PEGY, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, and Financial Health—across all four factor families (cheapness, dividend, GARP, risk), with high data quality (19/20 metrics). At P/E 23.0, P/B 1.0, dividend yield 2.1%, and ROE 4.0%, the market is pricing low returns on equity whilst still assigning a full book multiple—consistent with goodwill-heavy balance sheet optics rather than underlying cash economics.

Business quality, inferred from Yahoo fallback financials, rests on a global specialty-ingredients distribution platform with recurring working-capital dynamics: gross margin held near 23% (FY2025 gross profit €968m on revenue €4.13bn), and free cash flow remained strong at **€397m** in FY2025 despite net income falling to **€111m** (from €181m in FY2024). The screen’s negative headline growth (-67.6%) aligns with the EPS trajectory (€0.74 → €0.46), yet FCF conversion and dividend continuity (cash dividends ~€55m in FY2025) support the “Financial Health” and “Dividend Growth” passes. The value case assumes normalisation of operating income (FY2025 EBIT €271m vs. €362m in FY2024) and that acquisition integration (€152m spend in FY2025; €241m in FY2024) continues to expand the network without goodwill write-downs on **€2.47bn** of goodwill (FY2025).

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Data limitation:** Without filing bodies, going-concern statements, debt covenants, contingencies, and litigation disclosures cannot be quoted. The following risks are inferred from Yahoo financials and news only.

1. **Earnings quality / cyclicality:** FY2025 profit collapse (-39% net income) alongside flat-to-down revenue raises impairment and multiple-compression risk if margins do not recover.

2. **Goodwill and acquisition model:** Goodwill of €2.47bn (~46% of total assets) against negative tangible equity creates asymmetric downside in a downturn; sustained ROE ~4% is inconsistent with historical acquisition premiums.

3. **Leverage:** Net debt/EBITDA ~3.6x with €96m interest expense limits flexibility if specialty-chemical end-markets weaken further; refinancing risk cannot be assessed without filing covenant detail.

4. **Shareholder structure / governance:** News (11 Apr 2026) flags upcoming ordinary and extraordinary general meetings (13 May 2026) covering share-capital powers, buyback authority, founder rights, and board composition—potential governance overhang. EQT fully exited its remaining stake (27 Feb 2026), removing a supportive holder and increasing free float supply.

5. **Capital returns vs. balance sheet:** An equity buyback programme announced 24 Apr 2026 **expired** (7 May 2026 per MarketScreener)—limited evidence of active repurchase support at current prices. Dividend continuity alongside rising net debt warrants monitoring.

6. **Market sentiment:** Analyst consensus price target cut from €17.39 to €16.25 (25 Nov 2025); commentary on institutional ownership pressure after a €127m market-cap drop (23 Jan 2026). Coverage is thin and largely valuation/governance themed rather than operational.

7. **Pension:** Yahoo shows only €13m non-current pension/post-retirement liabilities—likely incomplete without annual report notes.
