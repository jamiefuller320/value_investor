## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Nutrien Ltd. is the world’s largest crop-nutrient producer, combining low-cost potash mining with nitrogen, phosphate, and a global retail distribution network (Nutrien Ag Solutions). The quantitative screen rates the name a **Buy** on cheapness (P/E 13.5, P/B 1.3), dividend support (3.3% yield), quality (ROE 9.8%), GARP, and balance-sheet metrics (debt/equity 55%), with nine of 22 models passing across five factor families. Reported earnings rebounded sharply in FY2025 after a trough in FY2024, supported by improved commodity pricing, cost savings, and asset sales, though the cycle remains the central debate. The valuation hook is a sub-market multiple on a scaled, integrated franchise with potash cost leadership and a shareholder-return programme (dividends plus buybacks). Key risks are fertiliser price and volume cyclicality, inventory build, and mixed sell-side sentiment — including a July 2026 Jefferies downgrade — offset partly by operational momentum (record potash volumes, accelerated cost cuts) cited in recent news flow.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The screen’s Buy signal fits a **cyclical quality compounder trading at a trough-to-mid-cycle multiple**. Nutrien passes classic value screens (Graham Enterprising, Dreman Contrarian, Magic Formula, Composite Value) alongside dividend and health tests (Dividend Growth, Piotroski F-Score, Financial Health), suggesting the market still prices the stock primarily as a commodity proxy rather than a scaled, integrated operator.

**Business quality.** The FY2025 Form 40-F (filed 27 February 2026) confirms Nutrien as a dual-listed Canadian issuer (TSX/NYSE: NTR) with audited annual statements, MD&A, and AIF incorporated by reference. The integrated model — upstream production plus downstream retail — provides volume leverage in potash (industry-leading reserves per NI 43-101 disclosures referenced in the filing) and geographic diversification across North America, Latin America, and other markets. This scale supports the screen’s quality and risk-family passes despite sub-double-digit ROE at current commodity levels.

**Quantitative fit.** At P/E 13.5 and P/B 1.28, Nutrien trades below typical ag-chemical peer multiples on screen metrics, with FCF of approximately US$1.6bn (screen) and debt/equity of 55%. The stock passes Lynch PEG and Neff PEGY, indicating growth-adjusted value rather than a pure deep-value trap. Nine-model breadth across cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk families (composite 68%, persistent five weeks) adds modest conviction versus a single-factor call.

**Capital allocation.** Yahoo data (fallback) shows ~US$1.06bn of dividends and ~US$551m of share repurchases in FY2025, with share count falling from 491.0m (40-F cover page, 31 December 2024) to 482.0m (40-F cover page, 31 December 2025). FCF of US$2.0bn in FY2025 covered dividends roughly 1.9×, leaving room for buybacks and sustaining capex (~US$2.0bn).

**Operational narrative.** Recent headlines cite record potash sales volumes, faster-than-planned cost savings, and a refreshed global Code of Conduct — consistent with management’s stated focus on operational excellence and capital discipline. For a value investor, the case is **scaled crop-nutrient exposure at a modest multiple, with earnings normalising off a 2024 trough and an active return-of-capital programme** — the profile the screen is designed to surface.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Cyclical commodity exposure.** Fertiliser prices and farmer application rates drive earnings volatility, as evidenced by the 2022 peak-to-2024 trough swing in revenue ($37.9bn to $26.0bn) and net income ($7.66bn to $674m) on Yahoo data. Q1 2026 earnings weakness ($131m net income) underscores ongoing cyclicality not fully captured by static value screens.

**Inventory and working capital.** FY2025 inventory rose to $7.0bn (Yahoo) with negative working-capital cash flow (−$783m). In a price-down cycle, inventory write-down risk rises.

**Leverage and liquidity.** Net debt ~$10.0bn (Yahoo) with commercial paper ($399m) and revolver usage ($371m line of credit drawn per Yahoo balance sheet). The 40-F references contractual cash requirements and off-balance sheet arrangements in MD&A, but **specific covenant headroom is not available in body extracts**.

**Governance / controls.** The FY2024 40-F disclosed a material weakness in Brazil derivative controls (subsequently remediated). While FY2025 reports no material changes, this highlights operational complexity in emerging-market retail operations.

**Pension and employee benefits.** Non-current pension and post-retirement obligations ~$221m plus current portion ~$688m (Yahoo, FY2025). Long-term provisions total ~$1.47bn. Not alarming relative to scale but material in a downturn.

**Regulatory and reserve reporting.** The 40-F explicitly notes NI 43-101 mineral reserve classifications may not be comparable with SEC standards — relevant for potash reserve valuation debates. Environmental attestation reporting (US agency requirements, GHG assurance engagements) indicates ongoing regulatory scrutiny. Mine safety disclosures are referenced (Exhibit 99.10) but **not readable in available body extracts**.

**Goodwill concentration.** Goodwill ~$12.1bn against equity ~$25.3bn (Yahoo). Further commodity downcycles could trigger impairment charges, as seen in FY2023–2024 (Yahoo: ~$774m and ~$530m impairment charges).

**Analyst sentiment.** Jefferies downgraded Nutrien on demand and supply risks (16 July 2026, news manifest). Seeking Alpha published a bearish 2026 outlook citing fertiliser macro headwinds (27 February 2026). These do not invalidate the value case but flag consensus dispersion.

**Data gap.** Primary filing bodies lack readable financial statements, limiting ability to verify adjusted EBITDA, segment margins, or covenant language directly from source documents.
