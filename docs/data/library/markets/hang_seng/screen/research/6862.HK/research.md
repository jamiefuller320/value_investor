## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Haidilao International Holding Ltd. is a leading Chinese hotpot restaurant chain listed in Hong Kong, currently flagged as a **buy** on the quantitative value screen (9/22 models, composite 55%). The investment case rests on a combination of cheap cash-flow and earnings metrics (P/E 13.4, FCF yield 6.2%, dividend yield 6.2%, ROE 39.6%) against a franchise that has largely normalised post-COVID and continues to generate substantial operating cash. The central debate is whether headline cheapness masks a maturing domestic market and a recent earnings down-cycle: Yahoo-sourced financials show FY2025 net income falling to RMB4.05bn from RMB4.71bn in FY2024 despite modest revenue growth, while free cash flow has declined for three consecutive years. No primary regulatory filing extracts are available in the research library, limiting verification of management commentary, contingencies, and interim trends; governance and legal-structure concerns (Cayman/HK listing) add a layer of investor scepticism.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The quantitative screen identifies Haidilao across five factor families — **cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk** — passing nine models including FCF Yield, Neff PEGY (0.02), Quality Value, Buffett Quality, High Dividend Yield, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Acquirer's Multiple, and Financial Health. For a value investor, the hook is not deep asset cheapness (P/B 5.6) but rather **cash-generative quality at a mid-teens earnings multiple with a high shareholder payout**: FY2025 free cash flow of RMB3.92bn (Yahoo) equates to a ~6.2% FCF yield on the screen, while ROE of 39.6% signals efficient use of a relatively asset-light service model once store utilisation normalises.

Business quality remains supported by brand leadership in hotpot, a service-led operating model, and a balance sheet that, on Yahoo data, carries RMB6.60bn cash plus RMB2.63bn short-term investments against RMB5.93bn total debt (FY2025) — a net cash position that underpins dividend capacity and cushions cyclicality. Revenue has recovered from the COVID trough (RMB31.0bn in FY2022) to RMB43.2bn in FY2025, indicating the core franchise is intact even as growth moderates. The screen's reported earnings growth of −13.6% aligns with the FY2025 profit decline and is the key tension: the name screens cheap on trailing metrics, but the trajectory is not yet clearly re-accelerating. At one week on signal with 95% data quality (19/20 metrics), this is an early-stage quantitative flag rather than a long-established conviction position.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Data and disclosure gap.** With no annual or interim filing bodies in the library, going-concern language, contingent liabilities, related-party transactions, and covenant terms cannot be assessed from primary sources. This is a material limitation for a first-pass memo.

**Earnings and FCF trajectory.** FY2025 profit and free cash flow declines, rising capex, and margin compression suggest the easy post-COVID recovery phase is over. The screen's negative growth input (−13.6%) reflects this; a value trap is plausible if competition and store saturation continue to pressure unit economics.

**Dividend sustainability.** Payout ratios above 100% of net income in FY2024–FY2025 are funded from cash balances, not earnings growth. Dividend Growth model passage on the screen may reflect historical policy rather than forward capacity.

**Competitive and cyclical.** Hotpot is highly competitive in China (local chains, delivery platforms, consumer trading down in weaker macro periods). Consumption recovery in mainland China remains uneven; the library's `macro_context.json` carries no Hang Seng regime markers, so macro is not used as a veto here, but sector cyclicality is inherent.

**Governance and legal structure.** Haidilao is a Cayman Islands–incorporated company listed on SEHK; proposed amendments to its articles of association are scheduled for AGM shareholder vote (see News). Michael Burry's March 2026 commentary on the "Cayman Islands shell" structure for HK-listed Chinese firms highlights a structural risk — investors hold offshore holding-company equity rather than direct mainland operating assets — that the quantitative screen does not capture.

**Impairments and special items.** Yahoo data shows recurring special charges and asset impairments across FY2022–FY2025 (e.g. FY2025 special income charges of RMB444m, write-offs and impairments). Without filing detail, the nature and recurrence of these items is unclear.

**No primary filing red flags** (going concern, pension deficits, covenant breaches) can be cited because no filing bodies exist in the source set.
