## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

Going-concern language, contingent liabilities, related-party transactions, customer concentration, and covenant terms **cannot be verified** from primary sources in this bundle — a material research limitation.

**Cyclical and demand risk:** Consumer Cyclical sector classification implies exposure to end-market apparel/sportswear demand cycles. FY2023’s 10% revenue decline demonstrates sensitivity; recovery in FY2024–25 may reflect normalisation rather than sustained acceleration.

**Earnings normalisation:** FY2025 net income fell 7% despite revenue growth, and EPS declined from RMB 4.15 to RMB 3.88. If FY2024’s elevated non-operating gains were non-recurring, forward earnings may track closer to FY2025 than FY2024, challenging a single-digit P/E on peak earnings.

**Rising investment intensity:** Capex nearly doubled from FY2023 to FY2025; if demand softens, fixed-cost leverage and depreciation could pressure margins. FCF coverage of dividends narrowed (FCF RMB 2.81bn vs dividends RMB 3.67bn in FY2025).

**Working-capital and receivables:** FY2025 saw a substantial receivables build (−RMB 795m cash-flow impact). Without filing footnotes, credit quality and customer payment terms are unverified.

**Governance / geopolitical:** As a Hong Kong-listed company with mainland operational exposure (inferred from listing market only — not confirmed in filings), the name carries standard China/HK regulatory, trade-policy, and supply-chain relocation risks not captured by quantitative screens.

**Market sentiment:** Yahoo Finance (2 April 2026) notes a 19.5% one-year total shareholder return decline despite buyback and dividend news, suggesting the market prices structural headwinds beyond what screens measure.
