## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
China Hongqiao Group is China’s largest private aluminium producer, screening as a Strong Buy on deep value metrics: P/E 8.0, P/B 1.4, dividend yield 7.5%, and ROE 18.6%, with 16 of 22 quantitative models passing across cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk families. Yahoo-sourced financials (primary HKEX filing bodies were not retrieved) show a sharp earnings inflection from 2022–2025, with revenue rising from RMB 131.7bn to RMB 162.4bn and net income from RMB 8.7bn to RMB 22.6bn, supported by strong free cash flow of RMB 27.8bn in 2025. The central debate is whether current profitability reflects a cyclical aluminium upcycle and AI-driven demand tailwind versus normalisation risk, family-control governance, and balance-sheet leverage (total debt RMB 72.5bn). Valuation remains compelling on screen metrics, but conviction is tempered by absent regulatory filing extracts and a recent share placement that adds dilution.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The quantitative screen flags China Hongqiao as a classic value compounder disguised as a cyclical: the name passes Graham Defensive and Enterprising criteria, Earnings Yield, FCF Yield, Buffett Quality, Magic Formula, and Financial Health models, with a composite score of 80% (sector-relative 75%) and current ratio of 2.12. Business quality appears solid on trailing numbers—normalised EBITDA of RMB 48.3bn in 2025, operating margins near 23%, and FCF yield supported by RMB 27.8bn of free cash flow against capex of RMB 11.2bn—suggesting the cheapness is not purely distress-driven. Dividend capacity is meaningful: cash dividends paid rose from RMB 9.3bn (2024) to RMB 11.1bn (2025), consistent with the screen’s High Dividend Yield and Dividend Growth passes. For a value investor, the hook is buying a low-cost, vertically integrated aluminium leader at single-digit earnings multiples while it generates substantial cash and maintains ROE above 18%. The screen’s risk-family pass (Dreman Contrarian, Composite Value) implies the market may be overly discounting China/HK cyclicality rather than fundamental deterioration—though that discount may be partly warranted given leverage and governance opacity.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclicality:** Aluminium smelting earnings are highly correlated with LME prices and power costs. The 2024–2025 margin expansion may partially reverse in a downturn; operating income already softened in 2025 despite record revenue.

**Leverage and refinancing:** Total debt of RMB 72.5bn with RMB 27.1bn current portion requires ongoing capital-markets access. Interest expense was RMB 3.5bn in 2025 (net interest outflow RMB 2.2bn). Without filing bodies, covenant terms and maturity schedules are unknown.

**Governance and related parties:** The Zhang family controls the group; news coverage references founder Zhang Bo’s fortune and a November 2025 placement of up to 400m existing shares (HK$11.68bn / ~US$1.5bn at 9.6% discount) to fund projects and repay debt—dilutive to minority holders. Long-term equity investments of RMB 18.4bn (2025) and investments in associates suggest complex group structure typical of Chinese private industrials; related-party risk cannot be assessed without filings.

**Regulatory:** China’s aluminium capacity caps and environmental enforcement remain structural overhangs. No filing language on contingencies, going concern, or environmental liabilities is available in the source pack.

**Impairments:** Recurring special charges and asset impairments (RMB 2.6bn impairment of capital assets in 2024; RMB 417m in 2025) may indicate asset-quality issues or aggressive prior capitalisation—unverified without annual report notes.
