The value case assumes mid-cycle normalisation rather than a return to FY2022 super-cycle earnings (US$9.97bn net income, EPS US$15.09), and that balance-sheet strength allows the market to look through near-term FCF pressure.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclicality:** Container shipping earnings remain highly freight-rate dependent. FY2025 profit decline confirms the market is not pricing a super-cycle repeat, but further rate softening could compress the 6.1% dividend yield and P/E support.

**Dividend sustainability:** FY2025 payout (~89% of net income, per Yahoo) exceeded free cash flow. Continued large dividends alongside US$2bn capex will draw down cash unless rates stabilise or capex moderates.

**FCF and capex:** Negative/near-zero FCF in FY2025 alongside rising PPE signals a reinvestment phase. Value screens may overstate “quality” if capex stays elevated through a weak rate environment.

**Parent company / governance:** OOIL is controlled by COSCO Shipping Holdings. Strategic decisions on routes, alliances, and capital allocation may prioritise group interests. A related filings-index headline (“COSCO on the hook for huge bill at LBCT,” 25 March 2026, WorldCargo News) flags potential terminal/port cost disputes involving the COSCO ecosystem — not OOIL-specific but relevant to group financial and operational risk.

**Primary filing gaps:** Absence of HKEX annual report or interim results body text means no access to management commentary on going-concern language, contingencies, covenant details, charter-rate sensitivities, or geopolitical route disruptions (Red Sea, Panama Canal, trade tariffs). This is a material research limitation.

**Competitive and regulatory:** Industry overcapacity ordering, IMO emissions regulations (retrofit/scrubber costs), and US–China trade policy shifts can affect utilisation and costs — none quantified in available sources.
