# Trip.com Group Limited (9961.HK) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-18T18:26:28.181459+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Trip.com Group is Asia’s leading online travel platform (Ctrip/Trip.com, dual-listed as 9961.HK / NASDAQ TCOM), screening as a buy on cheap headline multiples (P/E 6.5, P/B 1.1, ROE 20.1%) with broad value/quality model support. The valuation hook is a post-pandemic earnings compounder trading below many global OTAs, amplified by a large 2025 return of capital that inflates trailing yield (~58% on the screen — treat with caution). The central debate is whether reported 2025 profitability (RMB 33.3bn net income) reflects durable operating power or one-off gains (securities disposals, business sales) while domestic China demand softens into 2026. Near-term sentiment has turned cautious after disappointing Q2 2026 guidance, despite resilient international booking growth.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The quantitative screen flags Trip.com across five factor families — cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP and risk — passing 13 of 22 models including Graham Enterprising, Schloss Low P/B, Magic Formula, Buffett Quality and Financial Health, with debt/equity at 19% and sector-relative composite score of 70%. Operationally, the business fits a value investor’s preference for scale franchises with improving returns: operating revenue grew from RMB 17.5bn (FY2022) to RMB 56.1bn (FY2025) per Yahoo fallback data, operating income rose from RMB 0.7bn to RMB 17.1bn, and free cash flow has been consistently positive since recovery (RMB 13.6bn in FY2025). ROE of 20.1% and P/B of 1.1 suggest the market may be capitalising normalised earning power at a discount, particularly if international expansion (Q1 2026 international platform bookings +65% YoY per news) sustains mid-teens revenue growth. The buy case is not a deep cyclical trough play but a quality compounder mispriced on headline metrics that blend extraordinary items with core OTA economics.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Earnings quality:** FY2025 reported net income is heavily distorted by gains on securities and business disposals (combined ~RMB 35bn+ pre-tax effect). Normalised earnings flat YoY despite 17% revenue growth warrants scrutiny of margin trajectory and reinvestment (R&D RMB 15.1bn; selling & marketing RMB 14.9bn in FY2025, Yahoo fallback).

**Near-term demand:** Multiple sell-side notes (Jun–Jul 2026) cite disappointing Q2 2026 guidance and China domestic travel softness; Barclays explicitly referenced China macro headwinds when cutting its target.

**Regulatory and structural:** As a Cayman-incorporated, China-operating platform, Trip.com carries standard VIE/governance and PRC regulatory overhang (data, consumer protection, platform economy oversight). Primary filing language on these risks is unavailable in the source pack.

**Litigation:** “TCOM Investor Alert: Trip.com Group Limited Securities” (GlobeNewswire, 5 May 2026, filings index) suggests ongoing or prospective securities litigation — a governance/red-flag item not captured by quantitative screens.

**Competitive:** Intense domestic competition (Meituan, Alibaba’s Fliggy) and global OTAs (Booking, Expedia) in international markets; heavy marketing spend required to defend share.

**Balance sheet:** Goodwill RMB 62.3bn plus other intangibles RMB 13.0bn create impairment sensitivity if growth slows. Related-party balances are material (due from/to related parties RMB 3.4bn current, Yahoo fallback).
