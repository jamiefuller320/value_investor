## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Hengan International Group (1044.HK) is a leading China-based manufacturer of tissue paper, sanitary napkins, and baby/adult diapers, trading at a deep value multiple on the quantitative screen (P/E 9.4, P/B 1.1, dividend yield 6.5%). The investment case rests on a cash-rich balance sheet, sustained dividend payout (~RMB 1.6bn annually), and earnings recovery in FY2025 after a softer FY2024, against a backdrop of mature but resilient domestic hygiene demand. The key debate is whether margin compression and China consumer sluggishness are cyclical headwinds or structural limits on growth, and whether the market is fairly discounting birth-rate and competitive pressures despite attractive headline multiples. Primary regulatory filings were not retrieved for this name; financial analysis relies on Yahoo fallback data, which limits audit-level conviction.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The quantitative screen flags a strong buy (13 of 22 models passed; composite score 75%, sector-relative 70%), with all four value families satisfied: cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk. Hengan scores on classic deep-value screens—Graham Enterprising, earnings yield, FCF yield, low P/E plus high yield, Magic Formula, Acquirer’s Multiple, and financial health—with debt/equity at 72% and ROE of 11.8%, consistent with a profitable, defensively positioned consumer business rather than a distressed cyclical.

Business quality supports the screen signal: Hengan is a scale leader in China’s tissue and personal-care categories, with national brands (e.g. “Hearttex”, “Anerle”, “Cosmo”) and vertically integrated production. FY2025 revenue of RMB 23.1bn (Yahoo fallback) represents modest recovery from FY2024’s RMB 22.7bn trough, while net profit rebounded to RMB 2.54bn from RMB 2.30bn. Free cash flow of RMB 2.19bn comfortably covers dividends of RMB 1.59bn, underpinning the 6.5% yield that multiple dividend models pass. The stock offers GARP characteristics—Lynch and Neff PEG models pass—because earnings are recovering while the multiple remains single-digit. For a value investor, the combination of market-leading franchise, net-cash-like balance sheet (net debt RMB 118m vs cash RMB 15.6bn), and a shareholder-return policy that has paid roughly RMB 1.6bn in dividends annually aligns screen cheapness with underlying cash-generation capacity.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Data verification:** Without HKEx annual reports or interim announcements in the filing library, going-concern language, contingent liabilities, related-party transactions, and covenant terms cannot be reviewed. This is a material research gap for a first-pass memo.

**Margin and earnings volatility:** Operating income fell ~23% from FY2023 to FY2024 and has not fully recovered in FY2025, despite revenue stabilisation. Persistent margin pressure would undermine the GARP and earnings-yield thesis even at a low P/E.

**China structural headwinds:** Declining birth rates weigh on baby-diaper demand; tissue and feminine-care categories face intense competition from multinational and local brands, limiting pricing power in a deflationary consumer environment.

**Investment portfolio risk:** The enlarged financial-asset book (RMB 7.0bn) generates interest income but adds earnings volatility (FY2025 securities losses of RMB 332m per Yahoo cash-flow data). This is not fully captured by simple P/E screens.

**Governance and disclosure:** As a family-controlled Hong Kong–listed Chinese consumer company, minority shareholders depend on dividend policy and disclosure quality; the absence of primary filings here prevents assessment of board independence, related-party dealings, or pension/off-balance-sheet obligations.
