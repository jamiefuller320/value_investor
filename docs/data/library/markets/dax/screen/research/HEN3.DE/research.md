# Henkel AG & Co. KGaA (HEN3.DE) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-18T12:49:36.981293+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Henkel is a dual-segment German consumer and industrial group (Adhesive Technologies and Consumer Brands) that the quantitative screen rates as a buy on cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk metrics (P/E 15.2, P/B 1.5, yield 2.8%, D/E 18%). The investment case rests on a conservatively levered balance sheet, rising earnings per share despite modest revenue pressure, and shareholder returns via dividends and buybacks. The key debate is whether margin resilience and bolt-on M&A can offset sluggish top-line growth, currency headwinds, and competitive pressure in adhesives and branded consumer categories. Primary regulatory filings were not available in the research corpus, limiting confirmation of management guidance and risk disclosures.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, Henkel offers a rare combination of industrial-quality cash generation, low leverage, and screen-validated valuation. The DAX screen passes six models across all four value families—Graham Enterprising, Neff PEGY, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Acquirer's Multiple, and Financial Health—with a persistent buy signal held for five weeks and high data quality (19/20 metrics). At P/E 15.2 and P/B 1.49, the market prices Henkel below typical global staples/industrial peers despite EPS compounding from €2.95 (2022) to €4.92 (2025) per Yahoo data. Reported ROE of 9.7% is modest but supported by a net-debt-light capital structure (net debt €334m on €20.6bn equity at end-2025), leaving room for continued dividends (~€850m paid in 2025) and repurchases (~€856m). The Adhesive Technologies franchise provides structural exposure to packaging, automotive, and electronics end-markets, while Consumer Brands adds defensive cash flow. The screen’s Acquirer's Multiple and Financial Health passes align with Henkel’s bolt-on acquisition strategy (notably ~€1.35bn of business purchases in 2024 per Yahoo cash-flow data) without balance-sheet strain.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
Without filing body extracts, going-concern, covenant, and contingency language cannot be cited from primary sources; the following risks are inferred from financial data and news.

- **Top-line pressure:** Reported revenue declined in 2025 (Yahoo); organic growth in Q1 2026 (+1.7% per WSJ headline) may not fully offset FX and volume weakness.
- **Goodwill/intangible concentration:** €17.3bn of goodwill and intangibles (~84% of common equity) raises impairment risk if Adhesive Technologies or Consumer Brands underperform—2023 already saw €201m special charges.
- **Acquisition integration:** €1.35bn of business purchases in 2024 (Yahoo) plus ongoing bolt-ons (Q1 2026 “flurry of deals”) create execution and synergy risk.
- **Competitive and cyclical exposure:** Q1 2026 earnings commentary cites competitive pressures in adhesives; Consumer Brands faces private-label and input-cost volatility.
- **Currency:** News repeatedly flags currency headwinds; ~40%+ of sales are outside Europe (per general company structure cited in valuation articles), so EUR strength (EUR/USD ~1.14 per macro context) can compress reported growth.
- **ROE structurally low:** Screen ROE 9.7% and large intangible base limit return metrics versus best-in-class peers.
- **Pension:** €613m combined pension provisions (current + non-current, Yahoo 2025)—not alarming but a long-dated liability to monitor.
- **Governance / talent:** Departure of executive Nils Hemmerle to Gerolsteiner (Yahoo, 14 Apr 2026) is minor but signals competitive talent market.
