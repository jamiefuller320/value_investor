# Fresenius (FRE.DE) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-18T12:49:55.969479+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Fresenius SE & Co. KGaA (FRE.DE) screens as a buy on the DAX value composite, passing four factor families (cheapness, dividend, GARP, risk) with a persistent signal at roughly 60% composite score versus 57% sector-relative. The investment case rests on a multi-year earnings normalisation after 2023 losses, continued deleveraging, and valuation at roughly 15.6× P/E, 1.2× P/B and 2.5% yield, with a reported FCF yield near 7.7%. Primary regulatory filing bodies for the parent issuer were not retrieved in this research pack, so financial trends below rely on Yahoo fallback data and must be treated with that caveat. The central debate is whether post-restructuring margin and cash-flow improvement at Helios and Kabi can offset slower Fresenius Medical Care (FMC) US dialysis volumes, China headwinds and reimbursement pressure, against a balance sheet still carrying substantial goodwill and pension obligations.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, FRE.DE offers a classic recovery-at-a-discount profile: the quantitative screen flags cheapness (FCF Yield), growth-at-a-reasonable-price (Lynch PEG, Neff PEGY with PEG ≈ 0.17), dividend quality (Dividend Growth) and balance-sheet repair (Financial Health), with five of 22 models passed and high data quality (19/20 metrics). Business quality is underwritten by a diversified healthcare platform—Helios (hospitals), Fresenius Kabi (pharma/nutrition) and a strategic stake in FMC (dialysis)—serving largely non-cyclical end markets. Reported net income has recovered from a Yahoo-sourced loss of €594m in 2023 to €471m in 2024 and €1,264m in 2025, while net debt has fallen from €11.5bn to €9.2bn over the same period, supporting the screen’s risk-family pass. ROE remains modest at 7.8% (screen) versus pre-impairment peaks, which keeps the name in value rather than quality territory and aligns with the GARP framing rather than a pure compounder thesis.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
Without primary filing bodies, going-concern, covenant and contingency language could not be reviewed; this is a material research gap for a regulated German KGaA.

**Structural and balance-sheet:**
- High goodwill/intangibles (€16.7bn) versus tangible equity (€2.4bn) leave limited hard-asset cover if impairments recur.
- Pension and post-retirement liabilities (~€1.9bn) add off-balance-sheet-style drag not fully captured by simple leverage ratios.
- Declining reported FCF despite higher earnings may signal working-capital normalisation, elevated capex or one-offs — warrants confirmation in the next annual report.

**Operating and regulatory:**
- Group complexity: FMC (~dialysis) performance, US CMS reimbursement proposals and declining US treatment volumes (news, May–Jun 2025/2026) affect consolidated value via the stake and potential capital calls/dividends.
- China headwinds cited in Bloomberg (“Fresenius Posts Narrow Profit Miss Amid Headwinds From China”, 6 May 2026) — geographic mix risk at Kabi/Helios.
- FMC lost EU backing for TAVNEOS® (news, 2 Jul 2026) — pipeline/regulatory setback at a key subsidiary.
- Humacyte regained ex-US Symvess rights from Fresenius (24 Apr 2025) — signals portfolio rationalisation but also prior strategic missteps.

**Governance / execution:**
- Ongoing restructuring (Helios strategy refinement, video updates through Jun 2026) implies execution risk until margins stabilise.
- Discontinued operations still present in accounts (−€272m in 2025), adding EPS noise.
