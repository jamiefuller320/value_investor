# Wipro Limited (WIT) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-18T18:30:55.185776+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Wipro screens as a Strong Buy on classic value metrics—P/E 14.3, P/B 2.1, ROE 15.4%, debt/equity 23%, and a headline yield of 10.4%—with 12 of 22 quantitative models passing across cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk families. The investment case rests on capital return (large buybacks and dividends) and a defensible balance sheet, not on growth leadership: Indian IT services revenue has been broadly flat for several years and management’s latest outlook is cautious. The central debate is whether depressed multiples and aggressive shareholder returns adequately compensate for sub-scale growth, margin pressure from AI investment, and persistent underperformance versus larger Indian IT peers. Deep-dive filing extracts are largely unusable for line-item analysis; operational conclusions lean on Yahoo financials (explicitly flagged below) and recent news flow.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, Wipro offers a rare combination in global IT services: statistically cheap equity (Graham Enterprising, FCF Yield, Acquirer’s Multiple, Dreman Contrarian, Composite Value all pass), acceptable balance-sheet quality (Financial Health, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat), and an unusually high apparent income return (High Dividend Yield, Dividend Growth). The screen’s 93% composite score (89% sector-relative) with 19/20 data-quality metrics supports that this is not a single-factor trap.

Business quality is mixed rather than poor. Wipro remains a scaled offshore IT and consulting platform (Capco in BFSI, Harman/connected services, design via Designit), with FY26 large-deal momentum cited in news ($7.8bn wins on ~$10.5bn revenue narrative). ROE of 15.4% and low reported leverage align with “quality” model passes. However, revenue growth has lagged the sector: Yahoo data show total revenue roughly flat from FY2023 (₹904.9bn) through FY2025 (₹890.9bn) before a modest rebound in FY2026 (₹926.2bn)—a profile more consistent with a capital-return value name than a GARP compounder, despite the Neff PEGY pass.

The Strong Buy signal is therefore best interpreted as **valuation + payout + balance sheet**, not as endorsement of superior organic growth. CEO Srinivas Pallia’s turnaround (in post since 2024) has yet to close the gap with TCS, Infosys, and HCLTech, and the screen’s conviction score is 0% with a new, one-week signal—timing data are insufficient. For value investors willing to tolerate cyclical IT spending and execution risk, Wipro fits the screen; for those requiring growth confirmation, the same data argue for patience rather than aggressive sizing.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Operational / cyclical:** Indian IT sector headwinds—cautious client spending, longer deal cycles, AI-related pricing and investment pressure—are recurring themes in news and the Q1 FY2027 call summaries. Wipro has **lagged peers** on growth and recent estimates (Reuters, 16 July 2026; JPMorgan target cuts, 17 July 2026). Americas exposure remains a sensitivity.

**Earnings quality:** Heavy reliance on **investment/security gains** in reported net income (Yahoo unusual-items breakdown) may overstate underlying services profitability; value screens passing on earnings yield may be partly distorted.

**Governance / insider:** News reports **CFO Aparna Iyer selling 45,000 shares** (GuruFocus, 20 May 2026). Premji-related entities hold a dominant stake (~60% per simplywall.st, October 2025)—aligned long-term ownership, but limited float-driven price discovery.

**Balance sheet / pensions / contingencies:** 20-F XBRL tags in body extracts reference **tax contingent liabilities**, **other contingent liabilities**, **onerous contract provisions**, **defined-benefit obligations**, **allowance for credit losses**, and **written put options to non-controlling interests**—but **no quantified disclosure or covenant language** is readable in extracts. Net debt rose to ₹62.3bn at FY2026 (Yahoo) from ₹39.8bn at FY2025 despite large cash balances, reflecting working-capital and debt mix shifts. Goodwill/intangibles at ~47% of total assets (FY2026) create acquisition-impairment risk.

**Regulatory / going concern:** No going-concern qualification found in available body text; absence of prose limits comfort. Macro context file is **missing**—no additional regime colour applied.

**Competitive / structural:** Fourth-largest Indian IT exporter status implies scale disadvantage vs TCS/Infosys in large deals and talent; Capco adds consulting mix but also cyclical BFSI exposure. AI could compress legacy outsourcing margins before revenue from AI services scales.
