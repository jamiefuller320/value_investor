## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Steadfast Group Limited is an ASX-listed insurance broking consolidator that the quantitative screen rates as a **buy**, passing eight of 22 models across cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk families at a composite score of 61%. On secondary Yahoo data (no ASX primary filings are available in the research library), the group shows a multi-year revenue and earnings trajectory, a 3.8% dividend yield, 16.5% ROE, and roughly A$490m FY2025 free cash flow — metrics that align with the screen’s Graham, Lynch, Buffett Quality, and FCF Yield passes. ## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, SDF presents as a **quality compounder trading at a reasonable multiple** rather than a deep cyclical discount.

**Quantitative alignment.** The screen passes five metric families — cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk — with high data quality (19/20 metrics). Specific passes include Graham Enterprising (P/E 16.4, P/B 2.51), FCF Yield (screen FCF ~A$582m), Lynch and Neff PEG/PEGY models, Quality Value, Buffett Quality (ROE 16.5%), Dividend Growth, and Financial Health (debt/equity 76%). Conviction is still **building** at 44% after two weeks at signal, which suggests the quantitative case is recent rather than long-established.

**Business quality (inferred from financial profile).** Yahoo annual data (FY2022–FY2025) show operating revenue rising from A$793m to A$1,320m (+67% over three years) and operating income from A$183m to A$266m, indicating scale gains with modest margin expansion. Reported diluted EPS grew from A$0.185 (FY2022) to A$0.304 (FY2025), a ~65% cumulative increase. Cash generation is robust: FY2025 free cash flow of A$490m comfortably covered dividends paid of A$249m. The group holds substantial cash (A$1.60bn at FY2025 year-end per Yahoo) and earns significant net interest income (A$127m in FY2025), consistent with an insurance broking model that holds client premiums in trust.

**Why buy, not just hold.** The name clears multiple independent value frameworks simultaneously — unusual for Financial Services — while offering income (3.8% yield) and growth (GARP passes). Debt/equity at 76% is below the screen’s 100% threshold, and working capital remains positive (A$785m FY2025). For a value investor, the hook is **predictable cash-backed dividends plus accretive consolidation in a fragmented broking market**, available at mid-teens earnings rather than premium multiples.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**1. No primary filing verification.** Without ASX annual reports or half-year releases, going-concern statements, contingent liabilities, related-party transactions, and debt covenants **cannot be assessed**. This is the single largest research gap.

**2. Goodwill and impairment cycle.** Negative tangible book (A$777m) and repeated impairments (A$63m FY2024; A$139m FY2025 per Yahoo) signal that prior acquisitions may be underperforming expectations. Further write-downs would hit reported equity and could compress P/B — already a screen-monitored metric at 2.5×.

**3. Leverage and acquisition dependency.** Total debt has risen ~74% since FY2022 while the group continues bolt-on M&A (A$382m cash spent in FY2025). The roll-up model requires disciplined pricing; overpayment erodes returns and increases impairment risk. Screen debt/equity of 76% is manageable but trending with acquisition pace.

**4. Takeover overhang.** On 10 June 2026, Steadfast entered an exclusivity and process deed with Amwins and Dragoneer; KKR subsequently joined the consortium (14 July 2026). A bid process introduces **binary outcome risk**: potential premium capture versus loss of standalone compounding and dividend trajectory if the company delists. Value investors must weigh whether current pricing already embeds bid expectations.

**5. Insurance broking cyclicality.** Soft insurance pricing cycles compress broking commissions and placement income. Yahoo data do not segment organic vs acquired growth or premium-rate sensitivity; this cyclical exposure is **not captured** by static value screens.

**6. Governance and concentration.** Google News (18 October 2025) notes ~58% institutional ownership — supportive for liquidity but offering limited insight into board independence or executive incentive alignment. No governance red flags surfaced in available sources, but coverage is insufficient to clear this area.

**7. Valuation scepticism in market commentary.** A Simply Wall St headline (18 August 2025) argued the business had “yet to catch up with its share price,” implying the market may be ahead of fundamental delivery — a tension with the screen’s cheapness classification.
