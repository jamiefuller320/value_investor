## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
Without ASX filing body extracts, standard risk disclosures (going-concern statements, contingent liabilities, covenant terms, related-party transactions) cannot be reviewed; this is a material research gap for an ASX-listed name.

**Cyclical and operational:** ARB operates in consumer cyclical 4WD accessories—a category tied to new vehicle sales, discretionary spending, and off-road tourism. FY2023’s impairment and FY2025’s margin compression suggest earnings sensitivity to volume and cost inflation. News coverage (30 Dec 2025) flags slowing returns on capital, which warrants monitoring as the company invests in US expansion, retail stores, and acquisitions.

**Working capital and inventory:** Inventory of A$249m with A$16.8m in allowance provisions indicates scale and obsolescence risk if demand softens; FY2025 inventory rose modestly versus FY2024 (A$240m).

**Foreign exchange and geographic expansion:** H1 FY2026 commentary (news, Feb 2026) cites currency headwinds and OEM partnership challenges alongside US growth—suggesting execution risk in international scaling without filing-level detail on segment profitability.

**Governance and payout:** The FY2025 dividend cut (A$23.5m vs A$44.7m prior year) is unexplained in available sources; combined with insider buying, this may signal management confidence in the share price but reduced near-term cash returns to shareholders.

**Competitive:** The aftermarket 4WD accessories market faces competition from OEM fit-outs, private-label products, and international brands; ARB’s moat (screen pass: Economic Moat) rests on brand and distribution, but is not impervious to vehicle-platform shifts (e.g., EV 4WD adoption).

**Institutional concentration:** ~52% institutional ownership (Sep 2025) implies sensitivity to fund flows and potential volatility on earnings misses.
