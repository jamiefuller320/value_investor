## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Data and governance:** Absence of primary ASX filing extracts prevents review of going-concern statements, contingent liabilities, covenant terms, related-party transactions, or reserve/resource audits. This is the largest research red flag in this pass.

**Jurisdiction and operating risk:** WAF operates in Burkina Faso. Without filing language, specific security, permitting, or fiscal-regime risks cannot be quoted, but West African gold producers face elevated geopolitical, community, and regulatory risk relative to ASX peers—a factor screens do not capture.

**Commodity cyclicality:** FY2025 earnings (EBITDA A$940m on revenue A$1.53bn) embed favourable gold-price and cost conditions. A material gold-price decline would compress margins quickly; the low P/E may reflect the market’s cyclical discount rather than mispricing.

**Balance-sheet and cash-flow signals:**
- Inventory doubled YoY (A$264.9m)—possible working-capital drag or stockpile build; needs filing disclosure to interpret
- FY2024 negative FCF (A$236m) during peak capex; FY2025 recovery depends on sustaining production rates
- Rising interest costs and A$453m gross debt create sensitivity to rates and gold prices despite net cash

**Minority interests and dilution:** Performance-rights conversion listed 190,445 additional shares (Jul 2025 news)—modest dilution but signals ongoing equity incentive issuance. FY2024 equity raise (A$150m issuance) and debt raise funded growth; further capital markets activity cannot be ruled out without filings.

**Pension:** Employee benefit obligations are small (A$3.7m) and not a material red flag on available data.

**Competitive:** Gold is a commodity; WAF’s moat rests on ore-body quality and cost position—screen “Economic Moat” pass should be treated sceptically without reserve-life and AISC disclosure from primary sources.
