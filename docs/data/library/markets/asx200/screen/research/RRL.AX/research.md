## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Regis Resources is a mid-tier Australian gold producer whose shares screen as a **Strong Buy** on quantitative value metrics—P/E 8.9, 3.2% yield, 29.2% ROE, and 16 of 22 models passed—supported by a sharp earnings recovery in FY2025 after two loss-making years. The investment case rests on operational turnaround (revenue up ~30% to A$1.65bn, net profit A$254m), strong free cash flow (A$545m), and a net cash balance sheet after A$300m debt repayment. The central debate is whether current cheapness reflects durable quality or cyclical gold-price tailwinds, compounded by the failed Vault Minerals merger (July 2026) that leaves Regis as a standalone ~300–400k oz producer while rivals consolidate into a A$13bn sector giant.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The quantitative screen rates RRL a **Strong Buy** (composite score 78%, sector-relative 79%; conviction 56%, building over two weeks). Five factor families pass—cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk—including Graham Defensive/Enterprising, FCF Yield, Buffett Quality, Piotroski F-Score (8), and Financial Health (current ratio 2.63).

For a value investor, the appeal is multi-dimensional:

1. **Cheapness with quality:** P/E 8.9 and earnings yield well above bond alternatives; P/E × P/B ≈ 20.5 sits below Graham’s 22.5 threshold despite P/B 2.3 (reflecting strong ROE rather than distressed book).
2. **Cash-generative operations:** Screen FCF ~A$654m; Yahoo FY2025 FCF A$545m on operating cash flow A$821m—genuine cash conversion, not accounting earnings alone.
3. **Balance-sheet repair:** Total debt fell from A$366m (FY2024) to A$119m (FY2025), with cash rising to A$505m—a net cash position of roughly A$386m, de-risking the cyclical profile.
4. **Dividend discipline:** 3.2% yield with dividend-growth screen pass; shareholder returns supported by FCF rather than leverage.

The screen’s “quality” and “risk” passes align with FY2025 fundamentals: return to profitability, margin expansion (EBITDA A$784m vs A$106m prior year), and working capital of A$425m. The standalone business appears operationally sound; the main qualitative gap is strategic scale relative to consolidating peers.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Cyclical / commodity**
Gold price drives revenue (A$1,647m FY2025) and margins. FY2024’s A$186m loss demonstrates earnings volatility when prices or grades disappoint. Forward earnings embedded in P/E 8.9 assume current conditions persist.

**Strategic / M&A**
The agreed A$7.7–10.7bn all-scrip merger with Vault Minerals (May 2025) would have created Australia’s third-largest gold producer (>700k oz p.a.). Regis withdrew its competing bid on 12 July 2026 after Genesis Minerals’ superior US$3.9bn offer; Genesis–Vault closed path to an A$13bn combined entity (14 July 2026). Regis remains standalone with uncertain growth strategy—scale disadvantage versus consolidating peers is a material strategic risk not captured by backward-looking screens.

**Operational**
Perenti’s Duketon mining contract extension (April 2026, ~A$180m over 12 months to March 2029) provides contractor stability, but production concentration and reserve-life data are unavailable without filings.

**Balance sheet / provisions**
Long-term provisions of A$172m (rehabilitation) and deferred tax liabilities (A$125m non-current) represent meaningful off-P&L obligations. Without filing extracts, closure-cost assumptions and discount rates cannot be assessed.

**Governance**
A November 2025 article flagged CEO compensation as potentially generous relative to performance (“We Think Shareholders Will Probably Be Generous With Regis Resources Limited's CEO Compensation,” Simply Wall St, 14 Nov 2025)—minor flag absent proxy detail.

**Regulatory**
Standard Australian mining environmental and native-title frameworks apply; no specific regulatory actions appear in the news manifest.

**Data quality**
Absence of primary ASX filings is the largest research red flag: contingencies, related-party transactions, and going-concern assessments cannot be reviewed.
