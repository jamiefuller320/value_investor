## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Perenti Limited is an ASX-listed mining services group (underground contractor Barminco, surface drilling, technology) that screens as a **Strong Buy** on quantitative value metrics: P/E 16.0, P/B 1.05, dividend yield 3.5%, FCF yield 8.4%, and D/E 35%, with nine of 22 models passed across cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk families. The investment case rests on a multi-year earnings recovery (revenue and net profit have risen materially since FY2022), improving balance-sheet leverage, and a run of contract wins that extend revenue visibility into the late 2020s. Valuation appears modest relative to cash generation and book value, with capital return via dividends and an active on-market buy-back. The central debate is whether contract backlog and integration benefits (including Technosia) offset cyclical mining-services risk, customer concentration, and modest reported ROE (7.8%) in a capital-intensive, goodwill-heavy business — all assessed here without access to primary ASX annual or interim filing text.

Contract extensions and new awards — Bellevue Gold (~A$850m over four years from August 2026), Barrick Fourmile (US$192m), and Regis Duketon (+~A$180m to March 2029) — speak to franchise strength in Australian underground mining, Perenti’s core competency.

The screen’s risk-family pass is notable given sector cyclicality: balance-sheet metrics are not stretched (D/E 35%, cash A$481m vs total debt A$786m), and interest coverage on FY2025 EBIT (~A$287m) against interest expense (~A$73m) appears comfortable (~4x). The gap between screen signal (strong buy) and conviction score (42%, “building”, two weeks at signal) reflects timing uncertainty rather than fundamental rejection — appropriate for a name where deep filing review is currently unavailable.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Cyclical and contract risk.** Mining services revenue is tied to customer production plans and exploration cycles. FY2025 revenue growth slowed to mid-single digits; news on the Half Year 2026 call (23 Feb 2026) flagged short-term revenue challenges despite strategic integration progress. Contract wins (Bellevue, Barrick, Regis) improve visibility but do not eliminate commodity-price or mine-plan deferral risk.

**Customer concentration.** Large multi-year awards imply dependence on a relatively small set of tier-one gold miners. Loss, re-tender, or scope reduction at a flagship site would disproportionately affect earnings — a standard sector risk not quantified in available sources.

**Goodwill and acquisition integration.** Goodwill of A$457m (Yahoo FY2025) equals roughly a quarter of total assets. FY2024 included A$74m business acquisition spend; Technosia integration is cited as ongoing with near-term revenue friction. Without filing footnotes, impairment triggers and integration costs cannot be assessed from primary documents.

**Leverage and pensions.** Net debt has improved, but total debt remains A$786m with A$73m interest expense. Current pension/post-retirement obligations of A$108m sit in current liabilities — a working-capital and cash-flow call that screens may underweight. No covenant or going-concern language is available from filing bodies (absent).

**Execution and dilution.** Basic average shares rose from ~690m (FY2023) to ~933m (FY2025), reflecting equity issuance history; buy-backs (~5m shares cumulative per July 2026 news) partially offset but do not fully reverse dilution. Minority interests absorbed A$17m of group profit in FY2025.

**Governance and disclosure.** Absence of ASX filing extracts in this research pass is itself a limitation: regulatory, environmental, and native-title/contingency disclosures typical in Australian mining-services annual reports cannot be reviewed here.

**Macro.** Geopolitical and energy-price volatility referenced in generalist market articles (May–Jun 2026) can affect miner capex budgets and contract pricing, though PRN-specific impact is not quantified in the manifest.
