## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Capstone Copper is a diversified Americas copper producer whose CDI on the ASX tracks the TSX-listed parent. The quantitative screen flags a buy on cheapness (P/E 15.7, P/B ~1.0), acceptable leverage (D/E ~42%), and improving quality (ROE 12.5%, positive FCF). Yahoo financials show a sharp earnings inflection in FY2025—revenue to US$2.36bn and net income to US$316m—after a loss-making FY2023, with free cash flow turning positive for the first time in four years. The investment case rests on copper exposure, record operating momentum, and growth optionality (Mantoverde Optimised, Santo Domingo), while the key debate is whether current profitability survives copper-price volatility, strike/disruption risk, and heavy growth capex (~US$519m in FY2025). Primary filing review is not possible in this library; conviction is therefore tempered by secondary-source reliance.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, CSC.AX offers a cyclical materials name trading near book with a reasonable earnings yield and a balance sheet that screens as manageable. The screen passes eight of 22 models across three families—cheapness, quality, and risk—including Graham Enterprising, Schloss Low P/B, Deep Value, Quality Value, Magic Formula, Piotroski F-Score, Composite Value, and Financial Health. That combination is unusual for a pure copper producer: the name is not merely cheap on price momentum but also clears basic financial-health and quality filters (ROE 12.5%, reported FCF ~US$219m on the screen snapshot).

Business quality, per Yahoo data, has improved materially. Revenue compounded from US$1.30bn (2022) to US$2.36bn (2025); operating income rose from US$134m to US$482m over the same span. FY2023 was a trough (net loss US$102m, negative FCF), but FY2024–FY2025 show recovery and scale. FY2025 operating cash flow reached US$685m against capex of US$519m, yielding positive FCF of US$166m—a meaningful shift from three prior years of negative FCF (2022–2024: −US$472m, −US$500m, −US$49m respectively). Net debt edged down to US$750m at FY2025 year-end (from US$898m in 2023) despite higher gross debt, supported by cash of US$304m.

The screen’s buy signal aligns with a business transitioning from investment-heavy, loss-volatile phase toward cash-generative production at higher copper prices. For value investors, the hook is book-adjacent valuation on normalising earnings, with Piotroski and financial-health passes suggesting the turnaround is not purely narrative. The signal has persisted four weeks (conviction score 35%, stable trend), which supports patience rather than momentum chasing.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclical and commodity risk.** Revenue and EBITDA are highly sensitive to copper prices. FY2025’s surge coincides with elevated copper markets; a price downturn would compress margins quickly given fixed cost base and depreciation (US$482m in FY2025).

**Operational disruption.** News reports (January 2026) reference strike-related disruption at Mantoverde; the company issued a media response. Labour relations and Chilean operational risk are material and not captured in static screen metrics.

**Growth capex and project execution.** News highlights advancing Mantoverde Optimised and Santo Domingo toward final investment decisions. FY2025 capex of US$519m consumed most operating cash flow; sustained high spend could keep FCF tight or require additional debt/equity if copper weakens.

**Balance-sheet items requiring filing verification.** Yahoo shows US$246m due to related parties (non-current), US$259m long-term provisions, and US$696m non-current deferred tax liabilities—all typical for miners but unverified without annual-report notes. Asset impairment of US$211m in FY2025 signals asset-level write-downs whose triggers and scope are unknown without filings.

**Earnings quality.** FY2025 includes large unusual/special items and securities gains/losses. Value screens passing on headline ROE and FCF may overstate through-cycle quality if normalised earnings are materially lower.

**Governance and disclosure.** ASX CDI structure adds a layer between Australian holders and Canadian primary listings; without indexed filings, pension, remediation, and litigation contingencies cannot be assessed from filing language (going concern, contingencies, covenants absent).

**No dividend.** Cash dividends paid: US$0 across all Yahoo years—appropriate for a growth capex phase but limits income-oriented value support.
