## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Yancoal Australia is a pure-play Australian coal producer trading below book value on a quantitative screen that flags cheapness, dividend yield, GARP, and balance-sheet risk metrics. The investment case rests on a net-cash balance sheet and asset backing through the commodity downcycle, with FY2025 free cash flow still positive despite a sharp earnings contraction from peak-cycle levels. The central debate is whether trough earnings and a $2.4bn Kestrel acquisition (shifting exposure toward metallurgical coal) represent a cyclical reset at an attractive entry point, or a structural step-down in thermal-coal economics with dividend and capital-allocation risk. Primary ASX regulatory filings are unavailable in the research library, limiting verification of rehabilitation provisions, covenants, and going-concern language.

The quantitative screen rates YAL as a buy with four of four factor families passing: cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk. For a value investor, the hook is straightforward: the stock trades at a discount to tangible book (~A$8.9bn per Yahoo balance sheet) while holding net cash and generating positive free cash flow at the bottom of the coal price cycle. Production reached a company record in FY2025 (67 million tonnes ROM, per news coverage), suggesting operational quality has not deteriorated even as revenue and margins have compressed. The screen's risk-family pass aligns with minimal leverage (total debt A$84m against A$9.0bn equity at FY2025). The GARP and dividend passes suggest the market may be pricing permanent impairment rather than normal cyclicality—a classic deep-value setup if commodity prices stabilise or met-coal exposure via Kestrel improves the mix.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclical and commodity risk:** Earnings are highly levered to global coal prices. The four-year revenue and EBITDA trajectory shows severe cyclicality; screens may capture cheapness at a trough but cannot forecast price recovery. Thermal coal faces long-term demand uncertainty; the Kestrel pivot toward metallurgical coal mitigates but does not eliminate this.

**Dividend and capital allocation:** FY2025 dividends (A$769m) exceeded both net income and free cash flow. Without filing disclosure, payout policy intent and sustainability are unclear. The A$2.4bn Kestrel acquisition (April 2026 news) would consume substantially all cash on hand (A$2,127m at FY2025) if funded from balance sheet, materially changing the net-cash profile that underpins the screen's risk pass.

**Rehabilitation and provisions:** Long-term provisions of A$1,294m on the balance sheet (Yahoo) represent a significant off-P&L liability typical of Australian coal miners. Magnitude, discount rates, and regulatory requirements cannot be verified without the annual report.

**Governance and related-party risk:** Not assessable from available filings or news manifest. Yancoal operates under Chinese parent ownership (general industry knowledge); no specific related-party or governance disclosures are present in the source set.

**M&A execution:** Kestrel is a large, transformative deal relative to market capitalisation. Integration, funding structure (debt vs cash vs equity), and returns on invested capital are unverified without transaction documents or filing extracts.

**Regulatory and ESG:** Australian coal mining faces ongoing environmental regulation, rehabilitation bonding, and export/market-access scrutiny. No specific regulatory actions appear in the news manifest.

**Screen limitations:** ROE of 4.8% is weak for a "buy" on quality grounds; conviction score is only 35%. Timing signal is insufficient. Primary filing absence prevents assessment of going-concern statements, covenant compliance, or contingent liabilities.
