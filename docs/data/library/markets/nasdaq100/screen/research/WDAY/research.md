# Workday, Inc. (WDAY) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-18T12:46:07.489601+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Workday is a leading enterprise cloud platform for human capital management (HCM), financial management, and AI-enabled workflow automation, serving more than 11,500 organisations and over 75 million users under contract (FY2026 10-K, filed 6 March 2026). The quantitative screen rates WDAY a **buy** (4/22 models; 80% composite), driven chiefly by free cash flow yield, Lynch PEG, composite value, and financial health — not headline growth multiples alone.

The valuation hook is a durable subscription backlog and rising cash conversion at a price the market has de-rated alongside the broader SaaS complex: subscription remaining performance obligations (RPO) stood at **$27.3 billion** as of 30 April 2026 (Q1 FY2027 10-Q), while the company repurchased **$1.6 billion** of stock in Q1 at an average **$133.05** per share. The central debate is whether Workday is an AI-disruption casualty (alongside Oracle, SAP, ServiceNow, and Microsoft) or an AI beneficiary with mission-critical ERP/HCM switching costs; interim results favour the latter, but sector sentiment remains the swing factor.

---

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value-oriented investor, Workday offers a rare combination in large-cap software: **double-digit organic growth, expanding margins, and high free cash flow conversion** at a price where the screen finds genuine cheapness on cash-based metrics.

**Business quality.** Workday operates a single cloud-applications segment with subscription-heavy revenue (>90% of total in Q1 FY2027). Customer scale (65%+ of the Fortune 500), weekly product updates, and deep HCM/finance domain expertise create high switching costs. FY2026 acquisitions — Flowise, Paradox, Sana, and Pipedream (10-K) — extend AI agent and knowledge capabilities rather than dilute the core platform.

**Quantitative screen alignment.** The buy signal passes three of four factor families (cheapness, GARP, risk). Key screen metrics: **P/E 45.2**, **FCF yield 8.7%**, **Lynch PEG 0.18**, **ROE 10.9%**, **FCF ~$3.1bn**. The PEG and FCF yield suggest the market is pricing Workday as if growth has stalled, while filings show subscription revenue still growing ~14% year on year in Q1 FY2027.

**Operating leverage emerging.** Q1 FY2027 operating income was **$338m** on **$2,542m** revenue (13.3% margin) versus **$39m** on **$2,240m** (1.7%) in Q1 FY2026, after the prior-year quarter absorbed **$166m** of restructuring charges. Restructuring charges were **$0** in Q1 FY2027, indicating the FY2026 workforce reduction plan is largely behind the P&L.

**Capital return.** Share repurchases have accelerated: **$1.6bn** in Q1 FY2027 versus **$293m** in Q1 FY2026, with **$1.3bn** authorisation remaining (September 2025 programme). This aligns capital allocation with a depressed share price.

**Risk family pass** reflects investment-grade balance-sheet structure: **$3.0bn** senior notes, undrawn **$1.0bn** revolver, leverage covenant compliance disclosed in Q1 FY2027 10-Q, and no going-concern language in filings.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Competitive and cyclical.** The FY2026 10-K names Oracle, SAP, ServiceNow, Microsoft, ADP, and others as direct competitors and warns that AI platform entrants may "develop applications or acquire companies" in Workday's markets. Macro uncertainty (tariffs, inflation, interest rates, FX) is flagged in forward-looking statements. Sector-wide SaaS de-rating remains a live cyclical headwind not captured by company-specific screens.

**AI execution risk.** FY2026 M&A (Paradox, Sana, Flowise, Pipedream) increases integration and goodwill risk; goodwill reached **$5.23bn** (Yahoo, FY2026). AI product monetisation via Flex Credits is early-stage.

**Governance and insider activity.** Dual-class structure (Class B carries 10 votes per share) limits outside influence, explicitly cited as a risk in the FY2025 10-K. Co-founder **David Duffield** conducted multiple disclosed share sales in 2026 (news manifest, March–July 2026).

**Restructuring and management transition.** The FY2026 Restructuring Plan (~8% headcount reduction, FY2025 10-K) created $166m Q1 FY2026 charges. **CEO transition to Carl Eschenbach/Bhusri** (Bhusri appointed CEO per Q1 FY2027 10-Q, February 2026) introduces execution uncertainty; Stifel lowered its price target on the CEO change (9 February 2026, news manifest).

**Regulatory and legal.** News reports a **lawsuit over AI hiring practices** (GuruFocus, 23 June 2026). Filing language (Q1 FY2027 10-Q, Legal Proceedings): management believes there is **not a reasonable possibility of material loss** beyond recorded accruals as of 30 April 2026 — but litigation risk is real.

**Financial structure.** Share-based compensation was **$409m** in Q1 FY2027 (~16% of revenue), a persistent GAAP earnings drag. **$3.0bn** fixed-rate senior notes mature 2027–2032; revolver covenants require max leverage **3.50:1.00** (compliant as of 30 April 2026 per 10-Q). No pension obligations of concern are disclosed; no going-concern qualification.

**Customer/revenue concentration.** No single customer exceeded 10% of receivables or revenue in Q1 FY2027 (10-Q) — positive, but enterprise deal timing remains seasonally Q4-weighted per 10-K.
