# PACCAR Inc (PCAR) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-18T12:43:04.530227+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
PACCAR is a premium North American and European commercial-truck OEM (Kenworth, Peterbilt, DAF) with structurally attractive Parts and Financial Services adjacencies that support through-cycle returns. The quantitative screen rates the name a buy on cheapness, dividend, GARP and risk factors (PEGY 0.21, Magic Formula, Financial Health, among others), though headline P/E of 26.9x sits above a pure deep-value threshold. The central debate is whether 2025’s earnings step-down marks a cyclical trough or a longer normalisation after the 2023–24 peak, and whether rising finance receivable provisions signal early credit stress. Filings confirm revenue and profit pressure through 2025, but Q1 2026 shows stabilising truck revenue and higher net income year-on-year; deep research broadly supports the screen’s accumulate stance, with medium cyclical risk.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value-oriented investor, PCAR offers a high-quality industrial franchise rather than a distressed cyclical. The business model combines truck manufacturing (~74% of 2024 net sales and revenues per the FY2024 10-K), a recurring Parts distribution arm, and captive Financial Services tied to Kenworth/Peterbilt/DAF customers — a mix that screens well on dividend growth, financial health and composite value metrics.

The screen passes four of four factor families (cheapness, dividend, GARP, risk) with a 70% composite score and 52% conviction over five stable weeks. Neff PEGY (0.21) and implied growth (~19.7% in the screen snapshot) suggest the market may be under-pricing normalised earnings power relative to forward expectations, even as trailing EPS fell sharply in 2025. ROE of 13.1% and positive FCF (~$1.5bn in the screen) indicate balance-sheet-backed quality.

Business quality anchors the case: global Class 8 leadership, vertically integrated engines, dealer-centric distribution, and a captive finance book that generates fee income while supporting unit sales. Parts and Financial Services provide counter-cyclical ballast that pure OEM peers lack. The buy signal is therefore less about absolute cheapness on peak earnings and more about quality-at-a-reasonable-growth-price after a freight-cycle correction.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclical / demand:** Class 8 truck markets are highly cyclical. Filing data shows double-digit revenue declines through 2025; a prolonged freight recession would further pressure unit deliveries and factory utilisation.

**Financial Services credit:** Finance receivables of ~$19bn fund a material share of group assets. Provisions for losses rose sharply (Q1 2026: $44m vs $18m prior year; 9M 2025: $84m vs $50m). The 10-Q notes ASU credit-loss measurement standards but does not indicate systemic deterioration; nonetheless, rising provisions in a softer freight market are a red flag.

**Leverage / funding:** FS segment carries ~$15bn in commercial paper and term notes (Q1 2026 10-Q). This is structurally normal for a captive finance arm but creates refinancing and spread risk in a higher-rate environment (10-year Treasury proxy ~4.5% per macro context).

**Competitive / technology:** Electric and alternative-power trucks (e.g. Tesla Semi coverage in news flow) and emissions compliance remain structural risks. PACCAR’s EPA-related DEF software update programme (Jul 2025) highlights ongoing regulatory interaction.

**Pension / OPEB:** Both annual 10-K index filings reference defined-benefit plans and industry pension funds (e.g. Metal and Electrical Engineering Industry Pension Fund in XBRL tags), but **pension funded-status figures are not readable in the truncated 10-K bodies**. Treat as an unquantified risk pending full filing review.

**Governance / legal:** 10-K and 10-Q indices reference Item 1A Risk Factors, Item 3 Legal Proceedings and Item 1C Cybersecurity, but **narrative risk-factor and legal text is not present in available body extracts** (10-Q Part II cross-references the annual 10-K). No going-concern language was found in available extracts.

**Earnings quality:** Repeated securities gains ($346m in FY2025 per Yahoo) inflate reported earnings; normalised profitability is weaker than GAAP net income suggests.

**Valuation:** Screen P/E of 26.9x on depressed trailing EPS is not optically cheap; the value case depends on cycle recovery and normalised earnings.
