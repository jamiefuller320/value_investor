## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Genting Singapore operates Resorts World Sentosa (RWS), Singapore’s only integrated resort outside Marina Bay Sands, spanning gaming, hospitality, attractions and MICE. The quantitative screen flags a **buy** on asset-backed cheapness (P/B 0.93), a 6.3% dividend yield, negligible leverage, and broad financial-health model passes — yet reported profitability has turned down sharply after the post-pandemic recovery peak. FY2025 net income fell to S$390m from S$579m in FY2024 (−33%), with 1H 2025 EPS of S$0.019 versus S$0.03 in 1H 2024 per headline coverage, raising questions about dividend sustainability and the return profile on heavy capex. The central debate is whether sub-book valuation and net-cash balance-sheet quality compensate for cyclical earnings pressure, licence/regulatory dependence, and majority control by Genting-linked entities (~53% per news coverage). Primary SGX annual/interim filing bodies were not available in the source library, limiting regulatory-grade confirmation of trends, contingencies and capital-allocation disclosures.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, G13 presents a **fortress-balance-sheet, below-book asset play** with an income overlay, rather than a classic earnings-compounder.

**What the screen captures well**

- **Cheapness:** P/B 0.93 passes Schloss Low P/B and Deep Value; tangible book value was S$8.05bn at FY2025 year-end (Yahoo) against total assets of S$9.19bn — the market prices the operating franchise at a discount to stated net assets.
- **Dividend:** 6.3% yield passes High Dividend Yield and Dividend Growth; cash dividends of ~S$483m were paid in both FY2024 and FY2025 (Yahoo cash-flow), consistent with a shareholder-return mandate.
- **Risk / balance sheet:** D/E effectively 0% (total debt S$2.6m, essentially capital leases); cash and equivalents ~S$3.2bn; working capital S$2.7bn — supporting Financial Health, Magic Formula and Acquirer’s Multiple passes.
- **Sector-relative score:** Composite 60% vs sector 53%, with all four factor families (cheapness, dividend, GARP, risk) represented among eight passing models.

**Business quality overlay**

RWS holds a structurally advantaged position as one of two licensed integrated resorts in Singapore, with diversified non-gaming revenue (Universal Studios, S.E.A. Aquarium, hotels, MICE). Post-COVID recovery drove revenue from S$1.73bn (FY2022) to S$2.53bn (FY2024), but FY2025 revenue eased to S$2.45bn and operating income fell to S$455m from S$593m — a **normalisation / investment-cycle pause** rather than balance-sheet distress. ROE of 4.7% (screen) is modest and reflects both the asset-heavy model and the recent earnings step-down; value investors may accept low ROE if book value is real and capex is value-accretive.

**Why buy, with nuance**

The buy case rests on **(1) asset backing + net cash**, **(2) durable monopoly-lite licence economics**, and **(3) income at a screen-validated yield**, not on near-term EPS momentum. GARP model passes are harder to reconcile with declining EPS (3.23c in FY2025 vs 4.79c in FY2024) unless one views current earnings as trough-like and capex (S$669m in FY2025) as front-loaded investment for the next licence cycle.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Regulatory and licence**
Singapore gaming is a **duopoly** under finite licence terms; renewal conditions, tax rates, and responsible-gaming/regulatory compliance costs are material and not visible in available filings. Any licence renewal capex or concession terms could re-rate the equity independently of P/B.

**Earnings cyclicality and recovery uncertainty**
FY2025 and 1H 2025 show clear earnings pressure. Without primary filings, drivers (VIP vs mass hold, visitation, cost inflation, competitive response from Marina Bay Sands) cannot be verified — a **research red flag** in itself.

**Dividend sustainability**
High screen yield is attractive, but FY2025 FCF/dividend mismatch and >100% payout on reported earnings warrant caution; parent Genting’s influence (~53% ownership per news) may support dividends, but minority holders bear dilution/governance risk.

**Governance and control**
Public-company block (~53%) and individual investors (~37%) per Simply Wall St coverage (Dec 2025) imply **controlled-company dynamics** — capital allocation, related-party dealings and minority treatment cannot be assessed without annual-report disclosure.

**Capex and execution**
Elevated capex (S$669m FY2025) for RWS revamp/expansion (news: casino revamp plans at RWS, ad-hoc-news.de, 16 May 2026) carries **execution and return-on-investment risk**; benefits may lag spend, pressuring ROE further near term.

**Competitive and macro**
Regional gaming/hospitality recovery is uneven; Singapore tourism volumes, FX (visitor mix), and rival product upgrades at Marina Bay Sands are exogenous pressures. Macro context file was **empty** — no additional regime markers applied.

**Source / disclosure gap**
Absence of annual/interim filing bodies prevents review of going-concern statements, litigation contingencies, related-party transactions, segment performance, and licence expiry dates — a material limitation for a regulated gaming issuer.

**Accounts receivable presentation**
Yahoo shows a large allowance for doubtful receivables (S$372m against gross receivables S$581m) — unusual magnitude for a casino operator and may reflect classification nuances; **cannot be reconciled** without primary notes.
