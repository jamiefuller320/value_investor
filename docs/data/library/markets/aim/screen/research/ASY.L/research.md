## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Andrews Sykes Group plc is an AIM-listed specialist equipment hire business (pumps, heating, cooling and related services) that screens as a buy on a blend of cheapness, quality, dividend and GARP factors. The quantitative hook is modest headline valuation (P/E ~13, FCF yield ~6.4%, dividend yield ~4.6%) against high reported returns (ROE ~36%) and earnings growth (~9.5%), with nine of 22 models passing including FCF Yield, Quality Value and Financial Health. Primary regulatory filings are absent from the research corpus—no annual or interim RNS bodies were retrieved—so this review relies on Yahoo-sourced financials for all numeric trends. The central debate is whether sustained cash conversion and shareholder returns offset a flat revenue profile since 2022 peak and the governance/liquidity constraints typical of a thinly covered AIM name.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The screen flags ASY.L as a buy with composite score 55% (sector-relative 57%), passing five factor families: cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP and risk. Nine models clear the bar—FCF Yield, Neff PEGY (PEGY 0.03), Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Dreman Contrarian and Financial Health—on metrics including P/E 13.1, P/B 4.4, yield 4.6%, ROE 36.3%, FCF yield 6.4% and growth 9.5%. Data quality is high (19/20 metrics present).

For a value investor, the appeal is a business that appears to compound equity through earnings rather than top-line expansion: operating profit and EBITDA have been broadly stable-to-rising while revenue has drifted below its 2022 high. Gross margins remain elevated (~64% in FY2025 per Yahoo), suggesting pricing power and asset utilisation in a niche hire fleet. Free cash flow has comfortably covered ordinary dividends in recent years (FY2024–FY2025 dividends ~£10.8m vs FCF ~£15m), and the balance sheet shows net cash after lease liabilities (~£28.4m cash vs ~£15.2m capital-lease debt, Yahoo FY2025). The 2023 exceptional dividend (~£35.7m, Yahoo) indicates a management posture favouring capital return when surplus cash builds—aligned with the dividend-family screen pass, though not repeatable every year.

The screen’s “economic moat” and “quality” passes are consistent with a capital-intensive rental model where fleet, depot network and emergency-response reputation create switching costs; however, without annual-report narrative we cannot verify moat claims from primary filings. Signal conviction is zero (new, one week at buy)—this memo is an overlay, not a replacement for the screen.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Filing and disclosure gap.** Absence of annual/interim RNS bodies means we cannot cite going-concern language, covenant terms, contingent liabilities, related-party transactions or audit emphasis of matter from primary sources. This is the largest research limitation.

**Corrupted RNS index.** The fetched RNS catalogue appears to contain third-party announcements; treat indexed “PDMR”, “AGM Online Access” and similar 17 July 2026 items as **unverified** for ASY until matched to company-specific releases.

**Cyclical hire exposure.** Revenue down ~8% from FY2022 peak (Yahoo) despite stable margins suggests volume or utilisation pressure—consistent with construction/industrial/end-market cyclicality not captured in static quality screens.

**Capital intensity and leases.** Rising CapEx and ~£15.2m lease liabilities (Yahoo FY2025) tie earnings to fleet maintenance and IFRS 16 fixed charges; interest expense ~£1.0m annually.

**Concentrated / illiquid AIM equity.** Thin news flow beyond dividend listicles; limited analyst coverage increases bid–offer and event-risk around earnings. Macro context file is empty (`macro snapshot missing`)—no additional regime colour.

**Pension.** Defined-benefit obligation ~£1.5m (Yahoo FY2025)—small but warrants monitoring in full accounts.

**Governance.** Without annual report, board independence, remuneration and control structure cannot be reviewed; AIM names carry elevated governance diligence requirements.

**Dividend sustainability.** Screen passes Dividend Growth, but FY2023’s outsized payout shows policy can shift; ordinary cover is adequate on Yahoo FCF but not generous if earnings soften.
