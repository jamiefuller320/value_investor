## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Frontier Developments is a Cambridge-based video-game developer trading on AIM, with a recovery narrative anchored by licensed franchises (notably Jurassic World Evolution) and owned IP (Planet Coaster, Elite Dangerous). The quantitative screen rates the stock a buy: 10 of 22 models pass across cheapness, quality, GARP and risk, with P/E 8.3, P/B 1.6, ROE 22.7% and modest leverage (debt/equity ~20%). The valuation hook is a sharp FY2025 profit turnaround after two loss-making years, paired with rising cash and positive free cash flow. The central debate is whether FY2025–FY26 momentum reflects durable operational improvement under a capital-light “CMS” (Create, Manage, Survive) strategy, or a cyclical peak in hit-driven licensing revenue that screens overstate because trailing multiples capture a one-off earnings step-up.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, FDEV offers a classic post-turnaround setup: the market appears to price normalised earnings and balance-sheet repair at single-digit multiples after a prolonged downturn. The screen’s composite score of 69% (sector-relative 69%) is supported by Graham Enterprising, Earnings Yield, FCF Yield, Lynch PEG, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Magic Formula, Composite Value and Financial Health passes — a unusually broad sweep across all four factor families (cheapness, quality, GARP, risk). Business quality rests on a portfolio of recurring simulation/management titles with strong community engagement, plus high-margin licensed work when execution lands (Jurassic World). Yahoo-sourced FY2025 data show net income of £16.4m on revenue of £90.6m, cash of £42.5m and FCF of £10.8m — metrics consistent with the screen’s quality and financial-health signals. The May 2026 RNS trading-update headline (“FY26 Revenue & Profit Upgraded”) and subsequent “CMS STRATEGY DELIVERS RECORD FINANCIAL RESULTS” announcement (filings index only; no body text) suggest management believes the turnaround has further runway. Offsetting this, screen conviction is only 31% (new signal, one week, improving), signalling that the quantitative case is fresh and not yet time-tested.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclicality and hit risk:** Revenue remains ~21% below the FY2022 peak; recovery is partly tied to a major licensed launch (Jurassic World Evolution 3). The simulation-game segment is notoriously lumpy — underperformance on the next title or DLC cycle could rapidly reverse FY2025 margin gains.

**Capital allocation and intangibles:** Heavy capitalised development spend (£48.5m intangibles on balance sheet) creates impairment risk if titles underperform. Prior-year commentary in third-party articles (e.g. “May Have Issues Allocating Its Capital”, Jan 2026) reflects ongoing investor scepticism on ROIC — screen “Economic Moat” pass notwithstanding.

**Governance / insider activity:** Multiple EBT share purchases (Jun 2026) and director option exercises (Jun 2026) are routine, but Simply Wall St flagged insider selling (7 Apr 2026) — a governance yellow flag absent full PDMR disclosure in filing bodies.

**Leverage and covenants:** Reported debt is lease-heavy rather than covenant-rich bank debt; without annual-report text, covenant or going-concern language cannot be assessed. Pension risk is not flagged in available sources.

**Screen limitations:** Conviction score 31% and “new” stability label indicate the buy signal has not persisted; timing data are insufficient. Trailing P/E of 8.3 may compress if FY2026 upgrade proves peak earnings rather than a new baseline.

**Regulatory / competitive:** Licensed IP (Universal/Jurassic, Games Workshop/Warhammer) depends on partner renewal terms not visible in sources. Competition from larger studios and platform-holder first-party titles is structurally intense.
