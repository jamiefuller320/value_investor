## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

dotdigital Group Plc is an AIM-listed marketing-automation SaaS vendor that the quantitative screen rates as a **buy**, driven by cheapness (P/E 15.4, P/B 1.3), a 2.6% dividend yield, positive free cash flow (~£10.6m), and minimal leverage (debt/equity ~1%). Revenue has compounded at roughly 12% annually (FY2022–FY2024, Yahoo), but reported earnings softened in FY2024 after restructuring and acquisition-related charges. The valuation hook is a net-cash balance sheet (~£42m cash vs ~£3m lease debt, FY2024 Yahoo) trading below book on a business generating mid-teens gross margins and consistent operating cash flow. The key debate is whether recent M&A (including a reported ~$60m Shopify-tool acquisition in March 2026) and AI/product investment will re-accelerate earnings, or whether goodwill/intangible inflation and competitive pressure in email/CRM SaaS justify the market’s scepticism—reflected in headlines such as Investors’ Chronicle calling the stock “undeserving” of its valuation.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, DOTD offers a rare combination on AIM: profitable SaaS economics, a dividend, net cash, and a sub-16x P/E—passing five of 22 screen models across all four metric families (cheapness, dividend, GARP, risk). The screen’s Graham Enterprising, FCF Yield, Neff PEGY, Dividend Growth, and Financial Health passes align with observable fundamentals: revenue grew from £62.8m (FY2022) to £79.0m (FY2024), gross margin remained ~79%, and operating cash flow held above £21m in each of the last three reported years (Yahoo). Free cash flow of £11.2m in FY2024, after £18.9m acquisition spend, supports the FCF Yield pass and underpins dividend continuity (~£3.1m paid in FY2024).

Business quality is reasonable for a small-cap software name: recurring SaaS revenue, expanding geographic footprint, and product extensions (loyalty, AI capabilities per July 2026 RNS headline). Management has returned capital via dividends and, more recently, share buybacks (RNS entries July 2026: “Transaction in Own Shares”, “Buy Back Extension”). The GARP element is present in mid-teens revenue growth, though normalised earnings growth lagged in FY2024. At 1.3x book and 8.8% ROE, the market prices in execution risk rather than distress—creating a value entry if integration succeeds and margins stabilise post-acquisition.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Data limitation:** Without annual report or interim RNS body text, going-concern language, covenant terms, contingent liabilities, and pension obligations **cannot be assessed** from primary filings. The following risks are inferred from Yahoo data and news context only.

1. **Acquisition integration:** FY2024 goodwill/intangibles nearly doubled; the reported ~$60m Shopify-tool deal (businesscloud.co.uk, 4 March 2026) adds further execution risk. Impairment or integration slippage would pressure EPS and ROE (already modest at 8.8%).

2. **Earnings quality:** Operating income flat-to-down despite revenue growth; rising amortisation and SG&A (£9.5m in FY2024) compress margins. FY2024 net income decline warrants monitoring in the next full-year results.

3. **Competitive/cyclical:** Marketing-automation SaaS faces intense competition (Klaviyo, Mailchimp/Intuit, Salesforce, etc.) and customer budget sensitivity in downturns— not captured by static screens.

4. **Intangible-heavy balance sheet:** 63% of equity in goodwill/intangibles limits tangible asset backing; a failed acquisition could trigger write-downs.

5. **AIM/small-cap factors:** Lower liquidity, less analyst coverage, and reliance on management guidance. News coverage is thin on operational detail beyond listicles and broker commentary.

6. **Capital allocation tension:** Simultaneous acquisition spend, dividends, and buybacks may constrain balance-sheet flexibility if growth disappoints.

7. **Governance/insider activity:** Multiple PDMR and holding notifications (July 2026 RNS) are routine; no adverse signals evident, but no deep governance review possible without annual report.
