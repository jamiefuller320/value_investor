## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
IG Design Group plc is a UK-listed AIM consumer cyclical specialising in gift, celebration, and creative products (brands including Paperchase, Tom Smith, and international sourcing operations). The quantitative screen rates the stock a buy on cheapness, dividend, and balance-sheet risk metrics (P/E 13.9, P/B 0.79, current ratio 3.03, dividend restored), but primary RNS filing bodies are unavailable in the source library, so this memo relies on Yahoo financials as a fallback. The investment case rests on asset-backed discount: the shares trade below book value with roughly US$105m cash against US$96m gross debt, plus a capital-return programme (dividends, buybacks, and the US$19.5m Zenit stake sale). The central debate is whether FY2025’s US$77m statutory loss and multi-year revenue decline reflect cyclical trough and restructuring charges, or structural impairment of returns—ROE is just 3.4% and third-party commentary has questioned earnings quality.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, IGR.L screens attractively on classic deep-value and financial-health criteria. Six of 22 models pass, spanning Graham Defensive, Schloss Low P/B, Deep Value, FCF Yield, Dividend Growth, and Financial Health, with a composite score of 62% (sector-relative 63%) and high data quality (19/20 metrics). The cheapness cluster is coherent: P/B below 1.0 implies the market capitalises tangible equity at a discount; P/E below 15 suggests the market is not pricing a recovery premium; and the current ratio above 3.0 indicates short-term liquidity headroom—consistent with US$157.8m working capital (Yahoo, FY2025 balance sheet).

The screen’s FCF Yield pass aligns with a business that generated positive free cash flow in FY2023 (US$32.8m) and FY2024 (US$53.1m) before a modest FY2025 outflow (US$2.3m), and that held US$105.4m cash at year-end. Management signalling—dividend reinstatement, a share buyback programme (RNS headlines, June 2026), and monetisation of a 13.6% Zenit interest for US$19.5m cash (May 2026)—supports a capital-allocation-led value realisation path rather than pure earnings momentum.

Business quality is mixed. IG Design operates in seasonal, retailer-dependent categories where post-pandemic normalisation and category disruption (evidenced by US arts-and-crafts retail bankruptcies in the news flow) create cyclical headwinds. However, the balance sheet provides a tangible floor: common equity of US$203.2m against a P/B of 0.79 implies a market value materially below reported net assets, and net cash before leases is roughly neutral (cash US$105.4m vs total debt US$96.5m). For a patient value investor, the screen identifies a cash-rich, below-book name where shareholder returns and asset sales may bridge the gap until operating performance stabilises—provided FY2025 impairments (goodwill fell from US$59.2m to US$17.7m) mark a clean-up rather than ongoing erosion.

Simply Wall St (9 July 2026) and Kalkine Media (10 July 2026) flagged concerns beyond headline earnings—consistent with FY2025’s large non-operating tax and special-charge distortions. Returns-on-capital commentary (Yahoo Finance, September 2025) remains a structural concern.

Cyclical and competitive: Revenue has declined four consecutive years. Seasonal gifting and arts/crafts retail exposure creates demand volatility; sector news (e.g. Beverly’s Fabric & Crafts Chapter 11, December 2025) underscores retail stress, though not directly tied to IG Design.

Balance sheet and off-balance items: Capital lease obligations (US$56.5m) and pension/post-retirement liabilities (current + non-current provisions visible on the balance sheet) add fixed commitments not fully captured by simple net-debt metrics. Goodwill impairment in FY2025 may indicate prior overpayment for acquisitions or brand deterioration.

Governance and management: Appointment of a CEO Designate (RNS headline, 16 April 2026) signals leadership transition during a turnaround—execution risk. RNS headlines on 17 July 2026 reference a “Further Statement re Possible Offer” and “Final Announcement Released”; without body text, the nature and status of any approach cannot be assessed—potential M&A overhang or uncertainty.

Filing transparency: Absence of annual report and interim body extracts prevents review of going-concern language, banking covenants, contingent liabilities, or audit emphasis-of-matter paragraphs— a material research gap for an AIM name.

Historical shareholder experience: News noted an ~87% five-year share-price loss (October 2025), reminding that cheapness can persist.
