# Stanley Black & Decker, Inc. (SWK) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T04:08:45.792771+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Stanley Black & Decker (SWK) screens as a buy on cash-flow yield (6.0%), dividend (3.8%), and balance-sheet metrics, against a franchise portfolio led by DEWALT, CRAFTSMAN, STANLEY, and BLACK+DECKER. The equity trades at a depressed multiple of normalised earnings after a multi-year deleveraging and portfolio-simplification cycle: the Global Cost Reduction Programme delivered roughly $2.1 billion of pre-tax run-rate savings by end-2025 (per FY2025 10-K), and a pending $1.8 billion CAM divestiture is earmarked for debt reduction. Valuation appeal rests on FCF generation and Dividend King status rather than growth; headline P/E (37.0x) and ROE (4.2%) still reflect a cyclical trough and restructuring charges. The central debate is whether margin recovery and deleveraging can close the gap between screen cheapness and still-thin reported profitability in a soft Tools & Outdoor demand environment.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, SWK offers a classic “quality franchise at a cyclical discount” setup. The quantitative screen passes four factor families—cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk—with six of 22 models triggered (FCF Yield, Neff PEGY, High Dividend Yield, Magic Formula, Composite Value, Financial Health) and a composite score of 76% (sector-relative 68%). That profile fits a business generating ~$688 million of free cash flow (Yahoo fallback for FY2025; filing bodies lack a full cash-flow statement) on ~$15.1 billion of revenue (FY2025 10-K), supporting a 6.0% FCF yield at current prices.

Business quality remains intact despite operational disruption. Tools & Outdoor generated $13.2 billion (87% of revenue) in FY2025; Engineered Fastening contributed $2.0 billion (13%) (FY2025 10-K). Iconic brands, scale with Home Depot (~15% of sales) and Lowe’s (~12%), and completed cost restructuring provide a durable cash-return engine. Net earnings rebounded from a FY2023 loss (Yahoo: -$310.5 million) to $294.3 million in FY2024 and $401.9 million in FY2025 (Yahoo fallback; consistent with the earnings recovery visible in interim filings). The screen’s negative growth metric (-34.8%) reflects base effects from the trough rather than structural decline—nine-month continuing EPS rose to $1.61 in FY2025 vs $0.61 prior year (Q3 2025 10-Q).

Capital allocation reinforces the case: quarterly dividend raised to $0.83 (25 June 2026 news), new share repurchase authorisation (April 2026), and explicit intent to apply CAM sale proceeds to debt (FY2025 10-K). At 1.6x P/B and sub-4% ROE, the market prices SWK as a leveraged turnaround, not a compounder—exactly where multi-factor value screens tend to find mispricing.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
Leverage and covenants: Despite net debt reduction from ~$6.7 billion (FY2023, Yahoo) toward ~$5.6 billion, SWK remains materially levered for an industrial tools business. Credit agreements require maintenance of an interest coverage ratio (Adjusted EBITDA to Adjusted Net Interest Expense) of not less than 3.50:1.00 (2.50:1.00 for certain periods through the second fiscal quarter of 2026) per Q1 2026 10-Q. Rising short-term borrowings in Q1 2026 warrant monitoring. Interest expense (~$516 million FY2025, Yahoo) consumes a large share of operating profit.

Cyclical and customer concentration: Tools & Outdoor demand remains soft; news coverage flags stalled growth and EPS pressure (Simply Wall St, 12 July 2026). Home Depot and Lowe’s together represent ~27% of consolidated sales (FY2025 10-K), creating pricing and shelf-space risk, compounded by private-label competition.

Earnings quality: Reported margins remain thin (Simply Wall St cited ~2.4% margins, 1 May 2026). Nine-month FY2025 impairments ($169 million) and ongoing restructuring ($52 million YTD) distort GAAP earnings. Q1 2026 earnings declined YoY despite higher sales.

Regulatory and environmental: Filing language explicitly warns that tariffs, import/export controls, and trade restrictions “have had, and may continue to have, a material impact” (FY2025 10-K). Environmental liabilities ($259 million reserved; up to ~$396 million reasonably possible) include Superfund and legacy site remediation (Centredale, Lower Passaic River references in filings).

Pension and intangibles: Non-current pension obligations ~$330 million (Yahoo FY2025). Negative tangible equity limits balance-sheet flexibility if goodwill (~$7.3 billion) were further impaired—trade-name write-downs already occurred in 2025.

Portfolio execution: The $1.8 billion CAM divestiture (announced December 2025, FY2025 10-K) is pending; failure to close or deploy proceeds efficiently would delay deleveraging.

Governance: No material governance red flags in available filings; standard large-cap SOX 404(b) attestation confirmed (FY2025 10-K).
