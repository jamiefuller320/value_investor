## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Xylem is a global water-technology platform (~$9bn revenue, four segments, ~150 countries) whose investment case rests on durable infrastructure demand, a growing services/recurring mix, and post-Evoqua scale. The quantitative screen flags a buy on growth-adjusted value (Neff PEGY 0.20), dividend growth, composite value, and financial health, despite a headline P/E of ~31x and modest ROE (~8.7%). Filings show steady organic revenue and margin progression through 2025, with Q1 2026 continuing that trend and a large share repurchase ($563m in the quarter). The central debate is whether structural water/AI-industrial tailwinds justify a premium multiple, or whether goodwill-heavy balance sheet, ongoing restructuring, and weak tangible book cap upside. Coverage is valuation-heavy rather than event-driven.

---

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, XYL offers a **quality compounder at a relative discount to its growth profile**, which is what the screen is capturing. Four model families pass—cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk—on a composite score of 66% (sector-relative 61%), with PEGY 0.20 and estimated earnings growth of 14.5% offsetting a P/E of 30.6x and P/B of 2.6x. The 1.4% yield and Dividend Growth pass add a shareholder-return floor; Financial Health pass aligns with net debt of ~$463m (Yahoo, FY2025) against ~$910m free cash flow.

Business quality supports the signal. The FY2025 10-K describes a leading global water-technology franchise with differentiated brands, a large installed base driving parts/service revenue, and four segments—Water Infrastructure ($2.6bn), Applied Water ($1.8bn), Measurement & Control Solutions ($2.1bn), and Water Solutions & Services ($2.5bn)—addressing scarcity, resilience, quality, and affordability. The May 2023 Evoqua combination roughly doubled scale; revenue has grown from $7.4bn (FY2023) to $9.0bn (FY2025, 10-K narrative), with operating income and EPS compounding accordingly. Services revenue is rising as a share of the mix (Q1 2026: $368m of $2,125m total revenue, ~17% in the quarter; WSS segment is 41% services at the annual level per the 10-K).

The screen’s buy rating is **new and low-conviction (30%, one week, improving)**, so this memo is an early overlay, not a substitute for the quantitative signal. Timing data are insufficient. Deep research broadly confirms the GARP/dividend case but flags goodwill concentration and headline valuation as offsets.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Goodwill and acquisition risk.** Goodwill of ~$8.3bn (~47% of total assets at year-end 2025) creates impairment sensitivity. FY2024 and FY2025 10-K risk factors explicitly cite "risk of future impairments to goodwill and other intangible assets." Tangible book of $876m means equity is largely accounting goodwill—typical post-Evoqua but a red flag for deep-value purists.

**Integration and restructuring.** Multiple restructuring plans (2015–2024 vintages referenced in XBRL) remain active; 9M 2025 restructuring charges of $70m exceeded the prior year. FY2024 10-K risk factors cite uncertainty around Evoqua revenue synergies and simplification/realignment costs. Ongoing portfolio shaping (Magneto divestiture, international metering held-for-sale, Pac Machine and Vacom acquisitions in 2025) adds execution risk.

**Pension and post-retirement.** Accrued post-retirement obligations of $308–317m (non-current) plus current pension-related accruals of ~$371m appear on interim balance sheets. FY2024 10-K risk factors flag "regulatory and financial market risks related to our pension and other defined benefit plans."

**Cyclical and macro exposure.** Risk factors across annual filings cite municipal/utility budget pressure, tariffs, inflation, supply-chain disruption (semiconductors), FX volatility, and geopolitical conflict. Water is partly defensive, but capex-heavy utility and industrial end markets are cyclical.

**Leverage and capital allocation.** Net debt remains manageable (~$463m at FY2025 year-end per Yahoo), but Q1 2026 repurchases of $563m against $808m cash at quarter-end suggest aggressive capital return; covenant detail is not in available body extracts (Note 18 contingencies referenced but not extracted).

**Governance / legal.** Item 3 Legal Proceedings is indexed in all filings but **body text is not present** in available extracts—cannot assess material litigation. No going-concern language found. 10-K confirms SOX 404(b) internal-control attestation (unqualified, per filing header).

**Valuation.** Screen P/E 30.6x and ROE 8.7% imply the market prices quality/growth; a de-rating on missed guidance or synergy shortfall is plausible. News flow includes 52-week lows near $114–116 (Apr–May 2025), indicating recent volatility.
