# International Flavors & Fragrances Inc. (IFF) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T04:17:10.603530+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
International Flavors & Fragrances is a global ingredients, taste, scent, and biosciences supplier undergoing a major portfolio reset: divesting lower-return businesses (Pharma Solutions, Food Ingredients, SCL) while refocusing on higher-margin core franchises. The quantitative screen flags IFF as a buy on cheapness, dividend, and risk metrics (composite score 78%, FCF yield ~19%, P/B 1.4x, dividend yield 2.1%), but reported earnings remain distorted by goodwill impairments, divestiture gains/losses, and debt-extinguishment items. The valuation hook is a simplified, less-leveraged IFF trading below book with improving underlying cash generation; the central debate is whether post-divestiture earnings power justifies the screen’s buy signal or whether revenue softness and intangible-heavy balance sheet cap upside.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, IFF offers a classic “complexity discount” on a franchise business. The screen passes four models — FCF Yield, Dividend Growth, Composite Value, and Financial Health — across three of four factor families (cheapness, dividend, risk), with sector-relative composite at 75% and high data quality (19/20 metrics). At P/E 23.8x and P/B 1.4x with 2.1% yield and ROE 6.1%, the market prices in years of integration missteps from the DuPont Nutrition & Biosciences merger and subsequent goodwill write-downs, not the steadier cash economics of Taste, Scent, and Health & Biosciences.

Operationally, interim filings show the turnaround gaining traction. Q1 2026 net sales were $2.74bn (−3.6% YoY), but operating profit recovered to $273m versus a $903m operating loss in Q1 2025 driven by a $1.15bn goodwill impairment. Adjusted Operating EBITDA for Q1 2026 was $568m (10-Q, filed May 2026). Net income attributable to shareholders was $169m ($0.66 EPS) versus a $1.02bn loss a year earlier. Nine-month 2025 revenue of $8.30bn trailed prior-year $8.71bn, yet the balance sheet improved materially: total debt fell from $9.05bn at end-2024 to roughly $5.82bn by March 2026 (short-term plus long-term per Q1 2026 10-Q), funded by divestiture proceeds.

Business quality remains anchored in R&D-intensive, customer-embedded formulations with global reach. Management is returning capital via a $0.40 quarterly dividend and a new $500m share repurchase programme (authorised August 2025, per Q3 2025 10-Q). Innovation pipeline activity (e.g. SENSORA pro-fragrance technology, per news flow) supports the higher-margin Scent franchise. The screen’s buy signal aligns with a de-risked capital structure and normalising cash flows, even if GAAP earnings continue to look noisy.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Transformation and earnings quality.** Portfolio reshaping creates lumpy reported results: Q1 2025 goodwill impairment of $1,153m, Q2 2025 debt-extinguishment gain of $488m, held-for-sale losses of $108–314m across 2024–2025, and divestiture-related charges. Normalised metrics are essential; GAAP P/E is not a clean measure.

**Intangibles and further impairment risk.** Goodwill of $8.22bn (March 2026 10-Q) plus other intangibles of $5.87bn represent over half of total assets. FY2023–2025 impairments demonstrate sensitivity to segment performance. No additional goodwill impairment was recorded in Q1 2026, but this remains a live risk.

**Revenue trajectory.** Net sales declined in FY2025 (−5% YoY per Yahoo fallback) and nine-month 2025 sales fell 4.7% YoY (Q3 2025 10-Q). Volume/mix softness in Food Ingredients and post-divestiture base effects require monitoring.

**Leverage and covenants.** Debt has fallen sharply, but total debt of ~$5.8bn against $562m cash still implies meaningful leverage. Filing bodies reference a revolving credit facility (Citibank) and term loans, but covenant detail was not available in the truncated extracts. No going-concern or substantial-doubt language was found in available filing bodies.

**Pension and retirement.** Retirement liabilities of $182–186m (interim balance sheets) and ongoing pension contributions ($5–20m quarterly) represent a manageable but persistent obligation.

**Contingencies.** Balance sheets reference “Commitments and Contingencies (Note 17)” without detail in available extracts. Customer concentration risk is disclosed qualitatively (trade receivable factoring with certain customers per Q1 2026 notes), but percentage thresholds were not extractable.

**Governance and complexity.** Segment reorganization effective January 2025 (Taste, Food Ingredients, Health & Biosciences, Scent) adds reporting complexity. Accumulated deficit and negative tangible equity limit balance-sheet flexibility.

**Cyclical and macro.** Consumer-staples exposure provides defensiveness, but geopolitical volatility (e.g. July 2026 Middle East-related selloff cited in news) and potential tariff impacts on global supply chains remain headwinds not fully captured by value screens.
