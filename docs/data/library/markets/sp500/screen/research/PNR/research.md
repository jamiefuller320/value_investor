## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Pentair is a UK-tax-resident, NYSE-listed water solutions group spanning Flow, Water Solutions, and Pool, with a long record of margin expansion and cash generation through FY2025. The quantitative screen flags attractive valuation (P/E 16.6, P/B 2.8, ROE 17.6%, D/E 55%) alongside quality, dividend, GARP, and risk metrics. The central debate is whether trailing fundamentals still hold after management’s July 2026 guidance cut, driven by Pool channel destocking and weaker demand, which triggered a ~15% share fall and multiple analyst downgrades. For a value investor, the case rests on a franchise Pool business and transformation-led margins, offset by cyclical Pool exposure, 18% customer concentration, and heavy goodwill.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The screen’s Strong Buy rests on five passed factor families—cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk—with nine models clearing thresholds including Graham Enterprising, FCF Yield, Neff PEGY, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Composite Value, and Financial Health. At P/E 16.6 and P/B 2.8, Pentair trades below typical industrial quality multiples despite ROE of 17.6% and a 1.6% yield with dividend-growth pedigree (five-year growth ~5.9% per news coverage).

Filings support the quality narrative. FY2025 consolidated net sales rose to **$4,176.0m** from **$4,082.8m** in FY2024 (FY2023: **$4,104.5m**), per the FY2025 10-K body. Adjusted operating income expanded to **$1,053.5m** from **$959.2m** (FY2023: **$855.1m**). Pool segment income reached **$527.1m** on **$1,558.8m** sales—Pentair’s highest-margin engine—while Flow rebounded (**$225.0m** income on **$1,001.2m** sales) aided by the **$292.1m** Hydra-Stop acquisition (September 2025). Transformation Programme and 80/20 initiatives are embedded in strategy.

The screen likely captures this through FCF yield (~$574m per screen; **$746m** FCF in FY2025 per Yahoo fallback), moderate leverage (D/E 55%), and consistent earnings. Pentair fits a value investor seeking a cash-generative industrial compounder at a reasonable multiple—provided the Pool downturn proves transitory rather than structural.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Pool cyclicality and channel inventory (critical).** Pool is ~37% of sales and the highest-margin segment. News reports (July 2026) cite management cutting FY2026 outlook to **-4% to -7%** sales growth (from prior +2% to +4%) on Pool destocking, weak demand, and higher rates—inconsistent with the screen’s trailing earnings base. This is the primary near-term red flag and is **not yet reflected in filed interim accounts**.

**Customer concentration.** One Pool customer was **~18%** of consolidated net sales in FY2025 (10-K body). Loss or inventory correction at this distributor would disproportionately affect results—as appears to be occurring.

**Goodwill and acquisition risk.** Goodwill/intangibles ~**67%** of assets (FY2024 10-K risk factors). FY2025 included **$99.7m** unusual charges (Yahoo) and **$49.1m** asset impairment (cash flow statement). Further Pool weakness could pressure impairment testing.

**Water Solutions structural decline.** Three-year sales decline ($1,799m → $1,614m under revised segmentation) and a commercial business exit in 2025 suggest portfolio rationalisation with revenue headwinds.

**Inflation, tariffs, and supply chain.** FY2025 10-K risk factors flag commodity inflation, US tariff uncertainty (including USMCA review in 2026), and supply chain disruption. Pentair anticipates pressures continuing into 2026.

**Governance.** Unexpected CFO departure (July 2026 news); interim CFO Bob Fishman appointed. Raises questions during a guidance reset.

**Financial structure.** Net debt ~**$1.5bn** with cash ~**$102m** (Yahoo FY2025). Negative tangible equity. Covenant compliance depends on EBITDA holding up through the Pool downturn—unverified from filings.

**Environmental and legal.** FY2025 10-K references environmental liabilities and litigation (Item 1A/Item 3); specific provisions are in truncated sections not available in body extracts. Penwald captive insurance subsidiary adds contingent liability complexity.

**Seasonality.** Pool, Water Solutions, and residential Flow demand peak April–August; weather and housing sensitivity amplify cyclicality.

**Transformation execution.** 80/20 and restructuring may reduce revenue by exiting low-margin customers (explicit 10-K risk); benefits may lag costs.
