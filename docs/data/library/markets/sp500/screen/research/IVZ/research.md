_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-19T15:58:33.600217+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Invesco Ltd. (IVZ) is a global asset manager whose quantitative screen flags a buy on cheapness, dividend income, and free-cash-flow yield (P/B 1.3, yield 2.9%, FCF yield ~6.6%), despite a negative reported ROE (-1.5%) distorted by FY2025 non-cash charges. Operationally, the franchise is strengthening: ending AUM rose from $1,846bn at FY2024 to $2,170bn at FY2025 (10-K), with Q1 2026 net long-term inflows of $21.8bn and average AUM of $2.2trn (+18% YoY). The valuation hook is a cyclically depressed earnings base and strong adjusted cash generation against a scaled ETF/QQQ platform; the key debate is whether reported GAAP losses and ETF fee competition (notably BlackRock’s Nasdaq-100 filing) cap upside, or whether sustained inflows and the new $1bn buyback authorisation re-rate the stock.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The screen’s buy signal rests on four passing models—FCF Yield, Dividend Growth, Composite Value, and Financial Health—with a 66% composite score and improving one-week trend, versus a sector-relative score of 58%. That profile fits a value investor seeking a cash-generative financial at a modest book multiple with a dividend, rather than a pure quality compounder.

Business quality, on an operating basis, is better than GAAP earnings suggest. FY2024 was a clean year (Yahoo fallback: revenue $6.07bn, net income $775m, diluted EPS $1.18). FY2025 GAAP results were heavily impaired (Yahoo fallback: net loss $282m, diluted EPS -$1.60, asset impairment charge $1.79bn), yet operating cash flow remained robust ($1.53bn per Yahoo; free cash flow $1.44bn). Interim filings show recovery: Q1 2026 operating revenues of $1,745m (+14% YoY), adjusted diluted EPS $0.57 (+30%), and adjusted operating margin 34.5% (Q1 2026 10-Q). Nine-month 2025 net income attributable to Invesco was $460m versus $329m in 9M 2024, with diluted EPS $1.01 versus $0.72 (Q3 2025 10-Q).

The franchise moat is narrow but real in passive/ETF scale: QQQ average AUM reached $399bn in Q1 2026 versus $320bn a year earlier, and ETFs/Index AUM grew 30% in FY2025 to $630bn (FY2025 10-K). Net revenue yield ex-performance fees was stable at ~23bps. For a value investor, the screen correctly identifies a mismatch between depressed accounting earnings and underlying cash economics; the thesis is that normalised earnings power, plus AUM compounding and capital return, closes the gap.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
AUM sensitivity. Filings state revenues and net income would be “adversely affected by any reduction in AUM” from market declines or net outflows; fixed costs mean margins fall faster than AUM.

ETF fee compression and QQQ concentration. QQQ is ~18% of average AUM in Q1 2026. BlackRock’s Nasdaq-100 ETF filing (Investing.com, 6 Apr 2026) pressured the share price; filings flag “competitive pressures… which may force us to reduce the fees we charge.” QQQ’s conversion to an open-end ETF (completed 2 Dec 2025 per Q1 2026 10-Q) is strategically important but execution-sensitive.

Goodwill/intangible overhang. $12.4bn of goodwill and intangibles on a $27bn asset base (Yahoo FY2025); FY2025 $1.79bn impairment (Yahoo). Risk factors explicitly warn of further impairment.

Preferred equity structure. ~$2.5bn preferred stock with ~$205m annual dividends ranks ahead of common; this explains much of the gap between consolidated net income and common EPS.

Consolidated investment products (CIP). CIP assets and liabilities are consolidated onto the balance sheet, adding complexity and fair-value volatility; litigation “could result in a significant decline in AUM” (Q1 2026 10-Q contingencies note).

Regulatory and litigation. Risk factors cite civil litigation, governmental investigations, ERISA/fiduciary exposure, and cybersecurity. Commitments include co-investment obligations callable in future periods (Q3 2025 10-Q Note 10).

Cyclical/macro. Money-market and fixed-income fee sensitivity to rate volatility is flagged in both annual 10-K risk sections. Employee benefit plan liabilities ($1.09bn accrued compensation at 31 Mar 2026) add pension-like exposure.

Governance/jurisdiction. Bermuda domicile; risk factors note Bermuda law differs from US law and subsidiary dividend upstream limits.
