## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Incyte is a profitable, cash-generative biopharma whose commercial base is anchored by JAKAFI/JAKAVI (ruxolitinib), with Opzelura cream and a broadening hematology/oncology portfolio (ICLUSIG, MONJUVI/MINJUVI, PEMAZYRE, ZYNYZ, NIKTIMVO) providing diversification. The quantitative screen flags the name as a buy on cheapness, quality, GARP, and risk metrics (P/E 16.4, ROE 30.8%, PEG 0.20, FCF ~$559m), reflecting a sharp earnings inflection after a distorted FY2024. The valuation hook is a mid-teens earnings multiple on a business that reported FY2025 net income of $1.29bn (Yahoo fallback; annual filing bodies lack Item 8 tables) and is still compounding product revenue at roughly 20% year-on-year in recent quarters. The central debate is whether Jakafi-driven cash flows and pipeline optionality (latarcibart/VGA039 in von Willebrand disease, JAKAFI XR, INCA033989) justify accumulation, or whether patent expiry (ruxolitinib patents mid/late 2028 per FY2025 10-K), EU regulatory friction on Opzelura, and Medicare/IRA pricing risk cap the upside.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value-oriented investor, INCY offers a rare combination in biotech: positive, scaling free cash flow, minimal leverage (~$40m total debt per Yahoo FY2025 balance sheet), and a fortress balance sheet (cash and equivalents $3.46bn at 31 March 2026 per Q1 2026 10-Q), without the binary pipeline dependency typical of pre-revenue names.

The screen passes six models across all four metric families — cheapness, quality, GARP, and risk — including Lynch PEG, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Magic Formula, and Financial Health. Key screen inputs (P/E 16.4, P/B 4.1, ROE 30.8%, FCF $559m, PEG 0.20, earnings growth 83.8%) align with a business that has moved from a loss-making FY2024 (distorted by a one-off R&D charge of $1.14bn in Q2 2024 per the Q2 2024 10-Q) to normalized profitability. The 83.8% growth figure is mechanically elevated by that low base; normalised trend growth is closer to high-teens revenue and strong operating leverage on the product portfolio.

Business quality supports the screen's quality and moat passes. Incyte commercialises its own US specialty portfolio (JAKAFI, Opzelura) and holds global rights across multiple approved haem/onc assets. The FY2025 10-K describes JAKAFI as first-line standard of care in myelofibrosis and notes ruxolitinib patents expiring mid/late 2028. Opzelura has FDA approvals in atopic dermatitis and vitiligo, with European Commission approval for vitiligo (April 2023) and expanded EU labelling for moderate atopic dermatitis (October 2024 per 10-K). CMS granted ruxolitinib phosphate the Small Biotech Exception under the Inflation Reduction Act (FY2024/FY2025 10-K), partially mitigating IRA price-negotiation risk near term.

Interim filings confirm momentum: Q1 2026 total revenues were $1,273m (+21% vs $1,053m in Q1 2025), net income $303m (+92%), and diluted EPS $1.47 vs $0.80. Nine-month FY2025 revenues reached $3,634m (+19% vs prior year), with net income of $987m against a $169m loss in the comparable 2024 period. Product revenue mix is broadening — net sales rose to $1,104m in Q1 2026 from $922m — driven by Jakafi and Opzelura growth (consistent with news reporting Q4 2025 revenue of $1.51bn, +28% YoY).

The name fits a GARP/value framework: profitable, cash-rich, growing, and trading at a discount to many large-cap biotech peers on earnings, with pipeline catalysts (latarcibart Phase 3 initiation, EU Opzelura expansion, NIKTIMVO launch commenced January 2025) providing optionality not fully captured by backward-looking screens.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Product concentration.** The FY2025 10-K summary risk factors state explicitly: "We depend heavily on JAKAFI/JAKAVI (ruxolitinib), and if we are not able to maintain revenues from JAKAFI/JAKAVI or those revenues decrease, our business may be materially harmed." Ruxolitinib composition-of-matter patents expire mid/late 2028. Generic entry post-expiry is the single largest long-dated risk.

**Regulatory and reimbursement.** The 10-K warns that Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement changes "could harm" results. While ruxolitinib received CMS Small Biotech Exception status under the IRA, policy shifts remain a live risk. News coverage (8–10 July 2026, simplywall.st) flagged Opzelura EU setbacks and index exclusion as reframing the bull case — a near-term sentiment headwind even as EMA backing for moderate atopic dermatitis was reported (2 July 2026).

**Competitive pressure.** JAK-inhibitor class warnings (cardiovascular events, thrombosis, secondary malignancies) appear in JAKAFI labelling per the 10-K, and competition across MF, PV, GVHD, and dermatology could erode share. The 10-K also flags that "competition for our products could harm our business and result in a decrease in our revenue."

**Customer concentration.** A limited number of specialty pharmacies and wholesalers represent a significant portion of JAKAFI and other product revenues (FY2025 10-K summary risk factors). Loss of a major distributor would be material.

**Pipeline and R&D execution.** R&D remains elevated at ~$516m per quarter (Q1 2026). Clinical failures (mixed trial results noted in news, 24–30 March 2026, Investing.com/Barclays and William Blair) can impair sentiment and contingent milestone values. Acquisition-related contingent consideration ($110m liability) reflects earn-out exposure.

**One-off earnings quality.** FY2025 profitability benefited from a $242m contract dispute settlement credit (Q2 2025). FY2024 was depressed by a $1.14bn R&D charge (Q2 2024). Investors should normalise earnings when assessing forward multiples.

**Intellectual property litigation.** The 10-K flags that IP arbitration/litigation "could be costly and disrupt our drug discovery and development efforts." No material legal proceeding details are available — Item 3 body text is truncated in the extract.

**Governance / insider activity.** Form 4 and Form 144 filings appear in recent news (15–16 July 2026, Investing.com), indicating ongoing insider transactions; not inherently negative but worth monitoring.

No going-concern, covenant breach, or pension deficit red flags were identified in available filing extracts.
