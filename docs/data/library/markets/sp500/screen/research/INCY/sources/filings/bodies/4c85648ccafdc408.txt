The standard outlines a valuation framework and creates a fair value hierarchy in order to increase the consistency and comparability of fair value measurements and the related disclosures. In determining fair value, we use quoted prices and observable inputs. Observable inputs are inputs that market participants would use in pricing the asset or liability based on market data obtained from sources independent of us. The fair value hierarchy is broken down into three levels based on the source of inputs as follows: Level 1—Valuations based on unadjusted quoted prices in active markets for identical assets or liabilities. Level 2—Valuations based on observable inputs and quoted prices in active markets for similar assets and liabilities. Level 3—Valuations based on inputs that are unobservable and models that are significant to the overall fair value measurement.
