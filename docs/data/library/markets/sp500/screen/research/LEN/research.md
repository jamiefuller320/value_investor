## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Lennar is the second-largest US homebuilder by volume, pursuing an asset-light land strategy via the Millrose spin-off, Rausch Coleman acquisition, and option-controlled homesites (98% at November 2025, per FY2025 10-K). The quantitative screen flags deep value—P/E 13.5, P/B ~1.0, current ratio 8.5, dividend yield 2.3%—but reported earnings are falling sharply as average selling prices compress and gross margins erode in a higher-rate housing market.

The investment case rests on scale, balance-sheet optionality after land separation, and multiples near book value after repeated 52-week lows. The central debate is whether margin and EPS trough are cyclical and mean-reverting, or structural as Lennar trades volume for share in a permanently tighter affordability environment. Filings confirm volume resilience (82,583 deliveries in FY2025) but also document pricing pressure, rising spec inventory, and H1 FY2026 earnings down 46% year-on-year.

---

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The screen’s Strong Buy rests on four passing factor families—cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk—supported by Graham Defensive, Schloss Low P/B, FCF Yield, Magic Formula, and Financial Health models. At P/B 0.96 and P/E 13.5, Lennar trades as a cyclical deep-value name rather than a quality compounder; ROE of 7.4% reflects the down-cycle, not franchise impairment.

Business quality remains high on operational metrics: FY2025 deliveries rose to 82,583 (+3% vs 80,210 in FY2024, per FY2025 10-K), active communities expanded to 1,708, and the land-light pivot (Millrose spin-off February 2025; exchange offer November 2025) reduced owned inventory and capital intensity. Homebuilding generated ~$32bn of ~$34bn consolidated revenue (94%, FY2025 10-K). Integrated financial services (mortgage, title) and multifamily provide ancillary earnings streams.

For a value investor, the screen correctly identifies a franchise trading below tangible book with a dividend, strong liquidity (current ratio 8.51), and aggressive capital return—$1.8bn of share repurchases in FY2025 (Yahoo fallback; filing bodies confirm ongoing repurchases in interim periods). The gap is timing: earnings and margins are in active decline, so the cheapness may be “earnings-normalisation risk” rather than mispricing alone. Volume-led share gains at the expense of ASP ($391k in FY2025 vs $423k in FY2024, per FY2025 10-K) support the land-light, even-flow strategy but defer margin recovery until rates ease or incentives abate.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Cyclical / demand**
- FY2025 and FY2024 10-K bodies state that higher interest rates reduced affordability, requiring price reductions and/or increased incentives—direct margin pressure confirmed in interim filings.
- Backlog fell from $6.6bn to $5.4bn (FY2024 10-K); cancellation rate improved to 14% but remains material.
- Completed unsold spec inventory rose to ~5,000 homes (2.9 per community) at November 2025 vs ~2,900 (2.0) a year earlier—inventory risk if demand softens further.

**Strategic / execution**
- Land-light model depends on Millrose and land-bank partners; Q2 FY2026 10-Q documents ongoing Millrose-related balance-sheet adjustments. Execution risk if option land costs rise or partner capacity tightens.
- Rausch Coleman integration (February 2025, FY2025 10-K) expands geographic footprint into weaker-demand markets (Arkansas, Oklahoma, Kansas/Missouri).

**Financial**
- FY2025 FCF collapsed to $28m (Yahoo fallback) despite $1.8bn of buybacks—capital return exceeded internal generation.
- Total debt rose to $5.9bn in FY2025 (Yahoo) while cash fell; net debt ~$2.1bn. Manageable against $22bn equity but rising in a downturn.
- ROE 7.4% (screen) is well below historical homebuilder-cycle peaks; screen FCF yield may reflect TTM smoothing—the FY2025 filing-year FCF figure is near zero.

**Contingent / regulatory**
- Q2 FY2026 10-Q references "Commitments and contingent liabilities (See Note 10)" but note detail is not in the truncated body.
- Financial Services segment carries representation-and-warranty liabilities (referenced in XBRL metadata; amounts not readable in extracts).
- **Going concern:** no going-concern language found in available filing bodies.
- **Risk Factors (Item 1A):** table of contents present in 10-K index, but risk-factor narrative is **not** included in truncated body extracts—a material research gap.

**Governance / structural**
- Dual-class structure (Class A LEN / Class B LEN.B) gives insiders effective control; not captured by quantitative screens.
- Lennar Other segment holds strategic tech investments (historically Opendoor, Hippo, etc. per FY2024 metadata)—potential mark-to-market volatility.

**Competitive**
- News flow shows analysts preferring D.R. Horton over Lennar on relative execution; Antipodes Global Fund divested citing weak housing recovery (June 2026).
