# The Trade Desk, Inc. (TTD) — First-Pass Research Memo

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Trade Desk is a leading independent demand-side platform (DSP) for programmatic digital advertising, earning fees on client ad spend across CTV, video, display, audio and mobile. The quantitative screen rates TTD a **buy** on cheapness, quality and financial health (FCF yield 6.5%, ROE 16.7%, P/E 21.1), reflecting a sharp de-rating after the share price fell roughly 76% over the past year. Fundamentals remain solid: revenue is still growing and the balance sheet is net-cash with strong operating cash conversion, but growth is decelerating and Q1 2026 net income fell 21% year-on-year despite a 12% revenue increase. The central debate is whether TTD’s independent, buy-side positioning and AI/CTV/retail-media expansion can re-accelerate growth against Google, Amazon and agency-friction headwinds—or whether the market has permanently repriced a former high-multiple compounder.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, TTD offers a rare combination: a structurally advantaged franchise (independent DSP without sell-side conflicts) now screening cheap on cash generation and quality metrics after a sentiment-driven collapse. The screen passes five models—FCF Yield, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Magic Formula and Financial Health—across three of four factor families (cheapness, quality, risk), with composite score 57% versus 57% sector-relative and high data quality (18/20 metrics).

Business quality aligns with the screen. The FY2025 10-K describes TTD as a global ad-tech leader with proprietary AI, omnichannel inventory access and API extensibility, differentiated from vertically integrated rivals such as Google and Amazon that also own media inventory. Revenue is recurring in nature via master services agreements; gross margins are high (Yahoo fallback: 78.6% in FY2025). The company generates substantial free cash flow—$783m in FY2025 per Yahoo, with $681m operating cash flow in the first nine months of 2025 per the Q3 10-Q filing—and carries no drawn debt under its revolving credit facility as of 31 March 2026.

The valuation hook is straightforward: at P/E 21.1 and FCF yield 6.5%, the market prices in a growth slowdown that may already be reflected in the share price, whilst the balance sheet (cash and equivalents of $878m at 31 March 2026, plus $528m short-term investments) and $750m undrawn revolving facility provide downside protection. CEO Jeff Green’s ~$148m insider purchase (March 2026) and ongoing share repurchases ($174m in Q1 2026; $958m over 9M 2025) signal management conviction. For a patient value investor, TTD is a quality compounder at a cyclically depressed multiple—provided growth stabilises above mid-teens.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Competitive intensity.** The FY2025 10-K identifies Google and Amazon as well-resourced competitors with inherent conflicts of interest from owning media inventory. TTD’s independent positioning is a moat, but walled-garden platforms can undercut on price and bundle inventory access.

**Growth deceleration.** Q1 2026 revenue growth of 12% is the slowest quarterly pace in the filing set, and news flow (July 2026) references weak forward outlook. The MD&A warns that future revenue growth depends on retaining clients and gaining share in a “highly competitive advertising market,” including differentiation against competitors offering “artificially low prices.”

**Agency and audit friction.** March 2026 news reports Publicis Groupe cutting ties over audit disputes (stock fell 7–8%), with a subsequent settlement reported in June 2026. The Trade Desk faces earnings test amid “agency tensions” (May 2026). Large agency relationships are concentration risk; the 10-K references customer concentration among holding companies.

**Privacy litigation.** The Q1 2026 10-Q discloses consolidated class-action litigation (*In re The Trade Desk, Inc. Data Privacy Litigation*, N.D. Cal.), with the court denying the company’s motion to dismiss in December 2025. Management considers claims meritless but acknowledges litigation uncertainty. No material accrual recorded.

**Stock-based compensation dilution.** SBC totalled ~$491m in FY2025 (Yahoo)—roughly 17% of revenue and exceeding reported net income. Q1 2026 SBC was $109m. Unrecognised option compensation of ~$154m remains (Q1 2026 10-Q). This is a persistent GAAP earnings drag.

**Aggressive capital return.** FY2025 share repurchases of $1,380m (Yahoo) and 9M 2025 repurchases of $958m (10-Q) have driven an accumulated deficit of $(725)m despite profitable operations. If growth stalls, buyback-heavy capital allocation may destroy value at prior elevated prices (though recent repurchases occur at much lower levels).

**Macro and cyclical exposure.** Forward-looking statements in filings cite macroeconomic uncertainty affecting client ad budgets. Ad-tech is cyclically sensitive to brand and performance marketing spend. No pension obligations identified.

**Credit facility covenants.** The restated $750m revolving facility (April 2026) requires maximum consolidated funded debt/EBITDA of 3.50:1. The company was in compliance as of 31 March 2026 with no outstanding borrowings ($445m available net of letters of credit). Covenant breach is not an immediate concern but constrains leverage capacity.

**Governance.** Dual-class share structure (432.9m Class A and 43.1m Class B outstanding at 31 January 2026 per FY2025 10-K) concentrates voting control with insiders. April 2026 news reports executive departures causing stock weakness.
