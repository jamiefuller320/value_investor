## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Newmont is the world’s largest listed gold producer, offering scale, tier-one assets (Cadia, Penasquito, Boddington, Lihir, Nevada Gold Mines), and a leveraged gold-price optionality that screens cheaply at 11.8× P/E and 2.8× P/B with 25.8% ROE. FY2025 was a step-change year: revenue and earnings roughly doubled versus FY2024 on higher gold prices, Newcrest integration, and portfolio reshaping, with free cash flow of ~$7.3bn funding debt reduction and $2.3bn of buybacks. The valuation hook is a quality cyclical trading below typical peak-multiple territory despite record profitability. The central debate is whether rising 2026 unit costs, asset disposals, and gold-price volatility offset the screen’s cheapness, quality, and cash-return profile.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The quantitative screen flags NEM as a strong buy across five families—cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk—with 15 of 22 models passing, including Graham Enterprising, FCF Yield, Magic Formula, Piotroski F-Score (composite 77%, sector-relative 78%). That breadth is unusual for a Basic Materials name and aligns with underlying business quality: post-Newcrest scale, a simplified core portfolio, low leverage (debt/equity ~16%), and FCF of ~$9.8bn on the screen versus ~$7.3bn in Yahoo FY2025 cash-flow data.

For a value investor, NEM offers cyclical exposure with balance-sheet optionality rather than distressed mining economics. FY2023–FY2024 impairments and integration noise are largely behind a FY2025 inflection (Yahoo fallback: net income $7.1bn vs $3.3bn prior year). Capital returns—buybacks and dividends—convert gold upside into per-share value. The screen’s low P/E and P/B, combined with high ROE and FCF yield, suggest the market is discounting cost inflation, production mix, and gold mean-reversion rather than structural weakness.

Filings metadata (FY2025 10-K body extract) confirms a continuing portfolio optimisation programme—disposals of Cripple Creek, Musselwhite, Porcupine, Éléonore, Akyem, Telfer, and others—toward a tighter, lower-cost asset base centred on Cadia, Lihir, Tanami, Boddington, Penasquito, and Nevada Gold Mines.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Commodity and margin cyclicality.** Gold price drives revenue and margins; recent news notes a 16% three-month share pullback and margin pressure from higher 2026 unit costs (royalties, sustaining capital, inventory, lower production). Screen cheapness partly reflects this cyclicality.

**Cost inflation and production.** News ("Can Newmont Protect Margins Amid Higher Unit Costs in 2026?", 14 Jul 2026) flags rising all-in costs. FY2023–FY2025 impairments ($1.9bn, $78m, $842m per Yahoo) indicate asset-level sensitivity to price and reserve assumptions.

**Integration and portfolio transition.** Newcrest integration remains a narrative theme. Large-scale disposals (Akyem, Éléonore, Porcupine, etc., per filing metadata) simplify the portfolio but reduce production base and introduce execution risk on remaining growth projects (Ahafo North, Tanami Expansion 2).

**Operational disruptions.** Cadia resumed after a seismic event (16 Jul 2026 news)—a reminder of geotechnical and safety risk at underground assets.

**Regulatory and environmental.** Filing metadata references environmental remediation across jurisdictions (including Yanacocha, Peru). Mining faces reclamation liabilities, community relations, and tax/regulatory changes in Ghana, Argentina, Mexico, and elsewhere— not fully captured by quantitative screens.

**Pension and employee benefits.** Defined-benefit pension and post-retirement plans appear in FY2025 10-K metadata; obligations are a long-tail liability not visible in simple D/E metrics.

**Governance / insider activity.** EVP Peter Toth sold ~$277k of stock (6 Jul 2026 news)—immaterial relative to market cap but worth monitoring alongside buybacks.

**Filing data gap.** Absence of readable 10-K/10-Q prose and 8-K bodies limits direct verification of contingencies, litigation, and covenant headroom; risk assessment relies partly on metadata tags and secondary sources.

**Screen limitations.** Dividend yield (1.1%) is modest for a “dividend family” pass; timing data insufficient (RSI/SMA200 null). Conviction score 55% is “building,” not mature.
