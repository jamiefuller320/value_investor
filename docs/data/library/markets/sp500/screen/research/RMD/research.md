# ResMed Inc. (RMD) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T04:05:59.762811+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
ResMed is the global leader in cloud-connected sleep-apnoea and respiratory-care devices, with a growing digital-health ecosystem (AirView, myAir) and residential-care software. The quantitative screen rates the name a buy on cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk factors (7/22 models; P/E 19.5, ROE 25.2%, PEGY 0.15). After a ~23% share-price fall, the stock screens as a high-quality compounder at a reasonable multiple, but the market debate centres on whether GLP-1 weight-loss drugs, Philips re-entry, and US reimbursement pressure will cap device growth and margins. Deep research confirms strong fundamentals and accelerating interim trends, partially offset by strategic uncertainty around the MatrixCare divestiture and repeated sell-side downgrades.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, ResMed offers a rare combination: a wide-moat medical-device franchise (dominant CPAP/APAP share, 30m+ cloud-connected patients per FY2025 10-K business description), durable double-digit revenue growth, expanding margins, and capital-light cash generation — yet it trades at a mid-teens P/E versus history and peers.

The screen’s buy signal is well supported by business quality:
- **Quality/moat:** Passes Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Quality Value, and Financial Health; ROE 25.2%, high data quality (19/20 metrics).
- **Growth at reasonable price:** Neff PEGY 0.15 with ~10.5% earnings growth; GARP family passed.
- **Shareholder returns:** Dividend Growth model passed (1.2% yield); FY2025 dividend payments of ~$311m and buybacks of ~$300m (Yahoo fallback).
- **Balance-sheet optionality:** Net cash position at FY2025 year-end (cash $1.21bn vs total debt $0.85bn per Yahoo fallback) supports M&A (VirtuOx, May 2025) and returns.

The disconnect is sentiment, not fundamentals: interim filings show revenue and earnings accelerating through FY2026, whilst news flow highlights analyst caution on margins and competition. That creates a classic quality-value entry where the screen’s cheapness signal aligns with a business that is still compounding.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Competitive and market structure**
- The FY2025 10-K forward-looking statements cite emergence of new or growing competitors and supply-chain disruption. Philips’ return to the CPAP market after its recall remains the central competitive overhang; Morgan Stanley downgraded ResMed on margin and competition concerns (17 June 2026) and cut price targets on market competition (27 April 2026).
- Pharmaceutical OSA treatments (mentioned in FY2025 10-K business section) and GLP-1 weight-loss drugs create demand uncertainty. The FY2024 10-K framed GLP-1s as potentially funnel-positive for diagnosis; the market currently prices the opposite.

**Regulatory and reimbursement**
- Filing language highlights evolving reimbursement frameworks, healthcare reform, and Medicare/commercial payor dynamics as material variables. US device sales flow through HME providers subject to insurer policies — a screen blind spot.
- NYU patent litigation (AutoSet/AutoRamp features) is disclosed in the Q3 FY2026 10-Q: PTAB invalidated challenged claims in December 2023; district court proceedings continue. ResMed states routine litigation is not expected to be materially adverse in aggregate.

**Operational and supply chain**
- FY2025 10-K manufacturing discussion flags single-source suppliers and periodic transport disruptions, including semiconductor constraints. Any halt could limit device production.

**Governance and capital allocation**
- Chairman/CEO Michael Farrell sold ~$1.09m of stock (9 July 2026) and ~$1.0m (11 May 2026) per news manifest — modest relative to holdings but worth monitoring alongside ~$500m YTD treasury-stock purchases (Q3 FY2026 cash-flow statement).
- MatrixCare divestiture ($490m to Frazier Healthcare Partners, 7 July 2026) sharpens focus on core sleep/respiratory but removes a software revenue stream acquired for ~$750m in 2018; Needham maintained Hold on the news (8 July 2026).

**Macro and FX**
- >140-country revenue base creates FX and tariff exposure; FY2025 10-K cites direct/indirect tariff effects. Q3 FY2026 included $10.1m losses on equity investments in other income.

**Financial structure**
- Credit facilities carry a maximum leverage-ratio covenant (funded debt to EBITDA) per Q3 FY2026 10-Q note — not near breach given net cash, but relevant if debt-funded M&A accelerates.
- No going-concern or pension-funding warnings in available extracts. Goodwill of $3,043m (36% of total assets at 31 March 2026) reflects acquisition history; impairment risk if software growth slows post-MatrixCare sale.

**Screen limitations**
- P/B 4.5x is not deep value on asset basis. Timing signal insufficient; RSI/SMA200 unavailable in snapshot.
