## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

TE Connectivity is a global industrial-technology leader in connectors and sensors, organised into Transportation Solutions (54% of FY2025 net sales) and Industrial Solutions (46%), serving electrification, AI infrastructure, automotive, and industrial end markets. The quantitative screen rates TEL a **buy** (9/22 models, composite 68%), supported by strong returns on equity, free cash flow, and quality/GARP factor passes, though conviction is still building at 48%. The investment case rests on accelerating organic growth, margin expansion, and durable cash generation, set against a debate over whether recent AI/electrification momentum is already reflected in the share price after a multi-year rerating. Key tension: operational momentum and backlog strength versus cyclical auto exposure, integration of recent M&A, rising leverage, and sell-side caution on valuation.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value-oriented investor, TEL offers a rare combination of **quality at a reasonable earnings multiple** rather than deep asset cheapness. The screen passes five factor families—cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk—with standout models including Lynch PEG, Neff PEGY, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Composite Value, and Financial Health. Headline metrics (P/E 20.4, P/B 4.4, dividend yield 1.5%, ROE 22.7%, FCF ~$2.3bn per screen) point to a compounder trading below typical high-quality industrial peers on earnings, with data quality high at 19/20 metrics.

Business quality supports the screen signal. The FY2025 10-K describes TEL as a global industrial technology leader with connectivity heritage dating to 1941, operating in a ~$200bn addressable market across two focused segments. Interim filings show sustained top-line acceleration: H1 FY2026 net sales of **$9,413m** versus **$7,979m** in H1 FY2025 (+18%), with operating income up to **$1,917m** from **$1,438m** (+33%). Gross margin expanded to **37.0%** of sales in H1 FY2026 versus **35.3%** a year earlier (filing-derived). Management has complemented organic growth with bolt-on M&A—notably Richards Manufacturing in FY2025 (10-K)—while returning capital via dividends and buybacks.

The value angle is **normalised earnings power**: FY2024 reported net income was inflated by a large tax benefit (Yahoo fallback: **$3.19bn** net income, **$10.33** diluted EPS), whereas FY2025 normalised to **$1.84bn** net income and **$6.16** EPS with revenue growing to **$17.26bn** (+9%). At ~20x forward-normalised earnings, the stock screens as GARP rather than deep value, but quality and FCF conversion (FY2025 FCF **$3.20bn** on **$4.14bn** operating cash flow—Yahoo fallback) justify accumulation for investors seeking durable industrial franchises at a modest premium to cyclical trough earnings.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Macroeconomic and cyclical exposure (FY2025 10-K):** TEL explicitly flags that conditions in global/regional economies, capital markets, tariffs, trade barriers—including US–China tensions—and cyclical industry demand may adversely affect results, financial position, and cash flows. Automotive production cycles and European seasonal declines can pressure Transportation Solutions in certain quarters.

**Competitive intensity:** The 10-K states that industries served are highly competitive, with competition based on product breadth, innovation, price, quality, delivery, and service—limiting pricing power if end-market demand softens.

**Input costs and supply chain:** Dependence on copper, aluminium, gold, silver, palladium, and resins, sourced from limited geographies, creates cost and availability risk; the company has implemented price increases but may not fully offset inflation or tariff-driven costs.

**M&A and integration:** FY2025 featured **$2.63bn** of acquisitions (Yahoo) and goodwill rising to **$7.44bn** (Mar 2026 10-Q). Richards Manufacturing, Schaffner (referenced in XBRL tags), and ongoing bolt-ons carry integration and impairment risk not fully captured by static screens.

**Leverage:** Total debt increased from **$4.33bn** (FY2024) to **$5.82bn** (FY2025, Yahoo), with long-term debt **$5.55bn** at Mar 2026. Filing extracts reference a five-year credit facility and SOFR-based pricing tiers tied to debt ratings—covenant compliance is assumed but specific covenant headroom is **not available** in truncated extracts.

**Pension and post-retirement obligations:** **$766–767m** long-term liability (interim balance sheets)—material but not alarming relative to equity; still a cash-flow drag in adverse market conditions.

**Tax volatility:** Q1 FY2025 demonstrated earnings sensitivity to discrete tax items; reported GAAP earnings may diverge from underlying operating trends.

**AI/electrification narrative risk:** News and third-party commentary (Seeking Alpha, Apr 2026) highlight technological uncertainty in near-term AI upside—connectors benefit from datacom build-out, but product mix and margin contribution remain debated.

**Governance/regulatory:** Cybersecurity is disclosed as a dedicated 10-K item (Item 1C). Legal proceedings are referenced (Item 3) but details are **not present** in truncated body extracts. No material weakness or going-concern flags found in available text.
