## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

First Solar is the leading US-headquartered thin-film (CdTe) solar module manufacturer, benefiting from domestic manufacturing incentives, tariff protection, and surging US utility-scale demand tied to data-centre load growth. The quantitative screen flags the stock as a strong buy on cheapness (P/E 13.6, P/B 2.3), quality (ROE 18.4%), GARP, and balance-sheet strength (D/E ~6%, FCF ~$1.15bn), with all four metric families passing. Revenue and earnings have accelerated sharply—FY2025 net sales rose to $5.22bn and net income to $1.53bn (Yahoo fallback; annual filing bodies lack full financial tables)—yet the central debate is whether IRA/OBBBA tax-credit economics and US pricing power persist amid policy churn, Chinese oversupply abroad, and emerging litigation over disclosure and production utilisation. We view the screen signal as directionally supported by fundamentals, but not without material policy and event risk.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, FSLR offers a rare combination: a profitable, cash-generative industrial compounder trading at a mid-teens multiple despite ROE near 20% and a net-cash-lean balance sheet. The screen passes ten models—including Graham Enterprising, FCF Yield, Lynch PEG, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, and Financial Health—reflecting both absolute cheapness and business quality.

The qualitative case aligns with the quantitative one:

- **Moat and differentiation.** The FY2025 10-K describes First Solar as the world’s largest thin-film PV manufacturer and the largest in the Western Hemisphere, with an integrated, non-Chinese supply chain—a structural advantage as US policy restricts foreign-entity-of-concern (FEOC) content and imposes tariffs on imported modules.
- **Policy tailwinds.** Section 45X advanced manufacturing credits (~17 cents per watt for US-produced modules, per Q1 2026 10-Q notes) directly reduce cost of sales; government grants receivable totalled $823m on the 31 March 2026 balance sheet (current plus non-current).
- **Demand visibility.** US net sales accounted for 96% of FY2025 revenue (10-K body); Silicon Ranch Corporation and NextEra Energy each represented ≥10% of module net sales. Deferred revenue of $1.79bn (current plus long-term, Q1 2026 balance sheet) provides forward demand indication.
- **Earnings momentum.** Interim filings show sustained growth: Q1 2026 net sales of $1,044m (+24% YoY) and diluted EPS of $3.22; Q3 2025 net sales of $1,595m (+80% YoY) with diluted EPS of $4.24 (10-Q bodies).

The screen’s “strong buy” is therefore grounded in real business quality, not merely statistical cheapness—but conviction is tempered by policy cyclicality and legal overhangs the quant models cannot fully capture.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Regulatory and policy (primary risk).** The FY2025 10-K flags extensive uncertainty from the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA, July 2025), which amended IRA provisions: accelerated termination framework for Section 45Y/48E clean-energy credits (utility-scale solar must generally begin construction by early July 2026 to remain eligible), plus FEOC eligibility restrictions on Section 45X manufacturing credits. Management warns that if IRA/OBBBA financial benefits “vary significantly from our assumptions,” results could be adversely affected. This is not fully captured by backward-looking screen metrics.

**Cyclical and competitive.** Risk factors cite “structural imbalances in global supply and demand for PV solar modules” and Chinese competitors operating at minimal or negative margins with sovereign backing. First Solar has already **reduced Series 6 output** at international plants—a real-time signal of global pricing pressure outside the US.

**Customer concentration.** Silicon Ranch and NextEra each ≥10% of FY2025 module net sales (10-K). Loss or contract termination by large customers is explicitly flagged as a risk.

**Product quality / warranty.** Q1 2026 10-Q Note 11 discloses Series 7 manufacturing issues (2023–24 vintage modules) with estimated remediation costs of **$35m–$70m**; a **$47m** specific warranty liability was recorded at 31 March 2026. Voluntary remediation beyond warranty could be material.

**Legal and contingencies (filing language).**
- **Workplace injury litigation:** Jury award originally $51.3m, reduced to $21.8m; appeals ongoing. At 31 March 2026, **$21.8m accrued** in other current liabilities with a matching insurance receivable (Q1 2026 10-Q Note 11).
- **Patent litigation (Trabant Solar):** First Solar defending indemnification claims; mediation held April 2026; **no estimate of possible loss** disclosed.
- **Securities class actions:** Not detailed in available filing bodies, but news coverage (July 2026) reports multiple suits alleging misleading statements on tariff policy and production utilisation—a red flag the screen does not capture.

**Governance / insider activity.** News reports CEO Mark Widmar sold ~$2.6m of stock (May 2026) and broader insider selling (~$8.5m, June 2026). Not inherently disqualifying but worth monitoring.

**Balance-sheet / covenant.** No going-concern issue. February 2026 revolving credit facility upsized to **$1.5bn** (Q1 2026 10-Q); pricing tied to net leverage ratio or public debt rating. Total debt remains modest relative to equity and cash. Q1 2026 negative operating cash flow reflects working-capital timing, not distress.

**Pension:** No material pension red flags identified in available extracts.
