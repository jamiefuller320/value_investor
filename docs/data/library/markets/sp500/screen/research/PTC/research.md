## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

PTC Inc. is a subscription-led industrial software franchise (CAD, PLM, ALM, SLM) serving discrete manufacturers, with 95% recurring revenue per its FY2025 10-K. Operating momentum has been solid—ARR reached $2.49bn in Q1 FY2026 (+13% year-on-year)—yet the share price has de-rated sharply amid Russell index removals, AI-disruption concerns, and manufacturing macro uncertainty. The quantitative screen flags compelling value (P/E 12.3x, FCF yield 6.7%, ROE 34.4%, PEG 0.05) against a business generating strong cash conversion and expanding margins. The central debate is whether reported earnings and cash flow—boosted by subscription renewals, divestiture proceeds, and ASC 606 timing—are sustainable through a slower industrial capex cycle and intensifying competition from Autodesk, Dassault, and Siemens, plus generative-AI-native entrants.

---

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The screen’s Strong Buy rating rests on a rare combination for enterprise software: **cheapness, quality, GARP, and risk** all passing (four of four factor families). PTC clears Earnings Yield, FCF Yield, Lynch PEG, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Magic Formula, Piotroski F-Score, Composite Value, and Financial Health—ten models in total with high data quality (18/20 metrics).

Business quality supports the signal. PTC’s subscription model drives predictable ARR, high gross margins, and rising free cash flow. Filings describe a sticky installed base of 30,000+ customers across industrials, aerospace/defence, automotive, and medtech, with ~75% direct sales to large accounts. FY2025 recurring revenue was 95% of total (FY2024: 93%), underpinning retention-led growth rather than perpetual-licence volatility.

The valuation hook is the disconnect between fundamentals and price action. News coverage highlights a ~27% six-month share decline and Russell growth-index removals (July 2026), creating potential forced-selling pressure unrelated to operating performance. At 12.3x trailing P/E and 6.7% FCF yield on ~$986m of FCF (screen snapshot), the market prices PTC closer to a cyclical hardware supplier than a 34% ROE software compounder—particularly after the Kepware/ThingWorx divestiture crystallised ~$523m of cash (Q2 FY2026 10-Q).

For a value investor, the case is: **buy a moated, cash-generative subscription business at a cyclical trough multiple, with capital return (share repurchases) and portfolio focus (IoT divestiture) as catalysts.** Conviction is tempered by debt (~$1.27bn per FY2025 10-K), intangible-heavy balance sheet, and the risk that headline EPS is inflated by one-offs.

### Financial assessment

The trajectory is **positive**: double-digit revenue and ARR growth, expanding operating margins (high-20s to low-30s %), and FCF conversion consistently above $700m annually. FY2025’s EPS step-up reflects both operating leverage and lower financing costs after retiring the 3.625% 2025 senior notes.

Caveats for value investors:

1. **ASC 606 timing** — PTC explicitly warns that on-premises subscription revenue recognition timing can swing reported growth (Q3 FY2025 MD&A). ARR is the better operating metric.
2. **One-off gains** — Q2 FY2026 EPS is dominated by the IoT divestiture gain; normalised earnings are materially lower.
3. **Post-divestiture ARR growth** slowed to 3% in Q2 FY2026—a key metric to watch.
4. **Low cash balance** pre-divestiture and **$1.2bn+ debt** constrain flexibility despite covenant headroom.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Competitive and technology**

- FY2025 10-K names Autodesk, Dassault Systèmes, and Siemens as primary CAD/PLM competitors; IBM, Oracle, SAP, Microsoft, and Salesforce in adjacent markets.
- AI risk is elevated: filings warn that AI integration may not drive expected ARR if customers adopt rival solutions; news ("PTC stock falls after Google unveils AI Deep Think upgrade," 12 February 2026) reflects market sensitivity.
- SaaS transition risk: Windchill+ and Creo+ may not achieve adoption rates assumed.

**Cyclical and macro**

- Heavy exposure to discrete manufacturing; filings cite tariffs, supply-chain disruption, high rates, and strong USD as demand risks. Q3 FY2025 MD&A describes a "challenging" selling environment for "a couple of years."

**Balance sheet and capital structure**

- **Substantial indebtedness** (~$1.27bn, FY2025 10-K); variable-rate credit facility exposes PTC to rate rises (5.1–5.6% on borrowings per interim filings).
- Covenant breach could accelerate repayment (FY2024/FY2025 10-K risk language)—though interim filings confirm **compliance** at each quarter-end reported.
- Intangible-heavy balance sheet; impairment risk if ARR growth stalls.
- **Underfunded defined-benefit pension plans** referenced in FY2025 10-K XBRL tags; no quantified deficit in available body extracts.

**Governance and regulatory**

- Global compliance exposure (FCPA, GDPR, trade sanctions) with partner-heavy go-to-market; past violations referenced generically.
- Expanding sustainability disclosure obligations (EU CSRD/CSDDD, California climate laws) add cost and customer diligence risk.
- Cybersecurity: filings acknowledge regular security incidents, none material to date.

**Divestiture and execution**

- Kepware/ThingWorx sale reduces IoT exposure but **ARR growth decelerated** post-close; transition services may not offset dis-synergies.
- Aggressive repurchases ($826m in six months to March 2026) reduce share count but consume divestiture proceeds and limit deleveraging.

**Filing gaps**

- No **going concern** language found in available extracts.
- **Item 3 Legal Proceedings** body text is absent (truncated 10-K); contingent liability detail cannot be assessed from primary sources.
- Nine 8-K items in the index lack downloadable bodies.
