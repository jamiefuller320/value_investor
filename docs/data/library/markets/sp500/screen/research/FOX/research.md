# Fox Corporation (FOX) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T04:03:02.659703+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Fox Corporation is a news, sports and entertainment group built around FOX News Media, FOX Sports, Tubi and the FOX broadcast network. The quantitative screen flags a buy on cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP and risk metrics (P/E 13.4, P/B 2.0, ROE 15.2%, debt/equity 68%). Fiscal 2025 was a strong year, but the first nine months of fiscal 2026 show softer reported earnings driven mainly by mark-to-market losses on equity investments—notably Flutter—rather than operating deterioration. Underlying segment EBITDA is still growing. The central debate is whether a discounted multiple adequately compensates for Murdoch governance, defamation litigation, cord-cutting, and balance-sheet risk from the proposed ~$22bn Roku acquisition.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, FOX offers a rare combination: premium live content franchises (NFL, news, sports) with a balance sheet that screens as conservative, meaningful free cash flow, and active capital return. The screen passes 11 of 22 models including Graham Enterprising, FCF Yield, Buffett Quality, Magic Formula and Financial Health—five of four required factor families (cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, risk). That profile fits a cash-generative media asset trading below broad-market multiples despite mid-teens returns on equity.

Business quality supports the quantitative signal. Cable Network Programming remains the earnings engine: nine-month segment EBITDA rose 4% to $2,371m (Q3 FY26 10-Q), underpinned by affiliate fee growth despite subscriber declines. Television segment EBITDA rose 15% to $733m over the same period, helped by Tubi digital growth and sports sublicensing, even as advertising fell on tough Super Bowl and political-cycle comps. The FY2025 10-K describes Tubi view time growing 13% and reaching ~2.2% of US television viewing—evidence of a credible streaming pivot without abandoning the linear moat.

Capital allocation reinforces the value case. The FY2025 10-K states FOX returned ~$1.25bn via buybacks and dividends and retired $600m of debt. Through nine months of FY26, the company repurchased ~31m shares for ~$1.9bn (Q3 FY26 10-Q), with ~$3.5bn authorisation remaining. A step-up tax basis from the 2019 Disney spin continues to provide cash tax benefits. At 13x earnings and ~2x book, the market appears to price structural decline more heavily than the filing record of growing distribution revenue and resilient segment margins.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Litigation and regulatory.** Q3 FY26 10-Q legal proceedings note: FOX News Media and FOX Television Stations face defamation lawsuits, including Smartmatic USA Corp. seeking $2.7bn in damages (filed Feb 2021). Summary judgment motions were argued 2 Dec 2025; no trial date set. The company states settlements to date are not material individually or in aggregate, but "the amount of additional liability, if any, … cannot be estimated." Regulatory and investigative inquiries are ongoing. XBRL tags in the FY2025 10-K reference Tubi and Fubo lawsuit matters. This is a material tail risk not captured by value screens.

**Governance.** Dual-class structure (FOXA/FOX) with Murdoch family control. A new stockholders agreement dated 8 Sep 2025 (Q3 FY26 10-Q) caps collective Murdoch voting power at 44% of Class B and grants the company a right of first refusal on certain Murdoch share sales—resolving the Sep 2025 succession dispute reported in news but entrenching family influence.

**Operating/cyclical.** Cord-cutting continues: MD&A cites lower average MVPD subscribers (~$175m nine-month revenue headwind). Advertising is cyclical and event-driven (Super Bowl, elections, sports calendar). FY2026 launch costs for Fox One direct-to-consumer service added ~$155m to operating expenses over nine months.

**Investment volatility.** Mark-to-market losses on Flutter and other equity securities drove $785m of non-operating losses in 9M FY26. Fair-value investments fell from $1.2bn (Jun-25) to $458m recurring-fair-value balance (Mar-26 per notes)—adding earnings volatility screens miss.

**M&A / leverage (news, not yet in filing financials).** Multiple headlines (Jun 2025) report a ~$22bn cash-and-stock acquisition of Roku. This is the largest strategic shift in the past year and is not reflected in indexed filing bodies. If completed, it would materially alter leverage, integration risk and the investment narrative; the screen's debt/equity metric would likely worsen.

**Pension.** Non-current pension/postretirement accrual of $264m (Mar-26); plans largely closed to new participants except certain union employees. Not a primary concern but worth monitoring.

**Other.** Venu Sports JV with ESPN/WBD (announced Feb 2024, Q1 FY25 10-Q) was blocked by preliminary injunction (Aug 2024); appeal pending—strategic uncertainty in sports streaming. No going-concern language in available extracts. Eight 8-K bodies unavailable for covenant detail.
