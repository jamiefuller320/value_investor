## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

EOG Resources is a large-cap US shale operator with a disciplined, returns-focused model: low-cost Delaware, Eagle Ford, and Utica acreage; 5.5 Bn Boe proved reserves; and a capital-return framework combining dividends and buybacks. The quantitative screen flags the stock as cheap on quality-adjusted metrics (P/E 13.6, P/B 2.4, ROE 18.2%, debt/equity 27%, yield 3.0%) while passing cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk families.

The investment case rests on reserve and production growth (Encino/Utica acquisition, international gas optionality) against a balance sheet that remains investment-grade in character, with interim filings showing strong Q1 2026 earnings momentum. Valuation looks reasonable for a premium shale operator, though not deeply distressed.

The central debate is whether 2026's earnings rebound and capital returns offset commodity cyclicality, integration risk from the ~$5.7bn Encino deal, and a step-up in leverage and capex. Filings confirm no going-concern issues; the main uncertainty is oil and gas price durability, not solvency.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, EOG offers a rare combination in upstream energy: screen-level cheapness plus demonstrable business quality. The screen passes Graham Enterprising, Lynch PEG, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Acquirer's Multiple, and Financial Health models — five of four required factor families — with high data quality (19/20 metrics). That breadth suggests the buy signal reflects fundamentals, not a single metric artefact.

Business quality supports the screen. The FY2025 10-K (filed 24 February 2026) describes a strategy focused on highest return, lowest cost production across the Delaware, Eagle Ford, Dorado, Appalachian/Utica, and Rocky Mountain basins, with 641 net well completions in 2025 and 585 planned for 2026. Proved reserves rose 16% to 5,514 MMBoe at year-end 2025 (from 4,748 MMBoe in 2024), with the Encino acquisition adding Utica scale. Production reached 449.8 MMBoe in 2025 (+16% YoY).

Capital discipline remains central. The board expanded the share repurchase authorisation to $10bn (effective November 2024; referenced in Q1 2026 10-Q), and Q1 2026 filings show $418m of treasury stock purchased alongside a $1.02 quarterly dividend. Management targets returning excess cash via dividends, special dividends, and buybacks rather than growth-at-any-cost drilling.

The screen's 3.0% yield and 18.2% ROE align with a company generating substantial operating cash flow even in softer commodity years. Encino and international gas entries (Bahrain, UAE Block 3) add optionality the screen cannot fully capture, but the core US asset base remains the value anchor.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Commodity cyclicality.** Filings repeatedly stress that EOG cannot predict oil, NGL, and gas prices. The Q1 2026 10-Q cites Middle East conflict impacts on full-year 2026 price assumptions. Q1 2026 benefited from ~$72/bbl US oil; FY2025 averaged $65.63/bbl. A sustained downturn would compress FCF and the buyback/dividend capacity the screen rewards.

**Post-acquisition leverage.** Total debt rose from $5.1bn (2024) to $8.4bn (2025, Yahoo) following Encino and senior note issuance. Long-term debt of $7.9bn at Q1 2026 remains manageable against $30.9bn equity and $3.0bn undrawn revolver, but represents a material shift from EOG's historically net-cash profile. Integration execution in the Utica is untested at this scale.

**Impairments and write-offs.** Yahoo records $770m asset impairments in 2025. Filing bodies reference proved/unproved property impairments across 2023–2025. Softening long-term price assumptions could trigger further charges.

**Regulatory and environmental.** The FY2025 10-K Item 1 outlines extensive federal (BLM/BIA), state, and climate-related regulation, including methane rules, waste prevention, and hydraulic fracturing restrictions. Filings note potential material adverse effects from federal lease limitations, royalty changes, and GHG regulation — partially offset by recent US policy reversals (OBBBA royalty rollback, methane charge deferred to 2034). Litigation over EPA endangerment finding rescission is anticipated.

**Customer concentration.** Two purchasers each exceeded 10% of total hydrocarbon and marketing revenues in 2025 (10-K Item 1). Loss of a refiner customer is deemed non-material individually, but concentration remains a cyclical risk.

**Governance and insider activity.** News manifest records multiple executive share sales in March–May 2026 (EVPs Leitzell and Janssen; other insider sales ~$257k in May 2025). These are not large relative to market cap but warrant monitoring alongside routine compensation-related disposals.

**Pension.** FY2025 10-K XBRL tags reference defined benefit pension plans (US and foreign). No material underfunding figure appears in available body extracts; pension is a secondary risk pending full Note disclosure.

**Legal/contingencies.** Q1 2026 10-Q references commitments and contingencies (Note 5) but available extracts do not disclose material litigation. Item 3 legal proceedings cross-references contain no substantive case detail in extracted bodies. No going-concern language identified.

**Index/flow risk.** Russell 1000 Dynamic Index removal (27 June 2026, per news) may create passive selling pressure unrelated to fundamentals — a technical headwind the screen does not capture.
