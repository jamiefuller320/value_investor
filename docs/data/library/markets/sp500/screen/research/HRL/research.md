## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Hormel Foods is a branded consumer staples business (SPAM, Planters, Jennie-O, Skippy, pepperoni) with more than $12bn of annual revenue, S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrat status, and a portfolio skewed toward protein and snacking. The quantitative screen flags a **buy** on cheapness, dividend yield (~4.6%), GARP (PEGY 0.07), and balance-sheet health, but headline P/E (~30x) reflects depressed earnings rather than growth. Fiscal 2025 was a trough year: net earnings fell sharply on impairments (Garudafood $163.7m; Planters/Chi-Chi’s trade names), commodity pressure, and portfolio repositioning. Interim results show modest top-line recovery but still-soft year-on-year profitability. The central debate is whether restructuring, divestitures (Brazil exit, turkey business, partial Justin’s sale), and snacking innovation can restore EPS power—or whether HRL is primarily a high-yield bond proxy with structurally lower returns.

---

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, the case rests on **quality of franchise, income, and balance sheet at a depressed earnings point**, not on near-term growth.

**Quantitative alignment.** The screen passes four model families (cheapness, dividend, GARP, risk) with a 59% composite score and high data quality (19/20 metrics). Neff PEGY (0.07) and high dividend yield screen well; Magic Formula and Financial Health models also pass. At ~1.8x P/B and ~4.6% yield, the market prices HRL as a mature, cash-generative staple rather than a compounder—consistent with a patient value entry after a multi-year de-rating.

**Business quality.** SEC filings describe a global branded food company with three segments (Retail, Foodservice, International), iconic trademarks, and roughly 20,000 employees. Walmart accounted for **15.6%** of gross sales in fiscal 2025; the top five customers **~38%**—concentrated but typical for US staples. The company is a Dividend Aristocrat with decades of payout growth; interim filings show quarterly dividends of **$0.29–0.29** per share.

**Why buy now.** Earnings are cyclically and idiosyncratically depressed (impairments, input costs, portfolio actions), while operating cash flow has rebounded in the first half of fiscal 2026 (**$528m** H1 vs **$366m** prior year, per Q2 FY2026 10-Q). Cash rose to **$827m** at 26 April 2026. Management is actively pruning (Justin's 51% sale, Brazil/turkey exits per news) and investing in snacking (Pepperoni Snack Bites). For a value investor, the screen’s dividend + health + relative cheapness on normalised cash earnings offers an asymmetric setup if margins stabilise—even with low ROE (5.8% on trailing data).

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Earnings quality and impairments.** FY2025 impairments (Garudafood **$163.7m**, Planters **$59.1m**) signal acquisition/portfolio missteps. International goodwill (**$258.9m**) is at “heightened risk of impairment”; **$683.3m** of indefinite-lived intangibles flagged similarly (FY2025 10-K). Justin’s trade name also flagged.

**Customer concentration.** Walmart **15.6%** of sales; top five customers **~38%** (FY2025 10-K). Loss of a major account would materially affect Retail/International.

**Commodity and input costs.** Pork, turkey, beef, nuts drive COGS; hedging limits upside in favourable markets (10-K risk language). WSJ reported price increases citing costlier inputs (28 August 2025).

**Labour and restructuring.** ~20% of employees under collective bargaining; **~700** workers’ contracts expire within a year. November 2025 restructuring cut **~250 jobs** (WSJ/PR Newswire)—execution risk on Transform and Modernize.

**Legal/regulatory.** Pork antitrust litigation referenced in 10-Q (class actions from 2018; settlements noted for certain plaintiffs). Q2 FY2026 10-Q states other proceedings are **not expected to be material** to financial condition or liquidity. No **going concern** language identified in available filing bodies.

**Pension.** Non-current employee benefit obligations **~$359m** (Yahoo FY2025); pension assets on balance sheet **~$212m** (Apr 2026 filing). Not alarming but a fixed-cost overhang.

**Governance/insider activity.** Director Gary Bhojwani sold **~$495k** in shares (10 July 2026, Investing.com)—immaterial size but worth noting.

**Macro.** Not used to veto the screen; 10-year Treasury ~4.54% (macro_context) supports yield-oriented names but does not change the fundamental earnings debate.
