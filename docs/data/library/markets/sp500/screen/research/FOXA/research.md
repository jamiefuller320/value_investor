# Fox Corporation (FOXA) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T04:06:54.420092+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Fox Corporation is a focused US news, sports, and entertainment group—FOX News Media, FOX Sports, the FOX broadcast network, 29 owned-and-operated stations, and AVOD platform Tubi—trading at a modest absolute valuation (screen P/E ~14.9x, P/B ~2.2x) despite franchise-level live content and a strong balance sheet. The quantitative screen flags cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk metrics across 10 passing models, consistent with durable affiliate fees, rising Tubi scale, and FY2025 free cash flow near $3bn (Yahoo fallback; see Financial Review). The central debate is whether the proposed ~$22bn Roku acquisition (June 2026 news flow) unlocks a scaled streaming distribution platform or dilutes a capital-light, cash-generative model, against a backdrop of cord-cutting, NFL rights renewal risk, and headline litigation (Smartmatic). We view the name as a quality value compounder with event risk, not a deep cyclical bargain.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, FOXA offers a rare combination: **must-have live news/sports inventory**, **moderate leverage** (screen debt/equity ~68%; net debt ~$1.3bn at FY2025 year-end per Yahoo), and **active capital return** ($1.25bn returned in FY2025 per the FY2025 10-K narrative, plus $600m debt retirement). The screen’s buy rating is supported by fundamentals the models capture well:

- **Cheapness / FCF:** FY2025 operating cash flow $3.32bn and free cash flow $2.99bn (Yahoo fallback) align with FCF Yield and Acquirer’s Multiple passes; FY2025 ended with ~$5.4bn cash (10-K body).
- **Quality / ROE:** FY2025 net income $2.26bn on stockholders’ equity ~$12.0bn implies mid-teens ROE (Yahoo), matching the screen’s 15.2% ROE and Buffett Quality pass.
- **GARP / contrarian:** P/E ~14.9x on depressed sentiment after the Roku announcement and YTD share weakness (news), while nine-month FY2026 segment EBITDA is still growing (10-Q).
- **Risk / financial health:** ~$1.0bn undrawn revolving credit facility; management states it does not anticipate covenant non-compliance (Q1 FY2026 10-Q). No going-concern language observed.

Business quality is above average for legacy media: FOX News has led cable news 20+ years (10-K); Tubi reached ~11bn viewing hours in FY2025 with ~2.2% US TV share (10-K); Television segment EBITDA rose through the first nine months of FY2026 despite advertising volatility (10-Q). The screen’s 100% “yield” metric is a data artefact—cash dividends were $0.56 per share over nine months to March 2026 (10-Q)—and should not be relied upon.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Regulatory & litigation**
- **Smartmatic:** Lawsuit filed February 2021 seeking **$2.7bn** in damages; trial not expected before early 2026; company states loss is neither probable nor reasonably estimable but warns resolution could be materially adverse (Q1 FY2026 10-Q). This is a tail risk no screen captures.
- **U.K. Newspaper Matters indemnity** inherited from the 21CF separation—ongoing contingent liability (10-Q contingencies note).
- **Fubo litigation** and other defamation matters referenced in filing metadata.
- **Delaware derivative actions** relating to Murdoch family trust/governance (10-Q).

**Governance**
- Dual-class structure; September 2025 **2025 Stockholders Agreement** with LGC Holdco and Murdoch family trusts caps collective Class B voting power at **44%** and grants Fox a right of first refusal on certain share sales (Q3 FY2026 10-Q). Concentrated control persists.

**Industry & cyclical**
- **Cord-cutting:** Nielsen-estimated FOX News subscribers fell from **67m to 61m** and FS1 from **67m to 61m** (June 2025 vs June 2024, FY2025 10-K)—affiliate fee growth depends on rate increases offsetting unit declines.
- **Sports rights inflation:** NFL renewal risk cited by BofA downgrade (news, 25 Feb 2026); FIFA/streamer bidding pressure flagged in recent coverage.
- **Advertising cyclicality:** Q3 FY2026 revenue −9% YoY on a 24% advertising decline in the quarter (10-Q MD&A).

**Balance sheet / M&A**
- **Roku deal (~$22bn, June 2026):** Would transform leverage and strategic profile; market reaction was negative (multiple news items June 2026). Financing, regulatory approval, and integration risk are not in historical financials.
- **FOX One** DTC service launched August 2025—SG&A stepped up (+10% nine-month corporate costs, 10-Q); profitability timeline uncertain.
- **Investment marks:** Flutter/FanDuel stake and other equity investments add earnings volatility.

**Pension:** Non-current employee benefit obligations ~**$276m** at FY2025 (Yahoo); plans appear funded per 10-K pension disclosures— not a primary red flag.

**Covenants / going concern:** Q1 FY2026 10-Q states the company **does not anticipate noncompliance** with revolving credit facility covenants. No going-concern qualification found.
