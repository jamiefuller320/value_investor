## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Zimmer Biomet is a global orthopaedic medtech leader—knees, hips, sports medicine, and robotics—whose shares screen as a buy on cash-flow yield, GARP metrics, and balance-sheet health despite weak reported ROE. Revenue is re-accelerating after Paragon 28 (foot/ankle) and Monogram (robotics) deals, with Q1 2026 net sales up 9.3% year-on-year to $2.09bn and operating profit up 28%, yet nine-month 2025 GAAP earnings still trailed the prior year on acquisition costs, restructuring, and higher interest. The valuation hook is modest multiples (P/E 24.1, P/B 1.4, FCF yield 5.8%, PEG 0.71) against a franchise generating ~$1.4bn annual free cash flow (Yahoo fallback for FY2025). The central debate is whether robotics-led growth and cost transformation can restore earnings power fast enough to offset rising leverage (~$7.5bn total debt), intangible-heavy accounting, and intensifying robotic competition—notably from Intuitive Surgical in adjacent categories.

---

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The quantitative screen flags ZBH across all four value families—cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk—with high data quality (19/20 metrics). That profile fits a value investor seeking a durable healthcare compounder at a cyclical trough in sentiment rather than fundamentals.

**Business quality.** Filings describe a broad musculoskeletal portfolio: reconstructive implants, SET (sports medicine, extremities, trauma, CMFT), bone cement, and integrated digital/robotic technologies. No single customer exceeds 2% of net sales; ~85% of revenue is from stocked/consignment inventory tied to surgical procedures (10-K FY2024/FY2025 business sections). Knees remain the largest category ($828.6m in Q1 2026, +4.5% YoY per Q1 2026 10-Q), but SET grew 19.5% YoY—partly reflecting Paragon 28 foot/ankle exposure.

**Why the screen works here.** Reported ROE (6.1%) understates economic return: heavy goodwill/intangibles ($14.7bn per Yahoo FY2025 balance sheet) and $258m restructuring/M&A charges (Yahoo FY2025 income statement) suppress net income to $705m while operating cash flow remained robust ($1.70bn; FCF $1.42bn, Yahoo). The screen’s FCF Yield, Lynch/Neff PEG, Dividend Growth, Composite Value, and Financial Health passes capture this cash-earnings gap. PEG of 0.71 against 34.1% screen growth likely reflects a low base and forward normalisation rather than sustained hypergrowth.

**Capital allocation.** Active buybacks ($250m in Q1 2026 alone per 10-Q cash flow; news reports a $250m authorisation increase, May 2026) and a 1.1% dividend yield support the income/risk buckets. M&A (Paragon 28, $1.28bn; Monogram, $176m upfront plus up to ~$570m CVRs) targets faster-growing niches and robotics—strategically coherent but execution-dependent.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Leverage and M&A integration.** Total debt rose from $6.2bn (Yahoo FY2024) to $7.5bn (Yahoo FY2025), with Q1 2026 10-Q showing $7.5bn outstanding. Paragon 28 ($1.28bn) and Monogram ($176m upfront, up to ~$570m CVRs through 2030) add integration risk and contingent liabilities. Monogram’s first development milestone was achieved in Jan 2026 (Q1 2026 10-Q)—positive, but regulatory/revenue CVRs remain uncertain.

**Earnings quality.** GAAP net income has declined two consecutive years despite revenue growth. Intangible amortisation (~$666m FY2025 per Yahoo), restructuring ($258m FY2025), and acquisition expenses mask underlying margin. Tangible book value is **negative $1.97bn** (Yahoo FY2025)—equity is largely goodwill-dependent.

**Competitive and technological.** FY2025 10-K forward-looking language cites *“competition; pricing pressures; dependence on new product development, technological advances and innovation”* and risks that acquisitions *“will not be realised within the expected time period.”* Robotics competition is acute: news flow frequently contrasts ZBH with Intuitive Surgical’s da Vinci 5/Ion momentum. ZBH’s robotics narrative depends on ROSA plus Monogram’s semi-/fully-autonomous platform—unproven at scale.

**Restructuring.** The 2025 Restructuring Plan expanded in Dec 2025 to **~$155m pre-tax charges by 2027** (Q1 2026 10-Q), including agent contract terminations—suggesting ongoing US distribution transformation risk flagged in the 10-K.

**Legal/regulatory.** Nov 2025 news: *“Zimmer Biomet Holdings, Inc. (ZBH) Investigation — Hagens Berman”* (17 Nov 2025)—shareholder investigation, not a filed charge; treat as sentiment overhang. Filings reference *“product liability, intellectual property and commercial litigation losses”* (10-K). No going-concern language in available extracts. **Defaults Upon Senior Securities:** none reported in interim filings.

**Pension.** Defined benefit plans referenced in AOCI reclassifications (Q1 2026 10-Q); no underfunding quantified in available extracts—**gap in source data**.

**Macro/cyclical.** Elective procedure volumes, hospital capital budgets, and FX (international ~42% of Q1 2026 sales) affect growth. Inventory **$2,246.8m** at Mar 2026 remains elevated.

**Governance/insider activity.** SVP Lori Winkler sold ~$250k stock (Mar 2026, news); routine but worth monitoring alongside heavy buybacks.
