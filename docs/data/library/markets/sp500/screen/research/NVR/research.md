## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NVR is a premium US homebuilder operating an asset-light lot-acquisition model (forfeitable-deposit LPAs rather than owned land), integrated mortgage banking, and one of the most aggressive share-repurchase programmes in the sector. The quantitative screen flags a Strong Buy on cheapness, quality, and risk (8/22 models; composite 75%; P/E 16.2, ROE 33.3%, FCF yield ~7%), consistent with a high-return franchise trading at a modest earnings multiple despite a shrinking share count.

The valuation hook is quality-at-a-discount: exceptional capital allocation, net-cash-lean balance sheet, and durable ROE, set against a cyclical trough in housing demand. Filings confirm earnings are already rolling over — FY2025 net income fell materially versus FY2024, and management guides to further revenue and margin pressure through H1 2026.

The central debate is whether screen-level cheapness captures a temporary cyclical dip in an elite operator, or a structural step-down in earnings power as affordability, cancellations (17% in 2025), and community repricing weigh on margins. Near-term operational trends weaken the screen signal even as the balance sheet and capital-return story remain intact.

---

## INVESTMENT THESIS

NVR fits a value investor’s quality-cheapness profile more convincingly than most homebuilders.

**Business quality.** NVR avoids direct land development in the ordinary course, controlling finished lots via fixed-price LPAs with forfeitable deposits — limiting downside to deposit forfeiture rather than full land write-downs. Homes are built primarily on a pre-sold basis across 37 metropolitan areas under Ryan Homes, NVHomes, and Heartland Homes. NVRM originates mortgages exclusively for NVR buyers (~16,400 loans / ~$6.0bn in 2025), adding ancillary revenue with limited balance-sheet risk (loans sold within ~30 days).

**Quantitative alignment.** The screen passes eight models spanning FCF Yield, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Magic Formula, Acquirer’s Multiple, Composite Value, and Financial Health — covering three of four factor families (cheapness, quality, risk). Key metrics: P/E 16.2, P/B 5.2, ROE 33.3%, profit margin 12.5%, FCF ~$1.25bn. For a capital-light builder with minimal dividend leakage (none paid, ever), a 7% FCF yield and sub-17x P/E on peak-normalised earnings is attractive relative to sector peers.

**Capital allocation as moat.** NVR has returned capital almost exclusively via buybacks. FY2025 repurchases totalled ~$1.83bn (Yahoo fallback — see Financial Review); the 2025 10-K confirms two $750m authorisations (May and August 2025) and a further $750m authorisation on 11 February 2026. Diluted share count fell from ~3.32m (FY2024) to ~3.07m (FY2025). Treasury stock at cost exceeded $15.7bn at year-end 2025 against shareholders’ equity of ~$3.86bn — a multi-decade shrink of the float that mechanically boosts per-share metrics.

**Why Strong Buy, despite cyclicality.** Screens reward current FCF generation, balance-sheet health, and return on equity — all of which remain strong. NVR’s deposit-based lot strategy and ~$1.9bn cash (FY2025) against ~$900m senior notes provide optionality in a downturn: community repositioning, lot deposit walk-aways, and opportunistic repurchases. The stock’s high absolute price (~$6,700) masks an underlying value-per-share accumulation story that quantitative models capture better than headline P/B.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Cyclical / demand.** Filings emphasise interest-rate sensitivity, affordability constraints, and cyclical history. FY2025 cancellation rate rose to 17%; Q2 2025 new orders fell 11%. Management expects weak demand to persist through 2026. This is the primary risk not fully captured by trailing screen metrics.

**Forward margin compression.** FY2025 10-K: repositioning to compete for fewer buyers will have a “materially negative impact” on gross margins in H1 2026. Q1 2026 gross margin already fell to 19.6%. Further pricing pressure is guided.

**Lot deposit and inventory risk.** Although the LPA model limits loss to forfeited deposits, filings warn of deposit forfeitures and land-under-development impairments in adverse conditions. Nine-month 2025 contract land deposit impairments were **$40.1m**; ~**$85.4m** impairment allowance on ~15,000 controlled lots at Q3 2025. Q1 2026 added **$8.9m** net impairments.

**Mortgage banking / secondary market.** NVRM depends entirely on homebuilding volume. Risk factors cite secondary-market disruption, repurchase/warranty claims on sold loans, and rate-lock mismatches. Repurchase facility: up to **$150m**.

**Leverage and covenants.** **$900m** senior notes; credit agreement contains restrictive covenants (no financial maintenance covenants on revolver, but restrictions on secured debt, sale-leasebacks, and asset disposals per Q1 2026 10-Q). Not a going-concern issue — no such language in filings.

**Governance / capital structure.** No cash dividend ever; capital return is buyback-only, benefiting continuing holders but offering no income buffer. Executive stock options granted at **$5,720.10/share** (May 2025, per news). March 2025 shareholder votes on emissions transparency and investor rights drew scrutiny (Simply Wall St, 19 March 2025). Dual-class not an issue; low public float (~2.8m shares outstanding) can amplify liquidity and volatility.

**Legal.** Item 3 (both 10-Ks): ordinary-course litigation “not expected to have a material adverse effect.” Contingencies noted in forward-looking statements but no quantified large exposures in available extracts.

**Competitive.** Highly competitive industry; NVR competes against better-capitalised nationals and the resale market. Market-leader positioning in core MSAs is a mitigant but not guaranteed.
