# Biogen Inc. (BIIB) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T04:18:02.243380+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Biogen presents a value-oriented biopharma case: a cash-generative franchise trading at ~22x earnings and ~6% free cash flow yield, with the quantitative screen flagging cheapness, GARP, and financial health. The investment hook is the LEQEMBI (lecanemab) Alzheimer's ramp—recent FDA approval of a weekly subcutaneous formulation should expand access—offset against a structurally declining multiple sclerosis (MS) portfolio and binary pipeline readouts. The central debate is whether LEQEMBI growth, anti-CD20 royalties, and pending M&A (Apellis) can re-rate a name where reported ROE is just 7.7% and recent Phase 2 tau data (diranersen) disappointed the market. We view the screen's buy signal as directionally supported but not without qualification.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The quantitative screen rates BIIB a buy on five models spanning FCF Yield, Lynch PEG (0.72), Magic Formula, Composite Value, and Financial Health, with a sector-relative composite of 72% and high data quality (18/20 metrics). For a value investor, the appeal is not growth-at-any-price but **cash-backed cheapness**: P/E 22.4 and P/B 1.6 against ~$1.9bn annual free cash flow (Yahoo fallback; see Financial Review) and a balance sheet that has strengthened materially—cash and equivalents reached $3.0bn at year-end 2025 (Q1 2026 10-Q) versus $2.4bn a year prior.

Business quality is mixed but improving at the margin. Core MS products (TECFIDERA, TYSABRI) face generic and biosimilar pressure, which filings explicitly flag. However, Q1 2026 interim results (10-Q, filed Apr 2026) show total revenue of **$2,478m** (+1.9% YoY), net income of **$319.5m** (+33% YoY), and diluted EPS of **$2.15** versus **$1.64**—driven partly by Alzheimer's collaboration revenue rising to **$59.5m** from **$33.0m** and anti-CD20 program revenue of **$419.1m** from **$378.2m**. Management's forward-looking statements (Q1 2026 10-Q) cite sufficient liquidity from existing funds plus operating cash flow to meet debt service and capital needs for the foreseeable future—consistent with the screen's Financial Health pass.

The screen's 31.1% growth metric likely reflects earnings normalisation after 2025 one-offs rather than top-line acceleration; revenue has been broadly flat at ~$9.7–9.9bn since 2023. Value investors should treat BIIB as a **cash-flow compounder with pipeline optionality**, not a classic deep-value asset play—intangibles and goodwill total ~$15.5bn on the balance sheet, suppressing tangible book and ROE.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Product and competitive**
Filings (Q1 2026 10-Q risk factors) emphasise substantial dependence on revenue timing from LEQEMBI and SKYCLARYS, plus exposure to generic entrants for TECFIDERA and biosimilar competition for TYSABRI. Alzheimer's is a crowded field—Eli Lilly's Kisunla (donanemab) is a direct competitor, and reimbursement/coverage determinations remain uncertain.

**Pipeline binary outcomes**
July 2026 news (see below) highlights Phase 2 CELIA data for diranersen (tau-targeting ASO with Ionis): cognitive decline slowed 26% over 18 months but the trial **did not meet its primary dose-response endpoint**. Biogen intends to advance to Phase III regardless—a capital-intensive bet with uncertain probability of success. A separate Parkinson's candidate also reportedly missed key trial goals (Jul 14, 2026 news).

**M&A and integration**
The pending **Apellis acquisition** (announced Mar 31, 2026) adds EMPAVELI and SYFOVRE to the portfolio and expands nephrology/immunology exposure. Filings reference contingent consideration remeasurement charges (**$20.5m** in Q1 2026 vs **$9.6m** in Q1 2025) and goodwill impairment assessment risk given **$6.5bn** of goodwill on the balance sheet. Integration of Reata (SKYCLARYS), HI-Bio, Alcyone, and potentially Apellis adds execution complexity.

**Legal and regulatory**
Filing metadata references ongoing **securities litigation**, **derivative actions**, and **IMRALDI patent litigation**. No going-concern language appears in available extracts. Management asserts liquidity is adequate for the foreseeable future including debt service—a positive, though not a substitute for pipeline success.

**Financial structure**
ROE of 7.7% (screen) reflects heavy intangible asset base; amortisation was **$137m** in Q1 2026 alone. Reported earnings remain volatile due to IPR&D milestones, restructuring, and fair-value remeasurements. Pension obligations are referenced but appear immaterial in comprehensive income (**$0.5m** OCI movement in Q1 2026).

**Governance**
Michael Parini appointed chief legal officer (Jul 15, 2026 news)—routine but noteworthy given active litigation docket.
