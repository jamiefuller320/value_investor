# Skyworks Solutions, Inc. (SWKS) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T03:13:48.885910+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Skyworks Solutions is a leading analog and mixed-signal semiconductor supplier to smartphone, Wi‑Fi, automotive, and industrial markets, currently trading at a depressed valuation after a multi-year earnings reset. The quantitative screen flags a strong buy on cheapness (P/E 23.7, P/B 1.5, FCF yield 8.0%, dividend yield 4.9%) and financial health, but reported ROE of 6.2% and screen-recorded earnings growth of −44.9% reflect a business in cyclical decline rather than classic quality compounder territory. The central debate is whether generous cash returns and a pending Qorvo merger (~$22bn combined enterprise value, expected close early 2027) can re-rate the stock, or whether Apple-linked share loss, margin compression, and integration/antitrust risk keep the multiple capped. Filings show no going-concern language; liquidity and covenant headroom appear adequate, but customer and receivables concentration remain acute.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, SWKS offers a rare combination in semiconductors: a sub‑2× tangible book multiple (P/B 1.5), an ~8% FCF yield on screen inputs, and a dividend that management has continued to raise (currently $0.71 per quarter per the Q2 FY2026 10‑Q). The screen passes seven of 22 models across all four families—cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk—with a composite score of 86% (sector-relative 77%) and high data quality (19/20 metrics). Neff PEGY of 0.05 and Magic Formula inclusion suggest the market is pricing a cyclical trough rather than permanent impairment.

Business quality is mixed. Skyworks retains scale (~6,900 customers, ~4,900 products per FY2025 10‑K), a broad RF/analog portfolio, and ~5,200 issued patents. FY2025 R&D rose to $785.5m (from $631.7m in FY2024 per the 10‑K), signalling continued investment despite revenue pressure. However, earnings power has fallen sharply from the FY2022 peak, and interim filings attribute recent weakness to market-share loss at a significant customer (widely understood to be Apple, which exceeded 10% of net revenue in each of FY2023–FY2025). The value case rests on mean-reversion of handset RF content, Wi‑Fi diversification gains (partially visible in interim MD&A), and capital return—$2.0bn repurchase authorisation through February 2027 with ~$1.2bn remaining as of April 2026—rather than on near-term growth.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Customer concentration:** Apple exceeded 10% of net revenue in FY2023, FY2024, and FY2025 (FY2025 10‑K). The three largest receivables balances represented 82% of gross AR at October 2025 and 80% at September 2024. Interim MD&A explicitly cites market-share loss at this customer as the primary revenue headwind.

**Cyclical and competitive:** The FY2025 10‑K states the semiconductor industry is "highly cyclical and subject to significant downturns," with competitors including Broadcom, Qorvo, Qualcomm, Murata, and Texas Instruments. Established-product ASP erosion is acknowledged as typical.

**Qorvo merger (transformative risk):** Announced 27 October 2025; combined enterprise value ~$22bn; consideration of 0.960 SWKS shares plus $32.50 cash per Qorvo share; expected close early 2027. Stockholder approvals obtained 11 February 2026. FTC issued a Second Request on 5 February 2026, extending HSR review. Bridge financing commitment of up to $1,500m remains available (Q2 FY2026 10‑Q). Termination fees of $298.7m or $100.0m apply under specified circumstances (FY2025 10‑K). The 10‑K warns the merger will require substantial additional indebtedness, may disrupt customer and employee relationships, and carries integration, synergy, and antitrust risks. Transaction costs of $53.4m were expensed in H1 FY2026 alone.

**Governance and management:** CFO transitions are recent—interim CFO Robert Schriesheim appointed May 2025 (Q3 FY2025 10‑Q); Phil Carter named CFO per news coverage (August 2025). CEO turnaround narrative is unproven.

**Operational:** Inventory build (+17% from FY2025 year-end to April 2026) warrants monitoring in a declining demand environment. Restructuring charges ($22.5m in H1 FY2026) relate to facility consolidation. Denso patent litigation was settled in Q2 FY2026 with an immaterial charge (Q2 FY2026 10‑Q Note 9).

**Pension:** Pension adjustments are immaterial in interim comprehensive income (≤$0.2m). No material pension liability flagged.

**Regulatory/geopolitical:** FY2025 10‑K cites China exposure, export controls, tariffs, and environmental compliance as material risks. No going-concern language appears in any indexed filing body.

**Screen blind spots:** The −44.9% earnings growth input and 6.2% ROE are not captured by cheapness or dividend models; cyclical trough vs structural decline is the key unscreened question.
