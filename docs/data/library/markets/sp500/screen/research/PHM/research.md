# PulteGroup, Inc. (PHM) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T04:00:52.844588+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
PulteGroup is a top-tier US homebuilder trading at a modest multiple despite a cyclical earnings trough. The quantitative screen flags value across cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk families (P/E 12.4, P/B 1.9, ROE 16.2%, debt/equity 19%), which aligns with a balance sheet that remains conservative for the sector. The central debate is whether margin compression and weaker backlog represent a normal downcycle that already discounts the stock, or the early phase of a more prolonged affordability-driven downturn. Filings confirm earnings pressure in 2025–2026 but also aggressive capital return, extended liquidity, and land-option flexibility that distinguish PHM from weaker peers.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, PHM offers a rare combination: screen-validated cheapness on earnings and book, plus business quality metrics (Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Financial Health passes) backed by a national scale builder with ~4% US new-home share, diversified brands (Centex, Pulte Homes, Del Webb, DiVosta, John Wieland), and a capital-light land strategy (57% of controlled lots under option at year-end 2025 per the FY2025 10-K).

The screen’s buy signal is supported by fundamentals the models cannot fully capture:

- **Balance-sheet quality:** At 31 March 2026 (10-Q), shareholders’ equity was $13.0bn against total assets of $18.2bn; notes payable were $1.82bn with no revolver borrowings and $1.4bn of remaining revolver capacity. The company was in compliance with all revolver covenants (minimum tangible net worth, maximum debt-to-capitalisation). Yahoo fallback (filing bodies truncated before full annual statements): net debt of ~$183m at FY2025 year-end — effectively net-cash relative to equity.
- **Capital return:** Management prioritises buybacks and dividends while keeping leverage modest (stated strategy in FY2025 10-K). Q1 2026 repurchased 2.4m shares for $308.2m; quarterly dividend rose 18% to $0.26/share (Jan 2026). Board added $1.5bn to repurchase authorisation in April 2026.
- **Operational resilience:** Despite volume declines, the company remained solidly profitable (Q1 2026 net income $347m, diluted EPS $1.79) and generated positive operating cash flow.

The screen’s 83.0% “yield” metric appears to be a data error (likely ~0.8% annualised from filing dividend data); dividend growth is real but not the primary thesis. Conviction on the screen is “building” at 48% — appropriate given cyclical uncertainty — and deep research supports the value case while flagging near-term earnings risk.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclical / demand (10-K Item 1A):** Management emphasises that elevated mortgage rates, inflation, and affordability constraints have materially affected demand despite Fed rate cuts from September 2024. Interest-rate volatility, reduced mortgage availability, and competition from existing-home inventory and rentals remain primary risks. Q1 2026 MD&A notes geopolitical volatility in March 2026 further weakened consumer confidence.

**Margin and land:** Gross margins are in sustained decline (24.4% Q1 2026 vs 27.5% prior year). Land acquisition and development costs are rising; there is an inherent lag before pricing actions recover margins. Land impairments and deposit write-offs continue ($10.9m in Q1 2026; $126.6m unusual/impairment items in FY2025 per Yahoo). The 10-K warns that deteriorating markets could require significant inventory write-downs, as occurred in 2006–2011.

**Inventory / spec risk:** House and land inventory rose to $13.30bn at 31 March 2026 from $12.93bn at year-end 2025. Management is actively reducing spec inventory but incentives pressure margins.

**Off-balance-sheet commitments:** Surety bonds of $3.0bn and letters of credit of $348m (31 March 2026). Land option remaining purchase price $9.7bn — flexibility is a strength, but walk-away costs and deposit exposure exist ($1.30bn deposits and pre-acquisition costs capitalised).

**Financial services:** Captive mortgage origination (64% capture rate in 2025) creates representation-and-warranty repurchase risk and interest-rate hedge mismatch risk (10-K). Pulte Mortgage repurchase facility: $455m outstanding at 31 March 2026.

**Legal / warranty:** No going-concern language in available filings. Litigation is described as routine; management does not expect material adverse impact, though outcomes are inherently uncertain (Q1 2026 Note 8). Self-insured construction-defect reserves total $267.5m; IBNR represents ~74% of general liability reserves — long-tail exposure extending beyond ten years.

**Governance:** Orderly CFO transition — James Ossowski succeeded Robert O’Shaughnessy in February 2025; O’Shaughnessy remains EVP until March 2026. No pension crisis flagged; employee benefit liabilities of $212m non-current (Yahoo FY2025 balance sheet) — not a primary concern but worth monitoring.

**Regulatory / tariffs / climate:** Extensive risk-factor coverage on building codes, environmental regulation, tariffs on imported materials, and climate-related insurance cost inflation.
