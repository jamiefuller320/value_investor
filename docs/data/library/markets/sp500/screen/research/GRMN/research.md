# Garmin Ltd. (GRMN) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-17T04:10:32.394858+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Garmin is a Swiss-domiciled, vertically integrated designer of GPS-enabled hardware and software across fitness, outdoor, aviation, marine, and auto OEM markets, with a fortress balance sheet and a multi-year earnings compounding profile. The quantitative screen rates GRMN as a **buy** (5/22 models; composite 57%), supported by Neff PEGY, Economic Moat, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, and Financial Health passes, with PEGY of 0.14 against ~21.5% growth and ROE near 20%. The central debate is not business quality—filings show double-digit revenue and earnings growth through FY2025 and into Q1 2026—but whether a ~28x P/E and a strong multi-year share-price run leave enough margin of safety for a pure value entry. Key watchpoints are Taiwan manufacturing exposure, tariff pressure on marine margins, and the auto OEM segment’s persistent operating losses.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, Garmin offers an unusual combination: **growth at a reasonable adjusted price** plus **balance-sheet optionality**. The screen’s buy signal rests on five distinct factor families (cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, risk)—broader than a single multiple trigger—and is corroborated by filing-level fundamentals:

- **Quality / moat:** Vertically integrated manufacturing (Taiwan, US, Europe, China), 2,100+ patents, high switching costs in certified aviation and marine ecosystems, and recurring subscription/service revenue (Garmin Connect+, inReach, aviation databases). The company passes Economic Moat and Financial Health screens.
- **Earnings power:** FY2025 revenue reached **$7.25bn** and net income **$1.66bn** (Yahoo fallback—see Financial Review); Q1 2026 net sales of **$1.75bn** (+14% YoY) and net income of **$405m** (+22% YoY) per the 10-Q filed 29 April 2026 show momentum is intact.
- **Capital return:** Dividends rose from **$3.00/share (FY2024)** to **$3.60/share (FY2025)** per the Q1 2026 10-Q; a new **$500m** repurchase programme (2026 Program) replaced the completed 2024 Program.
- **Risk screen pass:** Net cash posture (~$4.3bn cash and marketable securities at Q1 2026 per filing MD&A), minimal debt (~$165m capital lease obligations per Yahoo), and no going-concern language in available filings.

The screen’s PEGY of **0.14** and dividend yield of **1.7%** suggest the market still under-prices Garmin’s growth relative to quality peers, even at a headline P/E of **27.9**. The value case is “compounder at a GARP-adjusted discount,” not deep cyclical cheapness.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Geopolitical / operational:** The FY2025 10-K risk section (body extract) explicitly flags **Taiwan manufacturing concentration** and PRC–Taiwan conflict scenarios as material disruption risks. Consumer products are principally manufactured in Taiwan.

**Segment drag:** Auto OEM remains loss-making at the operating level (Q1 2026: **($6m)** operating loss; FY2025 10-K language warns that auto OEM investments may **negatively impact consolidated operating income** and could require restructuring if contracts underperform).

**Competitive:** Principal competitors named in filings include **Apple, Samsung, Google/Huawei** in fitness and **Honeywell, Collins Aerospace, ForeFlight/Jeppesen** in aviation—well-resourced rivals that can replicate features and outspend on marketing.

**Supply chain:** Sole-source components (semiconductors, displays, memory, batteries) and periodic shortages are acknowledged; inventory rose to **$1.85bn** at Q1 2026 (+4% vs year-end), increasing obsolescence and demand-forecast risk.

**Tariffs / trade policy:** Q1 2026 MD&A cites **higher tariff costs** on marine gross margin and notes uncertainty around US trade policy and potential IEEPA tariff refunds (no benefit recognised as of 28 March 2026).

**Cyber / IP:** Extensive risk-factor disclosure on IT failures, data breaches, and IP litigation; cyber insurance may be insufficient for severe events.

**Governance / pension:** No defined-benefit pension exposure identified in extracts; US subsidiaries use **defined contribution** plans. Swiss law limits severance for senior officers; **no employment agreements** with key executives and **no key-person insurance**—succession risk. CEO Clifton Pemble and CFO Douglas Boessen adopted **Rule 10b5-1 selling plans** in March 2026 (disclosed in Q1 2026 10-Q)—routine but worth monitoring.

**Legal / contingencies:** Q1 2026 Note 8 states management does **not** believe material loss contingencies are reasonably possible for the quarter; patent and product-liability matters are ongoing in the normal course. **No going-concern qualification** appears in available extracts.

**Valuation (screen gap):** At P/E ~28 and P/B ~5.2, Garmin fails most pure “cheapness” models (only 5/22 pass). The buy signal is GARP/quality-driven, not deep value—an overlay the screen alone does not emphasise.
