# Grafton Group plc (GFTU.L) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-19T10:07:31.154089+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Grafton Group is a pan-European merchanting and distribution group serving construction and DIY markets, currently flagged as a Strong Buy on a multi-factor value screen (P/E 12.6, P/B 1.1, ~4.0% yield, current ratio 2.11). The investment case rests on a cyclically depressed earnings base that is stabilising—FY2025 revenue re-accelerated while net profit remains well below the 2022 peak—combined with sustained capital return (serial buybacks, shrinking share count) and a net-cash-lean balance sheet. Valuation appears modest relative to cash generation (FCF ~£206m on Yahoo data) and dividend/GARP criteria all pass. The key debate is whether European revenue momentum and management’s 2030 growth plan offset persistent UK weakness and construction-cycle risk; primary RNS/annual report extracts were not captured in this research run, limiting filing-level risk verification.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The quantitative screen rates Grafton a Strong Buy with four of four factor families passing—cheapness, dividend, GARP, and risk—supported by nine individual models including Graham Defensive/Enterprising, Schloss Low P/B, FCF Yield, Neff PEGY, Dividend Growth, Dreman Contrarian, Composite Value, and Financial Health. At P/E 12.6 and P/B 1.1, the market prices the stock as a mature, low-growth distributor despite FY2025 revenue of £2.52bn (Yahoo) rising 10.4% year-on-year and H1 2026 revenue reportedly up 6.7% to £1.34bn (news headline, 13 Jul 2026). Business quality indicators align with the screen: current ratio 2.11, meaningful FCF (~£188–206m annually per screen/Yahoo), and a deliberate shrink of ordinary shares from ~223m (2022) to ~191m (2025) via buybacks. For a value investor, Grafton offers a rerating optionality if operating margins recover toward pre-2023 levels while the shareholder-return programme continues; the screen’s risk-family pass suggests leverage and liquidity are not immediate constraints, though ROE at 8.4% reflects the earnings trough rather than peak-cycle economics.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
Without primary filing bodies, going-concern, covenant, and contingency language cannot be assessed from this research bundle—this is itself a material research gap.

**Cyclical and geographic:** Construction merchanting is highly cyclical. News summaries explicitly flag UK market weakness through FY2025; a European slowdown could reverse the recent revenue rebound. H1 2026 revenue growth with held (not raised) profit guidance may signal margin pressure persisting.

**Earnings quality / peak-to-trough:** Net income remains ~35% below FY2022 despite higher FY2025 revenue, suggesting structural or cyclical margin erosion. ROE of 8.4% (screen) is low for a “quality compounder” narrative.

**Acquisition and goodwill:** Goodwill of £659m (~40% of equity) creates impairment risk if acquired businesses underperform or if integration costs rise—Yahoo shows ongoing M&A cash outflows (£17–69m p.a. 2022–25).

**Lease-adjusted leverage:** £397m of capital lease obligations are material; headline net debt looks benign, but total indebtedness including leases is closer to £600m against EBITDA of ~£340m.

**Pension:** Defined benefit obligations are small (£11.6m non-current liability) but should be monitored in full annual report notes (not available here).

**Governance / disclosure:** Recent RNS feed is dominated by routine buyback and PDMR notices; no governance controversies surfaced in news, but deep-dive filing review is pending.

**Market timing:** Screen timing is neutral with RSI ~69 (elevated); price is ~3.4% above 200-day SMA—not extreme, but limits near-term entry urgency.
