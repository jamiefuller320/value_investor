## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

ME Group International plc operates a global installed base of photobooths and unattended laundry machines, with management increasingly pivoting growth capital towards laundry partnerships. The quantitative screen rates the name a **strong buy** on cheapness (P/E ~7.7, yield ~7.3%), quality (ROE ~28%), modest leverage (debt/equity ~28%), and broad factor coverage (14/22 models; five families passed). Yahoo-sourced annual data show sustained revenue and profit growth through FY2025, but free cash flow has softened as capex rises and cash is returned via dividends and deleveraging. The central debate is whether laundry expansion and a discounted multiple compensate for cyclical photobooth weakness—especially in France—a June 2026 profit-guidance cut, and an earlier delay in FY25 audited accounts, none of which the screen fully captures.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

For a value investor, MEGP fits a “cash-generative asset base at a low multiple” profile. The screen passes cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk families, with a composite score of 79% (88% sector-relative) and high data quality (19/20 metrics). Reported fundamentals (Yahoo fallback, as no filing bodies are available) support that framing: revenue rose from £259.8m (FY2022) to £315.4m (FY2025), net income from £38.8m to £56.6m, and diluted EPS from 10.2p to 14.9p, while total debt fell from £118.1m to £43.0m and equity expanded to £213.0m.

The business combines photobooth installed-base economics with a faster-growing laundry segment that recent news describes as offsetting softer booth volumes. Strategic actions—a new Asda partnership (April 2026), elevated network capex (~£65.6m in FY2025), and share buybacks (July 2026)—point to reinvestment plus shareholder return. At roughly 7–8× earnings and a 7%+ yield, the market appears to price execution and consumer cyclicality rather than balance-sheet distress. That gap between asset-backed cash generation and sentiment-driven de-rating is the value hook—provided revised FY2026 guidance holds and governance noise does not escalate.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

1. **Governance / reporting:** The February–March 2026 **delay in FY25 audited financial statements** (RNS headlines) is a significant red flag; severity, remediation, and auditor commentary are unknown without filing bodies. June 2026 press (“The boardroom problem behind Me Group’s share price tumble”, *Investors’ Chronicle*, 4 June 2026) raises board/governance concerns not reflected in quantitative screens. A “Corporate Governance Report” RNS was published 17 June 2026, but body text is unavailable.

2. **Operational / cyclical:** Photobooth demand is discretionary and geographically concentrated; **French consumer weakness** drove a **~22–26% share fall** and guidance cut on 1 June 2026. Recovery into July interim results suggests volatility rather than structural collapse, but the segment remains cyclically exposed.

3. **Free cash flow vs. dividend:** FY2025 FCF (£25.2m) was below cash dividends paid (£29.8m); sustained divergence would pressure the 7%+ yield thesis if capex stays elevated.

4. **Leverage and liquidity:** Balance-sheet deleveraging is positive, but cash has fallen to £56.5m while inventory and capex rise. Total debt of £43.0m against £56.5m cash leaves a manageable net cash position, though working-capital build (inventory +£9.7m YoY) bears watching.

5. **Competitive / structural:** Unattended laundry is competitive but partnership-led (Asda); photobooths face long-term smartphone substitution risk—partially mitigated by location/convenience economics but not eliminated.

6. **Screen limitations:** Conviction score is only **50%** (new signal, one week at strong buy); price sits **below the 200-day moving average** with neutral timing—entry timing risk despite strong value metrics.

7. **Filing feed noise:** Several ticker RNS API entries in the index appear to relate to **other issuers** (e.g. Brera Holdings, DCC, Beazley)—suggesting feed contamination; ME-specific risk disclosure should be validated against clean primary documents.
