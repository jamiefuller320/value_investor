## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
ME Group International plc operates a global network of photobooths and unattended laundry machines, with a growing emphasis on laundry partnerships (including Asda). The quantitative screen rates the stock a **strong buy** on cheapness (P/E ~7.7, yield ~7.3%), quality (ROE ~28%), modest leverage (debt/equity ~28%), and dividend/GARP characteristics across 14 of 22 models. Yahoo-sourced annual data show five years of revenue and profit growth through FY2025, but free cash flow has softened as capex rises and cash has been returned via dividends and debt reduction. The central debate is whether laundry expansion and a discounted multiple compensate for cyclical photobooth weakness (notably France), a June 2026 profit-guidance cut, and an earlier delay in FY25 audited accounts—issues the screen cannot fully capture.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, MEGP presents a classic “cash-generative asset base at a low multiple” profile. The screen passes five factor families—cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk—with a composite score of 78% (87% sector-relative) and high data quality (19/20 metrics). Reported fundamentals (Yahoo fallback) support that framing: revenue rose from £259.8m (FY2022) to £315.4m (FY2025), net income from £38.8m to £56.6m, and diluted EPS from 10.2p to 14.9p, while total debt fell from £118.1m to £43.0m and equity expanded to £213.0m. Return on equity implied by FY2025 earnings on average equity is high-teens to high-twenties, consistent with the screen’s quality signals.

The business model combines installed-base economics (photobooths) with a faster-growing laundry segment that management and sell-side commentary describe as offsetting softer booth volumes. Strategic moves—a new Asda partnership (April 2026), ongoing network capex (~£66m in FY2025), share buybacks (July 2026)—align with reinvestment plus shareholder return. At roughly 7–8x earnings and a 7%+ yield, the market appears to price in execution risk and consumer cyclicality rather than insolvency. That gap between asset-backed cash generation and sentiment-driven de-rating is the value hook—provided revised FY2026 guidance holds and governance noise does not escalate.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
1. **Governance / reporting:** The February–March 2026 **delay in FY25 audited financial statements** (RNS headlines) is a significant red flag; severity, remediation, and auditor commentary are unknown without filing bodies. June 2026 press (“The boardroom problem behind Me Group’s share price tumble”, *Investors’ Chronicle*, 4 June 2026) raises board/governance concerns not reflected in quantitative screens.

2. **Operational / cyclical:** Photobooth demand is discretionary and geographically concentrated; **French consumer weakness** drove a **~22–26% share fall** and guidance cut on 1 June 2026. Recovery into July interim results suggests volatility rather than structural collapse, but the segment remains cyclically exposed.

3. **Free cash flow vs. dividend:** FY2025 FCF (£25.2m) was below cash dividends paid (£29.8m); sustained divergence would pressure the 7%+ yield thesis if capex stays elevated.

4. **Leverage and liquidity:** Balance-sheet deleveraging is positive, but cash has fallen to £56.5m while inventory and capex rise. A June 2026 **“Issue of Debt”** RNS headline (body unavailable) warrants monitoring.

5. **Competitive / structural:** Unattended laundry is competitive but partnership-led (Asda); photobooths face smartphone substitution risk over the long term—partially mitigated by location/convenience economics but not eliminated.

6. **Screen limitations:** Conviction score is only **49%** (new signal, one week); price sits **below the 200-day moving average** with neutral timing—entry timing risk despite strong value metrics.

7. **Filing contamination:** Several ticker RNS API entries in the index relate to **other issuers** (e.g. Brera Holdings, DCC)—suggesting feed noise; ME-specific risk disclosure should be validated against clean primary documents.
