# Rio Tinto Group (RIO.L) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-24T17:17:16.954447+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Rio Tinto is a dual-listed global miner (iron ore, copper, aluminium/lithium) that the quantitative screen rates as a **buy**, passing eight of 22 models across cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP and risk (P/E 15.1, yield 4.4%, ROE 16.4%, D/E 35%). Filings show revenue and underlying EBITDA recovering in FY2025 on copper and aluminium strength, while attributable profit fell to $10.0bn as iron ore margins normalised; the balance sheet remains investment-grade in tone but net leverage rose materially after the Arcadium lithium acquisition. The valuation hook is a cyclically depressed multiple on a tier-one asset base with a visible growth pipeline (Simandou, Oyu Tolgoi, lithium). The central debate is whether copper/lithium optionality and operational turnround offset iron ore cyclicality, rising capex, governance/social-licence overhang, and earnings that screens understate at the trough.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
For a value investor, RIO.L offers a rare combination of **screen-validated cheapness and demonstrable franchise quality**. The screen’s buy signal (composite 60%, sector-relative 66%, timing “accumulate”, RSI ~45) is supported by passes on Graham Enterprising, Neff PEGY, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Acquirer’s Multiple and Financial Health — a breadth that suggests the discount is statistical rather than a single-factor artefact.

Business quality anchors the case. Companies House annual extracts (FY2025 accounts, filed April 2026) show consolidated sales revenue of **$57.6bn** (+7% vs $53.7bn in 2024), **underlying EBITDA $25.4bn** (+9% vs $23.3bn), and **net cash from operating activities $16.8bn** (+8% vs $15.6bn). Copper underlying EBITDA more than doubled to **$7.4bn**; aluminium and lithium rose to **$4.6bn**; iron ore eased to **$15.2bn** from $17.0bn — a mix shift toward energy-transition metals that the market may not fully capitalise at current multiples. Management targets a **3% CAGR in copper-equivalent production (2024–2030)**, $650m annualised productivity benefits (with more targeted), and **$5–10bn** of asset-base cash release, while maintaining a **60% of underlying earnings dividend policy** (402 US cents declared in both 2024 and 2025).

The screen’s 4.4% yield and sub-3x P/B sit on a business that generated double-digit ROE in the snapshot and has paid dividends for a decade at the policy floor. Near-term price action (trading broadly in line with the 200-day SMA per the screen) supports staged accumulation rather than chasing momentum. The thesis is **quality cyclical at a reasonable price**, not deep value on distressed assets.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclical / commodity:** Iron ore still dominates earnings (~29% segment EBITDA in 2025, but historically higher share). Revenue and PAT remain below 2022 peaks despite higher 2025 EBITDA — screens may overstate normalised earnings if commodity prices mean-revert.

**Leverage / capital allocation:** Net debt roughly **tripled YoY** on Yahoo data (Arcadium plus capex). FCF ($4.5bn fallback) **trails dividends** ($6.1bn) in 2025 — dividend coverage depends on the cycle and asset-sale programme ($5–10bn targeted).

**Operational / safety:** FY2024 CH reported **five fatalities** at managed operations (vs zero in 2023). FY2025 emphasises Safe Production System rollout; safety risk is not fully captured by financial screens.

**Environmental / provisions:** 20-F metadata references large **decommissioning, restoration and rehabilitation provisions** and restructuring provisions (Kennecott). Long-dated closure liabilities are material but not quantified in readable extracts here.

**Governance / social licence:** Historical ESG controversies (e.g. Juukan Gorge) and ongoing stakeholder scrutiny are referenced in annual narrative; social-licence risk affects project approvals (Simandou, Pilbara expansions, lithium).

**Execution / M&A:** Arcadium integration, Simandou ramp-up, and Brockman/Pilbara capex ($1.8bn announced March 2025 per 6-K exhibit list) raise execution risk. **Glencore mega-merger talks (January 2026) collapsed** over price (February 2026) — strategic uncertainty without clear synergy realisation.

**Management transition:** Investegate headline (22 May 2025): **Chief Executive succession plan**; operating model and executive team updates (27 August 2025). Leadership change adds transition risk not in quantitative models.

**Pension / employee:** 20-F metadata includes **pensions and post-retirement healthcare** segments; amounts not in CH extracts — treat as unresolved.

**Competitive:** Peer iron ore supply (e.g. BHP record output, July 2026 news) caps pricing power; copper cost leadership is improving but Kennecott outages show asset concentration risk.
