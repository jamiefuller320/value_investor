Timing is neutral—RSI 35, trading below the 200-day moving average—so the screen recommends accumulation rather than chasing momentum.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Jurisdictional and operational:** Endeavour operates across West Africa—a region exposed to political instability, regulatory change, security incidents, and community relations risk. These are not captured by quantitative screens and require mine-level disclosure absent from the filing bodies.

**Earnings quality:** Yahoo data shows recurring asset impairments ($123m–$200m annually in 2023–2025) and special charges. Reported net income volatility (losses in 2022–2024, sharp recovery in 2025) suggests normalisation is essential before treating the current P/E as representative.

**Gold price cyclicality:** Revenue nearly doubled in 2025; a material portion likely reflects higher gold prices rather than volume alone. Screen growth metrics (105.7%) may overstate sustainable earnings power if bullion retreats.

**Minority interests:** Non-controlling interests of $383m (2025) indicate joint ventures or partial ownership structures that complicate attributable cash flow and governance.

**Leverage and covenants:** Net debt is low, but total debt of $686m and lease obligations remain. Without annual report extracts, covenant headroom, hedging policy, and reclamation provisions cannot be assessed.

**Governance / capital structure:** Recent RNS activity is dominated by buybacks and PDMR dealings rather than strategic updates. No going-concern, pension, or contingency language is available from filing bodies.

**M&A overhang:** June 2026 press speculation ("Barrick weighs UK listing for African assets in $30B tie-up with Endeavour", Mining.com, 1 June 2026) introduces integration and antitrust uncertainty, though this is unconfirmed.

**Valuation:** P/B of 3.3× is elevated for a traditional deep-value approach; the case depends on earnings and FCF, not asset backing.
