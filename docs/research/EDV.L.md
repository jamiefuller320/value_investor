## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Endeavour Mining is a FTSE-listed West African gold producer that screens as a strong buy on cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk metrics (P/E 13.9, FCF yield 17.5%, ROE 31.1%, yield 2.9%). The valuation hook rests on a sharp 2025 earnings and cash-flow inflection—revenue rose 58% to $4.23bn and free cash flow reached $1.13bn—against a balance sheet that has deleveraged materially (net debt down to $144m). The central debate is whether 2025 normalised profitability ($957m normalised income) is sustainable at lower gold prices, or largely a cyclical peak amplified by impairments and unusual items in prior years. Screen conviction is modest (42%, one week at signal) with neutral technical timing.

## INVESTMENT THESIS

The quantitative screen flags Endeavour as a rare multi-family pass: 11 of 22 models clear, spanning FCF Yield, Lynch PEG (0.13), Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Acquirer's Multiple, and Financial Health. That profile fits a value investor seeking cash-generative commodity exposure at a discount to growth—screen growth of 105.7% against a P/E of 13.9 and P/B of 3.6.

Business quality, as reflected in the screen's quality and risk families, is supported by operating scale and balance-sheet repair. Revenue has compounded from $2.07bn (2022) to $4.23bn (2025), gross profit margin expanded from 36.2% to 49.5%, and 2025 operating cash flow reached $1.66bn. Management has directed surplus cash to debt reduction ($1.53bn repaid), dividends ($288m), and buybacks ($89m), consistent with a capital-disciplined producer rather than a pure cycle lever.

The screen's dividend and GARP passes align with a company transitioning from loss-making reported earnings (2022–2024) to $679m net income and $2.74 diluted EPS in 2025. For a value investor, the case is that the market may still be pricing residual impairment noise and prior-year losses, whilst the underlying asset base—$3.74bn net PPE across West African operations—generates substantial free cash at current gold prices.

## FINANCIAL REVIEW

**Note:** `financials_annual.json` provides only partial 2021 data; the analysis below covers 2022–2025 (four complete years).

**Revenue and margins.** Total revenue rose from $2.07bn (2022) to $2.11bn (2023), $2.68bn (2024), and $4.23bn (2025)—a 105% increase over the period, with the largest step in 2025 (+58% year-on-year). Gross profit followed a similar trajectory: $749m → $745m → $869m → $2.09bn. Gross margin improved from 36.2% (2022) to 49.5% (2025), with operating income reaching $1.87bn in 2025 versus $633m in 2022. Reported EBITDA was $2.08bn in 2025 (normalised EBITDA $2.50bn), up from $1.20bn in 2022, though 2023–2024 were depressed by large unusual items.

**Earnings quality.** Reported net income to common stockholders was negative in 2022 (−$57m), 2023 (−$209m), and 2024 (−$300m), before turning positive at $679m in 2025 (diluted EPS $2.74). Losses in 2023–2024 were driven by substantial impairments ($123m and $200m respectively) and unusual items totalling −$233m and −$462m. Even in the strong 2025 year, unusual items of −$419m and asset impairments of $193m remain material; normalised income of $957m is meaningfully above reported $679m. Minority interests absorbed $210m of 2025 earnings, reflecting joint-venture structures that reduce attributable profit.

**Leverage and balance sheet.** Total debt fell from $1.11bn (2023) and $1.17bn (2024) to $686m (2025). Net debt declined from $727m (2024) to $144m (2025), with cash and equivalents of $453m. Common stockholders' equity was $3.03bn against total assets of $5.61bn; tangible book value stood at $2.90bn. Long-term provisions of $148m and deferred tax liabilities of $347m warrant monitoring but do not presently overwhelm equity. Working capital narrowed to $114m from $507m (2023), partly reflecting higher inventory ($431m vs $225m in 2023).

**Cash generation.** Operating cash flow strengthened from $1.02bn (2022) to $1.66bn (2025), though 2023 was weaker at $647m and 2024 at $943m. Free cash flow was $591m (2022), negative $116m (2023) amid $763m capex, $258m (2024), and $1.13bn (2025). Capex has been elevated throughout ($426m–$763m annually; $532m in 2025), consistent with sustaining and developing mine assets. 2025 financing cash outflows of $1.15bn—dividends, buybacks, and net debt repayment—confirm the company is in a cash-harvesting phase.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Commodity cyclicality.** Revenue and margin expansion in 2025 are consistent with a higher gold-price environment; a sustained bullion correction would compress EBITDA and FCF rapidly. News flow through 2026 repeatedly links EDV share moves to gold price swings, underscoring beta to the metal rather than idiosyncratic growth.

**Earnings volatility and impairments.** Three consecutive years of reported losses (2022–2024) followed by heavy unusual items and impairments in every year reviewed suggest earnings are not stable on a reported basis. Value screens passing on ROE and FCF may overweight the strong 2025 year.

**West African operating risk.** As a West African gold producer (per news coverage), Endeavour faces jurisdiction-specific risks—regulatory change, fiscal take, security, and infrastructure—that quantitative screens do not capture. No material regulatory actions appear in the news manifest, but absence of coverage is not evidence of absence.

**Minority interests and tax.** Minority interests consumed $210m of 2025 profit; tax provision was $454m on $1.34bn pre-tax income (effective rate ~34%). Deferred tax liabilities of $347m may create future cash tax headwinds.

**Governance and pension.** The source files contain no pension obligation data and no governance disclosures; these cannot be assessed in this pass.

**Capital allocation at peak.** Aggressive debt repayment, dividends, and buybacks in 2025 may prove pro-cyclical if gold prices retreat; the screen's dividend pass should be weighed against commodity timing.
