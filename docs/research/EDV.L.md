## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Earnings quality.** Reported net losses in 2022–2024 despite positive operating income, driven by impairments ($122–199m per year), write-offs, and tax provisions ($210–454m). Screen ROE and P/E may reflect 2025 normalisation rather than a stable run-rate.

**Commodity cyclicality.** The 58% revenue jump in 2025 is consistent with elevated gold prices; a bullion correction would compress revenue, margins, and FCF rapidly. News flow in H1 2026 shows repeated sector selloffs tied to gold pullbacks.

**West African operating risk.** Endeavour's asset base is concentrated in Burkina Faso, Côte d'Ivoire, Mali, and Senegal (per company profile implied by news). Political instability, mining-code changes, community relations, and security incidents are not captured in quantitative screens.

**Governance and minority leakage.** Substantial minority interests ($383m equity; $210m income allocation in 2025) reduce economic interest for ordinary shareholders and complicate capital allocation analysis.

**Tax and working capital.** Cash tax payments were $362m (2025); tax payables of $496m on the balance sheet suggest ongoing fiscal complexity across jurisdictions.

**Pension.** No pension liability data present in the source file; cannot assess.

**Data gaps.** 2021 annual financials are largely absent; net debt for 2022 is missing. Screen growth rate of 105.7% may be optically inflated by the 2024 earnings trough.
