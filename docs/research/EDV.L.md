The valuation hook is exceptional free cash flow yield (17.8% on screen), a low Lynch PEG (0.13), and a 2.9% dividend yield on a P/E of 13.7 and ROE of 31.1%. Normalised EBITDA of $2.50bn (versus reported $2.08bn after $419m of unusual items) indicates the operating business is stronger than headline net income history implies. At 13.7x earnings with 31.1% ROE and sector-relative composite outperformance (74% vs 67%), the stock fits a GARP/value overlay: cheap on cash, improving on leverage, and paying a meaningful yield — provided an investor accepts gold-price cyclicality.

## FINANCIAL REVIEW
**Revenue** has accelerated sharply over the five-year window (2021 income-statement data is incomplete in the source file). Total revenue rose from $2.07bn (2022) to $2.11bn (2023), $2.68bn (2024), and $4.23bn (2025) — a 105% increase from 2024 to 2025, consistent with the screen's growth metric. **Margins** improved materially in 2025: gross profit expanded from $869m (32.5% margin, 2024) to $2.09bn (49.4%, 2025), and operating income rose from $715m to $1.87bn. Normalised EBITDA followed a similar arc: $1.22bn (2022), $1.10bn (2023), $1.32bn (2024), $2.50bn (2025).

**Reported earnings** remain noisy. Net income attributable to common stockholders was a loss in three consecutive years — $(57)m (2022), $(209)m (2023), $(300)m (2024) — before swinging to $679m profit in 2025 (diluted EPS $2.74). Each year carried substantial impairments and unusual items (e.g. $419m unusual items and $193m asset impairments in 2025; $462m unusual items in 2024). Normalised income of $957m in 2025 is a more representative measure of underlying profitability than the prior loss years.

**Leverage** has improved markedly. Net debt fell from $551m (2023) and $727m (2024) to $144m (2025); total debt declined from $1.17bn to $686m. Long-term debt stands at $555m against stockholders' equity of $3.03bn (2025). Cash and equivalents rose to $453m. The balance sheet totals $5.61bn in assets against $2.19bn in liabilities; tangible book value is $2.90bn.

**Cash generation** is the strongest pillar. Operating cash flow was $1.02bn (2022), $647m (2023), $943m (2024), and $1.66bn (2025). Free cash flow turned negative in 2023 ($(116)m) during heavy capex ($763m), recovered to $258m (2024), and surged to $1.13bn (2025). Capex moderated to $532m in 2025 from a peak of $686–763m in 2023–24. The company paid $288m in dividends and repurchased $89m of stock in 2025 whilst repaying $1.53bn of long-term debt (partially offset by $1.00bn issuance, reflecting refinancing activity).

**Balance-sheet strength** is adequate and improving, though working capital absorbed $244m in 2025 (inventory up $152m), and minority interests deducted $210m from consolidated profit in 2025 — a persistent structural drag not visible in simple P/E metrics.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Commodity cyclicality.** Revenue and margins are overwhelmingly gold-price dependent. The screen's 105.7% growth rate reflects a favourable commodity window; a gold correction would compress the FCF yield that drives the Strong Buy signal.

**Earnings quality and impairments.** Reported net income was negative in 2022–24 despite positive operating cash flow, driven by asset impairments ($193m in 2025; $200m in 2024; $123m in 2023) and restructuring/M&A charges. This pattern raises questions about reserve/asset valuation and acquisition integration.

**West African jurisdiction risk.** Operations across Burkina Faso, Côte d'Ivoire, Senegal, and Mali carry regulatory, fiscal, security, and resource-nationalism exposure not captured by quantitative screens. Mining licence renewals, windfall taxes, and geopolitical instability can materially affect production and costs.

**Minority interests.** Non-controlling interests absorbed $210m of 2025 consolidated profit ($889m pre-minority vs $679m to common). This leakage reduces per-share economics relative to consolidated EBITDA.

**Governance and complexity.** The income statement includes discontinued operations (2022–24), business disposals, and frequent non-recurring items, complicating trend analysis. Pension liabilities are not separately disclosed in the provided financials.

**Concentration and execution.** High capex in 2023–24 ($763m and $686m) preceded the 2025 cash-flow surge; any project delays or cost overruns at key mines (e.g. Houndé, Ity, Sabodala-Massawa) would pressure FCF.

**2021 data gap.** The source file contains only fragmentary 2021 income-statement, balance-sheet, and cash-flow entries; a full five-year comparison is not possible for that year.
