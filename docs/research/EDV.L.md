**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

Endeavour Mining is a West Africa-focused gold producer that screens as a strong buy on cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk metrics (11/22 models; composite 74%). The investment case rests on a sharp 2025 earnings and cash-flow inflection—revenue of $4.23bn and free cash flow of $1.13bn—against a deleveraged balance sheet (net debt $144m) and headline valuation of 13.6x P/E with a 17.9% FCF yield. The central debate is whether 2025’s margin expansion (gross margin ~49%) is sustainable or largely gold-price driven, and whether recurring asset impairments ($419m unusual items in 2025) mask underlying operational risk. Timing is neutral (RSI 39; below 200-day MA), and screen conviction is low at 42% given the signal is only one week old.

**INVESTMENT THESIS**

For a value investor, Endeavour offers a rare combination of deep value and quality signals in a single name. The screen passes FCF Yield, Lynch PEG (0.13), Neff PEGY, Quality Value, Buffett Quality, Economic Moat, Dividend Growth, Magic Formula, Acquirer’s Multiple, Composite Value, and Financial Health—spanning all five metric families. Quantitative hooks are compelling: P/E 13.6, P/B 3.5, dividend yield 2.9%, ROE 31.1%, and reported growth of 105.7%, with sector-relative composite score of 67% versus 74% absolute.

Business quality, as reflected in 2025 financials, supports the screen: operating cash flow of $1.66bn, operating income of $1.87bn, and net debt cut from $727m (2024) to $144m (2025) while total debt fell from $1.17bn to $686m. The company returned capital via $288m dividends and $89m buybacks in 2025. Tangible book value recovered to $2.90bn from $2.57bn. These figures align with the screen’s quality and financial-health passes, suggesting the strong-buy signal is grounded in fundamental improvement rather than purely mechanical cheapness.

The GARP and dividend passes are particularly relevant: earnings normalised sharply in 2025 (diluted EPS $2.74 vs losses in 2023–2024), whilst the company maintained dividend payments through weaker years ($200m annually in 2023–2024). West Africa production scale—described in coverage as a leading regional gold producer—provides operational leverage to bullion prices, which is both the opportunity and the cyclical caveat.

**RISKS AND RED FLAGS**

**Jurisdictional and operational risk.** Endeavour’s West Africa production base exposes the group to political instability, regulatory change, community relations, and security incidents—risks not captured by quantitative screens. Recurring impairments ($193m in 2025; $200m in 2024) may signal reserve, cost, or asset-quality pressures at specific operations.

**Commodity cyclicality.** The 2025 revenue and margin step-change is consistent with elevated gold prices; a bullion correction would compress earnings rapidly, as evidenced by 2023–2024 profit weakness despite relatively stable revenue. Screens flag neutral timing and price 10.5% below the 200-day moving average.

**Earnings volatility and minority interests.** Reported profits are distorted by discontinued operations (2022–2023), tax provisions ($454m in 2025), and minority interest deductions ($210m in 2025). Normalised earnings are healthier but still depend on commodity assumptions.

**Governance and capital allocation.** Active buybacks and rising dividends during prior loss years warrant scrutiny of payout sustainability if gold retreats. BlackRock crossing a 13.37% voting threshold (June 2026) signals institutional interest but also concentration risk.

**Pension.** No pension liabilities are reported in the available balance-sheet data; this risk cannot be assessed from the source file.

**Data limitations.** 2021 financials are essentially missing from the source file, limiting full five-year trend analysis.
