# Vistry Group PLC (VTY.L) — Research memo

_Version 2 · Updated 2026-07-24T16:32:48.579527+00:00 · Mode: seeded_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Vistry Group is a UK housebuilder trading at a deep discount to book (P/B ~0.26, P/E ~6.4), which drives a **strong buy** on the quantitative screen (10/22 models passed, composite score 92%). The stock embeds a contrarian bet that normalised earnings and partnership-led affordable housing volumes will restore returns on a £3.3bn equity base with modest net debt (~£144m). The central debate is whether headline cheapness reflects a cyclical trough or structural margin impairment: management guided to an H1 2026 loss (~£30m), halted buybacks, and cut prices to drive sales, even as FY adjusted profit guidance (~£200m) is maintained. Leadership turnover, elevated short interest, and a sector-wide competition lawsuit add execution and legal overhang not captured by value metrics alone.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The screen flags Vistry as a classic deep-value + GARP candidate: it passes Schloss Low P/B, Deep Value, Earnings Yield, FCF Yield, Lynch PEG, Magic Formula, Acquirer's Multiple, Dreman Contrarian, Composite Value, and Financial Health, with leverage screened at D/E ~18%. At sub-0.3× book and ~6× trailing earnings, the market prices in sustained earnings erosion and/or balance-sheet stress that the numbers do not yet support.

Business quality is mixed but not broken. Vistry operates across open-market housebuilding and partnerships (affordable/social housing), giving some policy tailwind under Labour’s housing agenda. FY2025 (Yahoo fallback — see Financial Review) showed revenue down 4% to £3.61bn but net income recovering to £138m from £75m in FY2024, with free cash flow (FCF) of £175m and net debt below £150m. The equity base (£3.32bn) and tangible book (£2.17bn) provide a tangible floor if completions and partnership pipelines normalise.

For a value investor, the case rests on **asset-backed cheapness with improving cash generation**, not on high ROE (4.2%) or momentum (RSI ~53, below 200-day MA). The screen’s “risk” family pass reflects conservative leverage; the miss on quality/momentum families is appropriate given margin volatility and governance change. Conviction on the screen itself is low (30%, new signal, one week), so sizing should reflect that the quantitative signal is statistically strong but not yet time-tested on this name.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Cyclical and operational**
- UK housing demand, mortgage affordability, and Help to Buy / planning policy shifts remain primary drivers; the stock screens cheap partly because near-term earnings are under pressure.
- **Margin and pricing:** March and May 2026 updates describe price cuts to stimulate volumes; gross margins have not fully recovered from FY2024 lows. H1 2026 loss guidance vs full-year profit target creates credibility risk on phasing.
- **Inventory (£3.2bn):** A large, illiquid asset base; writedowns or slower turns would hit book value and FCF.

**Governance and leadership**
- **Executive Chair and CEO retirement** (Mar 2026); new Chair appointed (Mar 2026); **Adam Daniels** appointed CEO (Apr 2026). Transition during a profit warning increases execution uncertainty (Investors’ Chronicle: “Vistry held back by leadership uncertainty”, 4 Mar 2026).

**Legal and regulatory**
- **Competition class action** (Jun 2026): Vistry named among seven major housebuilders in a potential **£4bn+** claim alleging information-sharing on prices and incentives (Competition Appeal Tribunal approval required). Contingent liability not quantifiable from available filings.
- Sector scrutiny on build quality, fire safety, and consumer protection persists across UK housebuilders.

**Market and capital structure**
- **Elevated short interest** (Kalkine Media, 1 May 2026 — among most-shorted FTSE 350 names).
- **Buyback suspension** (May 2026) removes a prior capital-return pillar; insider buying (~£100k, Jul 2026) is modest relative to market cap.
- Interest expense ~£91m (FY2025) remains meaningful though coverage is adequate at current profit levels.

**Screen blind spots**
- Low **ROE (4.2%)** suggests the market doubts sustainable returns on equity despite cheap P/B.
- Goodwill and intangibles (£1.16bn) mean reported P/B understates tangible discount but also carries impairment risk.
- **Filing bodies absent** — unable to assess going-concern statements, debt covenants, fire-reform provisions, or off-balance-sheet commitments from primary text.
