## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Hikma Pharmaceuticals is a FTSE 100 generics and injectables manufacturer trading on a value screen at P/E 11.5, P/B 1.8, and 3.9% yield, with 12 of 22 quantitative models passing across cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP, and risk families. Revenue has compounded steadily to £3,349m in 2025 and reported net income has recovered to £402m (diluted EPS £1.81), but gross margins have compressed and 2025 free cash flow collapsed to £119m on working-capital absorption. The valuation hook is a cyclically depressed share price following a February 2026 guidance-related selloff, partially offset by a $250m buyback and reiterated 2026 guidance. The key debate is whether margin pressure and elevated net debt (£1,334m) are transitory execution issues or structural headwinds in US generics pricing — a question the screen's low P/E alone cannot resolve.

## FINANCIAL REVIEW
**Revenue and profitability.** Total revenue progressed £2,517m (2022) → £2,875m (2023) → £3,127m (2024) → £3,349m (2025). Operating income followed £482m → £610m → £612m → £633m. Reported net income was depressed in 2022–2023 (£188m and £190m respectively) by large unusual items (£155m and £240m), before recovering to £359m (2024) and £402m (2025). Diluted EPS rose from £0.836 (2022) to £1.81 (2025). Normalised income in 2025 was £411m versus reported £402m.

**Margins.** Gross profit grew in absolute terms (£1,238m to £1,441m) but gross margin compressed from 49.2% (2022) to 43.0% (2025), consistent with the margin warnings flagged in February 2026 news. EBITDA improved from £485m (2022) to £845m (2025), with normalised EBITDA at £857m. Operating expense as a share of revenue has been broadly stable (~24% of revenue in SG&A plus ~4.5% in R&D).

**Leverage.** Net debt increased from £943m (2022) to £1,334m (2025); total debt rose from £1,283m to £1,604m. Long-term debt was £1,445m at year-end 2025 versus £1,074m in 2022. Net debt to EBITDA (reported) is approximately 1.6x (£1,334m / £845m), manageable but trending higher. Interest expense rose from £59m to £88m. Equity expanded from £2,135m to £2,593m, and the screen's debt/equity of 62% is corroborated (£1,604m / £2,593m ≈ 62%).

**Cash generation.** Operating cash flow has been consistently strong: £585m (2022), £608m (2023), £564m (2024), £436m (2025). Free cash flow was £360m, £404m, £329m, then £119m in 2025 — a sharp decline driven by a £251m working-capital outflow (inventory +£86m, receivables +£97m, other current assets +£143m). Capital expenditure stepped up to £317m in 2025 from £225m in 2022, including £197m in PPE and £120m in intangibles. Cash on hand fell modestly to £217m (from £270m in 2022) with £111m restricted cash added in 2025.

**Balance sheet.** Total assets grew from £4,471m to £5,671m. Goodwill and intangibles total £1,170m; tangible book value is £1,423m (P/B 1.8 is therefore above tangible book). Inventory rose to £1,106m and accounts receivable to £997m — both warrant monitoring as potential cash traps. Pension liabilities appear in 2022 current liabilities (£32m) but are not separately disclosed in 2025 balance-sheet data provided.

**Data gap.** FY2021 income-statement data in the source file is incomplete (only partial line items); the five-year trend analysis above covers FY2022–FY2025 in full.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Margin and guidance risk.** February 2026 coverage reported in-line 2025 results but a 17% share fall on margin warnings and softer forward guidance ("Hikma posts 2025 results in line; stock down 17% on margin warning," Investing.com UK, 26 Feb 2026; "Hikma Pharma's gloomy outlook kicks shares to more than three-year low," Reuters, 26 Feb 2026). Gross margin compression in the financials (49.2% → 43.0%) corroborates this concern.

**Working capital and FCF.** The 2025 FCF collapse to £119m from £329m in 2024, despite higher earnings, signals that reported profit is not fully converting to cash. Rising inventory and receivables may reflect channel stuffing, US pricing pressure, or integration of acquisitions — none of which the value screen captures.

**Leverage and capital allocation.** Net debt has risen £391m over three years while the company simultaneously funded £622m in cumulative dividends (2022–2025) and buybacks. Debt issuance of £2,751m and repayment of £2,450m in 2025 suggests active refinancing; interest coverage remains adequate but the direction of travel is worth watching.

**Regulatory and legal.** Recent headline "Why Is Hikma Pharmaceuticals Suddenly In The Regulatory Crosshairs?" (Kalkine Media, 14 Jul 2026) flags emerging regulatory scrutiny — details are thin in the manifest but this is a material sector risk for a US-focused generics player. FDA inspection outcomes, ANDA approvals, and pricing investigations are ongoing industry hazards. Hikma was party to Pfizer VYNDAMAX patent settlements (April 2026), delaying generic entry until 2031 — limiting near-term upside from that product but reducing litigation overhang.

**Competitive and cyclical.** US generics pricing remains deflationary; injectables offer better margins but require sustained FDA compliance and capacity investment (construction in progress £477m in 2025). The company exited 503B compounding in April 2026 (trading update), a strategic rationalisation but also a revenue headwind.

**Governance.** Positive signal: Independent Chairman Victoria Hull increased her stake by 241% (simplywall.st, 2 May 2026). Institutional ownership at 59% (simplywall.st, 6 Feb 2026) provides liquidity but limits activist optionality. No major management changes flagged in the news manifest.

**Pension.** Current pension provisions visible in 2022 (£32m) but not separately quantified in later balance sheets within the source data — pension risk cannot be fully assessed from these files alone.
