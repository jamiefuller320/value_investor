# Hikma Pharmaceuticals PLC (HIK.L) — Research memo

_Version 1 · Updated 2026-07-19T10:05:19.408587+00:00 · Mode: initial_

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Hikma Pharmaceuticals is a London-listed generics and injectables group trading on a value screen that flags **Strong Buy** (13/22 models; composite score 75%, sector-relative 91%). At roughly 11.4× P/E, 1.8× P/B, 4.0% yield and 16.5% ROE, the market prices in margin compression and execution risk rather than business failure. Reported earnings have recovered strongly since 2023, but February 2026 guidance and margin warnings triggered a sharp de-rating despite in-line 2025 results. The central debate is whether a “strategic reset” (portfolio simplification, buybacks, injectables scale) can stabilise margins and free cash flow, or whether US pricing, regulatory scrutiny and litigation will keep multiples depressed.

## INVESTMENT THESIS
The quantitative screen identifies Hikma as a rare FTSE 100 healthcare name passing five factor families — cheapness, quality, dividend, GARP and risk — with high data quality (19/20 metrics). Classic value screens (Graham Enterprising, Earnings Yield, Low P/E + High Yield, Lynch PEG, Neff PEGY, Magic Formula, Acquirer’s Multiple, Dreman Contrarian) all pass, suggesting the stock is statistically cheap relative to reported earnings power and balance-sheet quality.

Business quality supports the screen: revenue has compounded from **$2.52bn (2022)** to **$3.35bn (2025)** (Yahoo fallback), with net income rising from **$190m (2023)** to **$402m (2025)** and diluted EPS from **$0.85** to **$1.81** over the same recovery period. ROE of **16.5%** and debt/equity of **62%** indicate a leveraged but manageable capital structure for a mid-cap pharma operator with diversified injectables, branded and generic franchises across MENA, Europe and the US.

For a value investor, the hook is asymmetric: the market appears to have repriced forward margins after management’s February 2026 caution, whilst Citi (July 2026) and other brokers frame the reset as a compelling entry. Capital return — a buyback programme launched February 2026, ongoing share repurchases through July 2026, and chairman insider buying (May 2026) — adds a shareholder-friendly overlay to the cheapness signal. The screen’s negative trailing FCF metric (**−$66m** in the snapshot) warrants scrutiny, but underlying operating cash generation remains positive; the tension is working-capital absorption and elevated capex rather than earnings collapse.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Margin and guidance risk:** The February 2026 results/guidance event is the most material near-term red flag. Despite in-line 2025 earnings, management’s margin outlook triggered a substantial de-rating. Interactive Investor (March 2026) characterised the tone as injecting “a big dose of caution.” Forward earnings — on which the value screen partly relies — may need revision if US generic pricing or mix shift persists.

**Free cash flow and balance-sheet drift:** FCF fell to **$119m** in 2025 whilst dividends rose to **$185m** and net debt increased. Sustained working-capital absorption (inventory **+$120m**, receivables **+$101m** YoY) bears monitoring. Debt/equity of **62%** is within screen tolerance but rising leverage reduces flexibility.

**Litigation and regulatory exposure:** The May 2025 **Xyrem® US lawsuit settlement** (headline only; terms not in source bodies) confirms ongoing US legal overhang. Kalkine Media (14 July 2026) flagged Hikma being “in the regulatory crosshairs,” suggesting heightened FDA/compliance scrutiny — relevant for a US-heavy injectables and generics operator. April 2026 exit from **503B compounding** signals portfolio rationalisation but also prior regulatory/business-model risk in that channel.

**Competitive and pricing pressure:** Sector-wide generic deflation, US tariff rhetoric (Interactive Investor, August 2025, noted Hikma reiterating profit growth hope despite tariffs), and patent settlements such as Pfizer’s **VYNDAMAX/tafamidis** agreements with Hikma (April 2026) — which delay generic entry until **2031** — cut both ways: litigation clarity but deferred revenue opportunity.

**Governance / M&A overhang:** Brookfield confirmed in February 2026 it would **not** make an offer, removing a near-term bid floor but also confirming external interest had been explored. No primary filing language on covenants or going concern was available to assess.

**Screen-specific flag:** Trailing FCF metric is negative in the screening snapshot despite positive reported FCF in Yahoo annual data — a discrepancy that lowers confidence in cash-based quality screens until reconciled with filings.
