## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Hikma Pharmaceuticals is a London-listed generics and injectables group (Injectables, Generics, Branded) that screens as a **Strong Buy** on cheapness, quality, dividend yield, GARP, and balance-sheet metrics. At roughly 11× earnings, 1.8× book, and a ~4% yield with 16.5% ROE, the valuation embeds scepticism after a February 2026 guidance reset that sent the shares down sharply despite in-line FY2025 results. The investment case rests on whether a “strategic reset” — portfolio simplification (503B compounding exit), buybacks, and pipeline/FDA opportunities — can stabilise margins and restore free cash flow after a weak 2025 working-capital year. The central debate is whether current multiples adequately compensate for US pricing pressure, litigation overhang (Xyrem), and rising net debt, or whether the market has over-discounted a durable compounder.

For a value investor, the hook is a **quality generics franchise trading at a cyclical trough multiple** after management’s own margin warning, with capital return (buyback programme launched February 2026; ongoing transactions in own shares through July 2026 per RNS headlines) and renewed sell-side support (Citi Buy initiation, July 2026) offering a catalyst path. The screen’s negative trailing FCF figure (**-$66m**) warrants caution but may reflect timing; Yahoo reports **$119m** FCF in 2025 — still down from **$329m** in 2024 — suggesting the cheapness is partly earned until cash conversion improves.

## RISKS AND RED FLAGS

**Guidance and margin risk:** February 2026 headlines (“Hikma posts 2025 results in line; stock down 17% on margin warning”, 26 Feb 2026; “Hikma’s shares plummet after guidance cut”, Investors’ Chronicle, same date) indicate the market’s core concern is forward profitability, not historical earnings. Without filing bodies, specific guidance ranges are unavailable here.

**Cash conversion:** FCF fell to **$119m** in 2025 (Yahoo) while dividends were **$185m**; inventory and receivables are rising. The screen’s trailing FCF metric is **negative (-$66m)**, a divergence that weakens the “Financial Health” pass on a forward-looking basis.

**Litigation:** RNS headline “Settlement agreement for Xyrem® US lawsuits” (8 May 2025) confirms ongoing US legal exposure on a legacy product; settlement terms and residual liability are not in the source bodies.

**Patent/competitive setbacks:** Hikma was party to Pfizer Vyndamax (tafamidis) patent settlements (Yahoo, 28–30 Apr 2026) delaying generic entry until **2031** — a lost near-term US opportunity, not a balance-sheet hit but a pipeline setback.

**Regulatory scrutiny:** “Why Is Hikma Pharmaceuticals Suddenly In The Regulatory Crosshairs?” (Kalkine Media, 14 Jul 2026) flags heightened regulatory attention; details are thin in the manifest.

**Leverage trend:** Net debt up **~26%** YoY to **$1.33bn**; interest cover remains adequate but direction is unfavourable if margins soften further.

**Governance / insider activity:** Independent Chairman Victoria Hull increased her stake significantly (“Buys 241% More Shares”, 2 May 2026) — a positive signal, but institutional control (~59% per Simply Wall St headline, 6 Feb 2026) limits takeover optionality.

**Filing coverage gap:** Absence of annual report / interim RNS body text prevents assessment of going-concern language, pension obligations, contingencies, or covenant headroom from primary sources — a material research limitation.
