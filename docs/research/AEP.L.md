## RISKS AND RED FLAGS
**Regulatory and country risk:** Indonesian palm-oil export policy is a material overhang. The indexed RNS “Indonesian Government Export Proposals” (20 May 2026) coincided with share-price weakness; news titles “AEP shares fall as Indonesia announces export controls for palm oil” (London South East, 20 May 2026) and “Palm oil stocks struggle after Indonesian intervention” (Investors’ Chronicle, 27 May 2026) highlight sensitivity to Jakarta policy shifts. Without filing body text, the precise operational impact remains unverified.

**Commodity cyclicality:** Revenue fell from $447.6m (FY2022) to $370.4m (FY2023) before recovering, consistent with palm-oil price volatility. FY2025 strength may partly reflect favourable pricing that is not guaranteed to persist.

**Operational:** The AGM trading update (June 2026) reportedly showed mixed production in the first five months—potential yield or milling inefficiency not captured by valuation screens.

**M&A and integration:** The $162m Pinago Utama acquisition (May 2025) expands the Indonesian footprint but introduces integration and execution risk. Yahoo records only $0.3m goodwill/intangibles in FY2025—likely incomplete post-deal. CB Industrial’s divestment to AEP (The Edge Malaysia, 14 October 2025) adds further portfolio complexity.

**Capital structure and liquidity:** The 10-for-1 share split (announced June 2026) and £8m buyback programme (July 2026) aim to improve liquidity, but concurrent buybacks tighten free float (multiple TipRanks headlines, July 2026), which can amplify volatility in a small-cap name.

**Governance and disclosure:** A “Change of Name” RNS (25 November 2025) indicates recent corporate rebranding. Minority interests ($4.2m) and the proposed Indonesia IPO introduce structural complexity. Pension obligations ($8.0m non-current) are modest but present.

**Screen blind spots:** Quantitative models cannot fully capture ESG/regulatory pressure on palm oil, weather/el Niño crop risk, or related-party transactions in Indonesian listed structures. No going-concern, contingency, or covenant language is available from filing bodies in this source pack.

**Data noise:** News and filings search results frequently conflate AEP Plantations with American Electric Power (NASDAQ: AEP)—e.g. “$2.6B equity offering” headlines (May 2025) refer to the US utility, not this issuer. Several indexed RNS items appear unrelated (e.g. “Recommended Offer for Urban Logistics REIT”, May 2025).
